# Trip Goal and Parameters

## Trip Goal

Design a scenic, family-friendly 3-week motorcycle tour in Japan for a family of three:

* Experienced rider on a large adventure motorcycle, preferably Honda Africa Twin or similar.
* Newer rider wife on a smaller motorcycle under 500 cc, preferably Honda CB400X / NX400.
* 6-year-old child riding as passenger with the experienced rider.

The trip should combine famous Japan highlights with quieter rural regions, remote villages, onsen towns, coastal roads, river valleys, and island routes. The plan should prioritize safety, comfort, and memorable family experiences over aggressive riding or long-distance endurance.

The preferred route should include:

* Hakone and Fuji area.
* Izu Peninsula.
* Shikoku remote villages and scenic mountain roads.
* Shimanto River area.
* Dogo Onsen / Matsuyama.
* Shimanami Kaido.
* Seto Inland Sea.
* Kansai finish, ideally Osaka/Kyoto area.

## Rider Skill and Safety Parameters

### Wife / Newer Rider

The wife is a newer rider, so the plan must be built around her comfort and confidence.

Routing should avoid:

* Dense city riding where possible.
* Narrow mountain roads after dark.
* Wet twisty roads.
* Aggressive rider roads on weekends.
* Tiny Google Maps shortcuts.
* Long transfer days back-to-back.
* Technical gravel or forest roads.
* Tight ferry/hotel ramps where possible.

Preferred road type:

* Paved rural roads.
* Wider national and prefectural roads.
* Scenic coastal roads.
* Gentle mountain roads.
* Low-traffic village roads.

The wife should usually ride near the front so her pace controls the group.

### Experienced Rider / Child Passenger

The experienced rider carries:

* 6-year-old child passenger.
* Extra snacks.
* Rain layers.
* Basic tools.
* First aid kit.
* Spare gloves/layers if possible.

Child passenger requirements:

* Properly fitting motorcycle helmet.
* Protective jacket, gloves, pants, and boots.
* Ability to sit securely.
* Ability to reach passenger pegs.
* Top case/back pad preferred.
* Intercom preferred.
* Stop every 60–90 minutes.

## Hotel and Overnight Parameters

Prioritize:

* Secure or easy motorcycle parking.
* Family-friendly rooms.
* Private or family bath where possible.
* Onsen ryokan in scenic areas.
* Nice hotels in cities.
* Laundry access every 3–4 days.
* Dinner included in remote areas.
* Western beds where available, unless ryokan experience is specifically desired.

Preferred 2-night stays:

* Hakone.
* Iya / Oboke.
* Shimanto River area.
* Dogo Onsen / Matsuyama.
* Onomichi / Setoda.

## Route Design Priorities

The itinerary should prioritize:

1. Safety for a newer rider.
2. Comfort for a child passenger.
3. Scenic paved roads.
4. Remote villages and rural Japan.
5. Beautiful onsen towns.
6. Memorable kid-friendly stops.
7. Good hotels and food.
8. Moderate daily distances.
9. Rest days in beautiful places.
10. Avoiding unnecessary urban traffic.

The plan should not optimize for maximum mileage, technical riding, or famous motorcycle roads if those roads add stress for the newer rider.

## Recommended Overall Route

Preferred high-level route:

Tokyo/Yokohama → Hakone → Izu Peninsula → Fuji Five Lakes → Nagoya/Gifu transfer → Awaji Island → Iya Valley / Oboke → Kochi → Shimanto River → Uchiko / Uwajima → Dogo Onsen / Matsuyama → Shimanami Kaido → Onomichi / Setoda → Takamatsu / Naoshima or Kurashiki → Himeji / Kobe → Osaka / Kansai

## Rental Logistics Assumption

Best logistics:

* Pick up motorcycles near Tokyo/Yokohama.
* Return motorcycles near Osaka/Kansai.
* Use one-way rental if available.

Fallback logistics:

* Start and end in Osaka/Kansai.
* Do Shikoku + Shimanami as the main motorcycle route.
* Add Hakone/Fuji separately by train or separate Tokyo-area rental.

## Legal and Documentation Assumptions

Each adult rider should have:

* Valid home-country motorcycle license.
* Valid 1949 Geneva Convention International Driving Permit.
* Passport.
* Credit card.
* Rental reservation confirmation.
* Travel insurance that covers motorcycle riding.
* Confirmation that passenger riding is allowed on the rented motorcycle.

The itinerary should not assume that every rental company allows one-way rental, passenger use, child passenger use, or large-bike rental without checking first.

## Trip Style Summary

This is a premium family ADV-style paved touring trip, not a hard adventure ride.

Desired feeling:

* Beautiful mountain villages.
* Quiet rural roads.
* Onsen evenings.
* Coastal and island scenery.
* Castles, boats, ropeways, river activities, and kid-friendly stops.
* Enough rest days that the newer rider and child still enjoy the trip.

Avoid turning the trip into a mileage challenge, sport-riding trip, or logistics endurance test.
