# Trip Brief — "From Desert Up to Glaciers" (Kazakhstan & Kyrgyzstan Loop)

**A 16-day guided ADV motorcycle loop, Almaty → Almaty, ≈ 2,800 km, Sep 5–20, 2026** *(planned — confirm dates with the operator; this is a custom extended edition of the published 10-day tour)*.

## What it is

A **real ADV step-up** for Ruslan — experienced rider (BMW R1300GS at home, guided Morocco ADV tour completed) — riding a **custom extended loop** built with **[Silk Off Road Tours](https://www.silkoffroadtours.com/motorcycle-tours-kazakhstan-kyrgyzstan)**: ride leader + support vehicle, rental **Suzuki DR650SE**, from the Kazakh desert steppe up to 4,000 m Tian Shan glaciers on the old Silk Road — and back. Morocco-style "anchor-point adventure" in the same **14+2 shape** (Day 1 arrival + a 14-day tour body of 1 training + 12 riding + 1 rest day + Day 16 departure): a **Day-2 training day** to dial in the bike, fixed nightly anchors, adventurous riding between them, luggage in the truck, and a **real rest day** mid-trip. Start = finish: **no open-jaw**.

## Flexible by design

Participants aren't fixed yet, so the plan is **anchor-first**: **5 pre-booked ★ anchors** — Altyn-Emel/Singing Dune, Karakol (2 nights), the Issyk-Kul south-shore yurt camp (2 nights, **the mid-trip rest-day anchor**), Song-Kol yurt camp, Saty/Kolsai — connected by easier/harder **route options** the group picks on the Day-2 training day. The itinerary below is the **reference line** through those options; days freed by faster options become extra anchor nights or weather buffer. Full menu: [tour/03-anchors-and-options.md](tour/03-anchors-and-options.md).

## The route

**Almaty** (Day 1 handover; **Day 2 training day** — skills session + ~120 km foothill loop) → **Altyn-Emel NP** (1 night, Basshi) → **Singing Dune & Aktau mountains ridden en route** to the **Chundzha** hot springs (the operator's original 250 km leg) → **Charyn Canyon** and the **Kegen/Karkara border** into Kyrgyzstan (clocks +1 h) → **Karakol** (**2 nights**: Jeti-Oguz + Altyn-Arashan day loop) → up Barskoon gorge over **Suek Pass (4,021 m)** to the **Issyk-Kul south-shore yurt camp** (**2 nights — Day 8 rest day**) → **Kochkor** (eagle-hunting family lunch) → 33 Parrots Pass switchbacks to **Song-Kol Lake (3,016 m**, yurt camp) → Moldo-Ashuu & Kara-Keche passes to the **Suusamyr valley** → **Too-Ashuu Pass** down to **Bishkek** → **Boom gorge** to **Cholpon-Ata** (petroglyphs, north shore) → **Karkara border back into Kazakhstan** (clocks −1 h) → **Saty** (**Lake Kaindy**'s sunken forest, **Kolsai Lakes**) → home to **Almaty** (celebration dinner; fly out Day 16 from ALA).

## Logistics in one breath

**Round-trip flights to Almaty (ALA)** — transfers included, no Bishkek flight logic. US citizens are **visa-free** in both countries (KZ: 30 days per visit; KG: 30 days per 60-day window under the Jan 1, 2026 rules) — passport valid 6+ months with blank pages for **four mid-trip border stamps**. Kazakhstan runs on **UTC+5** (unified March 2024), Kyrgyzstan on **UTC+6** — watch forward an hour on Day 5, back an hour on Day 14. Cash (tenge/som) for fuel and meals; fill the DR650's ~250 km-range tank at every stop the leader calls — especially around Kegen before the Saty/Kolsai leg; pack **layers for 4,000 m**, a **sleeping-bag liner for three yurt nights**, swim kit for the rest day, and **medical + evacuation insurance that explicitly covers motorcycling** — coverage is nil at Song-Kol, on the passes, and patchy at Kolsai.
