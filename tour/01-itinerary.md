# Itinerary & Daily Schedule (Day 0–25)

**≈ 1,166 miles (1,877 km) of riding on an Osaka-anchored loop (start and finish in Osaka), inside a ≈26-day door-to-door trip (Day 0–25).** Distances and driving times are from the **Google Directions API** (real road routing through each day's stops). The riding loop itself is unchanged — only the non-riding city/museum bookends were added.

**Getting to/from the loop:** the family flies **nonstop SEA ⇄ Tokyo** and rides the **Tōkaidō Shinkansen (~2h30m) Tokyo ⇄ Shin-Osaka** on each end (see [flights](02-flights.md)). A **Tokyo arrival night (Day 0)** and a **full Tokyo museum day (Day 1)** precede the train down to the **Day-2 bike pickup**; at the far end the **bikes return on Day 22**, a **Super Nintendo World / USJ day fills Day 23**, a **reposition-to-Tokyo day (Day 24)** precedes the **homebound flight (Day 25)** — so the door-to-door span is **≈ 26 days (Day 0–25)**.

Average riding time on the **13 transfer days is 2h42m** (1h45m–4h33m). No two long days run back-to-back, rest days stay light, and **Day 9 swaps a long road transfer for a ~2h15m Nankai ferry** across the strait to Shikoku. The loop opens down the **central Kii spine** — Kōyasan and the sacred Kumano interior on the paved **Route 168 pilgrim road** — before reaching the Pacific. There are no high alpine passes, so no November snow-closures or near-freezing dawns; but the **Kii interior & Pacific coast (Days 3–9) is the typhoon-exposed side of the loop**, and **Routes 168/311 can close after heavy rain**, so keep early-trip lodging cancellable (see [overview](00-overview.md) and the October notes).

## Day notes

### Day 0 — Tokyo Arrival Night
*SEA ✈ Tokyo (Haneda) → Shinagawa · 0 mi · no riding · Tokyo*

**Arrival bookend (not part of the riding loop).** The **nonstop SEA → Tokyo** flight (ANA/Delta to Haneda, JAL to Narita; ~10h30m) crosses the date line and lands **mid/late afternoon**. From Haneda, the **Keikyu Line** reaches **Shinagawa** (a Shinkansen stop) in ~15 min; overnight near the station for a gentle jet-lag reset. Easy dinner, early night — there's a **full Tokyo museum day tomorrow** (Day 1) before the train south, so **don't forward the luggage yet**; an optional short evening stroll is fine if energy allows. See [flights](02-flights.md).

### Day 1 — Tokyo Museum Day
*Mitaka · Toyosu · Shibuya (train & on foot) · 0 mi · no riding · Tokyo*

A full, kid-focused Tokyo day before heading south — three of the city's best family experiences, paced so a jet-lagged 6-year-old isn't overstuffed (three is plenty; don't add a fourth). **Morning — the Ghibli Museum** in **Mitaka** (on the edge of Inokashira Park): Hayao Miyazaki's whimsical, hands-on museum of *Totoro*, the Catbus, a rooftop robot and an exclusive members-only short film. **Afternoon — teamLab Planets TOKYO** in **Toyosu**: an immersive, barefoot, wade-through world of digital art — mirrored light rooms, knee-deep water and projected flowers that delight every age. **Early evening — Nintendo TOKYO** in **Shibuya PARCO**: the official Nintendo flagship store (Mario, Zelda, Pokémon, Splatoon) with the **Pokémon Center Shibuya** next door — a gentle finish near Shibuya Crossing before dinner and an early night.

> **Book these the moment they release.** The **Ghibli Museum admits by advance, date-and-time-specific tickets only** — there are **no door sales**; tickets go on sale **~one month ahead via Lawson** (from the 10th of the previous month) and routinely **sell out within hours**, so set a reminder and buy the instant your date opens. **teamLab Planets** also needs a **timed online ticket** — reserve a slot in advance. Pair a **morning Ghibli slot with an afternoon teamLab slot**, and keep Shibuya/Nintendo as the loose early-evening cap.

**Stops along the way (all on foot / by train):**
- 🎨 **Ghibli Museum (Mitaka)** — Miyazaki's magical museum, the Catbus and a members-only short film **[KID]**
- 💧 **teamLab Planets TOKYO (Toyosu)** — Barefoot, immersive digital-art world of water, light and flowers **[KID]**
- 🎮 **Nintendo TOKYO (Shibuya PARCO)** — Official Nintendo flagship store + Pokémon Center next door **[KID]**

### Day 2 — Shinkansen to Osaka & Bike Pickup
*Tokyo 🚄 Shin-Osaka → Suita base · 0 mi · no riding · Kansai*

**Strictly no riding today.** After the Tokyo museum day, a **late-morning Nozomi** Tokyo/Shinagawa → **Shin-Osaka** (~2h30m, ≈¥14,720/adult, child ~half) glides in **around midday**. From **Shin-Osaka** it's a short local hop / taxi out to the **Suita base** of [Bike Rental Japan](https://bikerentaljapan.com/) in northern Osaka — there is no Tokyo counter, which is exactly why this trip is an Osaka loop. The afternoon is the rental handover only: paperwork and insurance, a full walk-around of the Africa Twin and CB400X, fitting and adjusting the child's gear, pairing the intercoms, loading luggage, and a slow **on-foot shakedown** to learn the controls. The first real ride is **Day 3 morning, fresh and rested.** Then an easy first Osaka evening in the neon canyon of **Dōtonbori / Namba** — takoyaki, okonomiyaki and a welcome dinner.

> **Connection & luggage timing:** forward the big cases ahead from the Tokyo hotel by **takkyūbin** that morning so the train is light, and confirm the Suita branch's opening hours allow a same-afternoon collection after the midday Nozomi. If the flight landed late or jet-lag lingers, the **Day-0 and Day-1 Tokyo nights** absorb it — never rush the pickup.

See [child-pillion safety](00-overview.md#child-pillion-safety-africa-twin) in the overview before Day 3 — fit and test the footpeg reach, backrest/grab bar and tandem belt while the bike is parked today.

> 🧳 **Luggage — start the chain:** the cases forwarded from Tokyo are waiting at the Osaka hotel. **Repack into two parts:** a **chain case** with most of your clothes — **forward it today (or Day-2 morning) from the Osaka front desk to the Shirahama rest base (Days 7–8)**, the first reliable forwarding target, before the late-morning courier cutoff (next-day delivery) — and a heavier **"base-camp" case** of anything you won't need on the road, left in the **Osaka hotel's left-luggage for the whole loop** (collect Day 22). Onto the bikes goes only riding gear + a soft overnight bag. Confirm the front desk will hold the forwarded case under your name for the Day-7 check-in.

**Stops along the way (train / local / on foot):**
- 🚄 **Nozomi Shinkansen** — ~2h30m glide Tokyo → Shin-Osaka, arriving around midday
- 🏍️ **Suita rental base** — Bike pickup, paperwork, gear-fit and on-foot shakedown
- 🍜 **Dōtonbori / Namba** — Welcome dinner under the neon — takoyaki & okonomiyaki

### Day 3 — Up to the Sacred Mountain
*Osaka → Kōyasan · 45 mi · 2h19m riding · Kōya, Wakayama*

The first real ride is gentle and short. Slip south out of the Kansai sprawl and pick up the paved **Route 480 / 370** approach that winds up into the forested highlands of **Kōyasan** — a UNESCO World Heritage monastic town founded in 816, set in a high basin at ~800 m. **Arriving around midday is deliberate, not dead time** — Kōyasan is a whole sacred town and rewards an unhurried afternoon: lunch in the temple village, then the vermilion **Danjō Garan** pagoda complex, the head temple **Kongōbu-ji** (with Japan's largest rock garden and gold-leaf *fusuma* rooms) and, if there's appetite for it, the **Reihōkan** treasure museum. Save **Okunoin** — the cedar avenue of 200,000 tombs to Kōbō Daishi's mausoleum — for the **lantern-lit dusk walk**, when it's at its most atmospheric, then settle into a **shukubō** (temple lodging) for a **shōjin-ryōri** Buddhist-vegetarian dinner and a quiet mountain night.

**For the newer rider:** ride the first 30 minutes very gently, settling into the bike, the left-side traffic and the group spacing before any pace builds. The final climb is a winding two-lane mountain road — relaxed and well within the cap, with no schedule to beat. Re-check the child's gear at the foot of the climb. **October note:** Kōyasan is cooler than the lowlands and turns colour early — pack a warm layer for the child for the evening.

**Stops along the way:**
- 🛕 **Danjo Garan & Konpon Daitō** — The vermilion great pagoda, spiritual heart of the mountain (early-afternoon arrival walk)
- 🏯 **Kongōbu-ji** — Head temple of Shingon Buddhism: the Banryūtei rock garden and painted *fusuma* halls (afternoon)
- ⛩️ **Okunoin** — Lantern-lit cedar avenue to Kōbō Daishi's mausoleum (a dusk walk)
- 🍵 **Shukubō shōjin-ryōri** — Temple lodging and a Buddhist vegetarian dinner

### Day 4 — The Pilgrim Road (Route 168)
*Kōyasan → Hongū / Yunomine · 52 mi · 2h02m riding · Kumano, Wakayama*

Drop off the mountain and turn south down **Route 168**, the classic pilgrim road, following the **Totsukawa River** through deep forested gorges and **Totsukawa**, Japan's largest village by area. Pause to walk the swaying **Tanise suspension bridge** high over the river, then roll into the **Hongū** basin — the spiritual centre of the Kumano Kodō. Visit **Kumano Hongū Taisha**, head shrine of the three Kumano Sanzan, and the nearby **Ōyunohara** sandbank crowned by **Japan's largest torii** (33.9 m). Overnight at the ancient onsen hamlets of **Yunomine** or **Kawayu**.

**For the newer rider:** Route 168 is moderate, paved two-lane — winding but not technical, ridden at a calm pace with frequent valley pull-offs. **October / typhoon note:** this is the most landslide-prone corner of the loop — **Routes 168/311 can close after heavy rain** — so keep these interior nights cancellable and check road and river status before setting off.

**Stops along the way:**
- 🌉 **Tanise no Tsuribashi** — A 297 m swaying pedestrian suspension bridge over the Totsukawa
- ☕ **Totsukawa** — River-valley leg-stretch in Japan's largest village
- ⛩️ **Kumano Hongū Taisha & Ōyunohara** — Head Kumano shrine and Japan's largest torii

### Day 5 — Kumano Interior Rest Day
*Yunomine / Kawayu (light riding) · 20 mi · rest day · Kumano, Wakayama*

A slow day in the sacred interior. Soak in the tiny **Tsuboyu** at Yunomine — the only UNESCO World Heritage bath you can actually bathe in (reserve a 30-minute slot) — and **boil eggs** in the public hot spring. At **Kawayu**, scoop out your **own riverbed bath** in the warm gravel of the Ōto River. Walk the gentle **Hosshinmon-ōji → Hongū** final section of the Kumano Kodō for a manageable taste of the pilgrimage, and return to **Ōyunohara** for the great torii at golden hour.

**Rest-day balance:** a genuine downtime day — the core is onsen soaking, not sightseeing. Keep the **Hosshinmon-ōji → Hongū** Kumano Kodō walk to its short final stretch (the full section is ~7 km / 2–3 h, too much for a 6-year-old); a 30–40 min taster is plenty. **Kawayu's dig-your-own riverbed bath depends on river level** — after the heavy rain this typhoon-exposed corner can bring, the Ōto River runs too high and cold to scoop a bath, so treat it as weather-dependent. **Wet-weather fallback:** the free **Kumano Hongū Heritage Center** by the shrine (Kodō exhibits), the covered ryokan/Honkan indoor hot-spring baths, and boiling eggs at the Yunomine *yuzutsu* spring all work in the rain.

> 🧳 **Luggage:** nothing to send today — you're in the remote Kumano interior (2-day delivery), so the overnight kit stays on the bikes. Your chain case is on its way to the **Shirahama** rest base, waiting there for your **Day-7 arrival**, where you'll have the full suitcase for two nights.

**Stops along the way:**
- ♨️ **Tsuboyu, Yunomine** — Soak in the only World Heritage bath you can bathe in
- ♨️ **Kawayu Onsen** — Dig your own bath in the warm riverbed
- ⛩️ **Ōyunohara & Kumano Kodō walk** — Giant torii and a gentle pilgrim-trail stretch

### Day 6 — Down to the Sacred Coast
*Yunomine → Doro-kyō → Nachi-Katsuura · 52 mi · 1h52m riding · Wakayama*

A short, scenic descent from the mountains to the Pacific. Drop along the **Kitayama River** to **Doro-kyō**, where a **jet-boat** threads the glass-clear gorge between towering cliffs — a thrill for the child. Continue to the coast for the day's centrepiece: **Nachi Falls**, Japan's tallest waterfall, framed beside the vermilion pagoda of **Kumano Nachi Taisha**, reached up the cobbled **Daimon-zaka** path under giant cedars. With the riding done by mid-afternoon, this is one to **bank as downtime**: roll into the tuna port of **Katsuura** and spend the rest of the day in the water — the famous **seaside cave onsen** (Hotel Urashima's **Bōki-dō**, a natural sea grotto with the surf booming below) and a fresh **maguro** dinner straight off the boats. If anyone wants one more shrine, the third Kumano Sanzan, **Kumano Hayatama Taisha** at Shingū, with its 1,000-year-old sacred *nagi* tree, is a quick stop on the way down.

**For the newer rider:** all paved, relaxed two-lane riding with low mileage. **October note:** still the typhoon-exposed side — check the forecast and road status, and keep lodging cancellable.

**Stops along the way:**
- 🚤 **Doro-kyō jet-boat** — Glass-clear gorge cruise between sheer cliffs (a kid highlight)
- 🥾 **Daimon-zaka** — Short cobbled pilgrim path under giant cedars (kid-friendly)
- 🍜 **Nachi Falls & Kumano Nachi Taisha** — Waterfall, pagoda and shrine, with lunch
- ⛩️ **Kumano Hayatama Taisha (Shingū)** — Optional third Kumano grand shrine & sacred *nagi* tree, on the way down
- ♨️ **Katsuura cave onsen** — Afternoon soak in Bōki-dō, a natural sea-cave bath over the surf

### Day 7 — Capes & White Sand
*Katsuura → Shirahama · 56 mi · 1h51m riding · Wakayama*

A short, scenic coastal day around the peninsula's southern tip. Pause at the photogenic row of sea pillars at **Hashigui-iwa** and the sheer **Sandanbeki** cliffs, then a tuna lunch at **Tore-Tore Ichiba**, rolling into the white-sand resort of **Shirahama** by **early afternoon (~13:30)**. **The early arrival is the whole point** — this is the trip's first beach rest base (two nights), and the afternoon is for the resort, not the road: lay out a towel on the brilliant white quartz sand of **Shirarahama beach** (shallow, gentle swimming for the child), clamber the tilted "1,000-tatami" rock platform of **Senjōjiki**, soak at **Sakino-yu** — a 1,300-year-old rock bath cut right at the surf line — and finish with the classic sunset shot through the natural sea arch of **Engetsu Island**. A relaxed half-day here, deliberately, before the bigger Adventure World rest day tomorrow. *Extending the ride would only eat into the beach time — so it's held short by design.*

**Stops along the way:**
- 📸 **Hashigui-iwa** — A line of pillar rocks marching out to sea
- 🇹🇷 **Kushimoto: Ōshima & the Ertuğrul story** — At Japan's southernmost mainland point, the **Turkish Memorial & Museum** on Ōshima tells the moving 1890 tale of the wrecked Ottoman frigate *Ertuğrul* and the villagers who rescued the survivors — the origin of Japan–Turkey friendship, and a piece of history found nowhere else. Pair it with **Kushimoto Marine Park** (Japan's first marine park, 1970, with an undersea observation tower) for the child. **[KID]** *A distinct historical/coastal hook; pick one to keep the short day relaxed.*
- 📷 **Sandanbeki** — 50 m cliffs with a sea-cave lift to the waves
- 🍜 **Tore-Tore Ichiba** — Giant seafood market for a tuna lunch
- 🏖️ **Shirarahama beach** — White-sand beach time on arrival; shallow, gentle swimming (afternoon)
- 🪨 **Senjōjiki** — Wave-cut "1,000-tatami" rock terrace, fun to clamber on (afternoon)
- ♨️ **Sakino-yu onsen** — Rock open-air bath at the surf line, in use 1,300+ years (afternoon)
- 🌅 **Engetsu Island** — Sunset through the "round-moon" sea arch (early evening)

### Day 8 — Shirahama Rest Day
*Shirahama (light riding) · 15 mi · rest day · Wakayama*

The trip's first beach rest base, built around a single big kid day: **Adventure World**, with giant pandas and a drive-through safari. Beach, an open-air sea-edge onsen and an Engetsu Island sunset round it out.

**Rest-day balance — be honest:** **Adventure World is a full, tiring day out, not downtime.** If the family wants the pandas-and-safari day, accept it as the trip's biggest kid day and bank the real rest into the short Days 6–7 and the evening onsen. For a genuinely low-key version, skip the park and do **Shirarahama beach + the Sakino-yu seaside bath + a Tore-Tore Ichiba seafood lunch** at an easy pace. **Wet-weather fallback:** Adventure World runs rain-or-shine (much of the zoo/marine park is covered); the indoor **Kyoto University Shirahama Aquarium** is a well-rated small rainy-day stop for kids; only the beach is weather-dependent.

> 🧳 **Luggage:** the base-camp case stays in Osaka storage; your full chain case is here at Shirahama. **Forward it onward today, Shirahama → Iya/Oboke** — a remote 2-day leg, so sending it now sees it waiting at the Iya ryokan for your Day-10 check-in. Just the overnight bag rides on for the Day-9 ferry/Naruto stop.

**Stops along the way:**
- 🎯 **Adventure World** — Pandas, safari and a marine park — a full joyful day

### Day 9 — Ferry to Shikoku
*Shirahama → Wakayama → ferry → Naruto · 91 mi ride + ~2h15m ferry · 2h46m riding · Tokushima*

Rather than backtrack through Osaka, the loop crosses the water. Ride up to **Wakayama Port** (a castle stop on the way) and roll the bikes straight onto the **Nankai Ferry** for the ~2h15m sailing to **Tokushima** — a restful break and a small adventure for the child. Land beside **Naruto**, where huge tidal whirlpools churn under the Ōnaruto Bridge.

**A noodle bookend on the strait (Ramen Trail, stops 1–2):** this is a two-ramen day for a noodle lover — the opening leg of the trip's **[Ramen Trail](00-overview.md#the-ramen-trail-a-foodie-thread-for-galiya)**. Before the ferry, Wakayama City is the home of **Wakayama chūka-soba** — a thin-noodle soy-and-pork-bone bowl, classically eaten with a side of pressed **haya-zushi** mackerel sushi while you wait. The marquee shop is **[Ide Shoten (井出商店)](https://maps.google.com/?cid=16098671254010911637)** — the "TV Champion" original that put Wakayama ramen on the national map (4-84 Tanakacho, Wakayama City; a queue forms, so go early); the classic *garage-front-style* **[Marusan (丸三)](https://maps.google.com/?cid=14907922861654292334)** is the calmer alternative. On the far shore, **Tokushima ramen** is the local prize: a dark, sweet-savoury soy-tonkotsu broth topped with stewed pork belly and a raw egg, with rice on the side — the marquee bowl is at **[Chūka Soba Inotani (中華そば いのたに)](https://maps.google.com/?cid=16395492876013054298)** in Tokushima City, the shop most credited with the style. *For Aslan, a plain bowl without the raw egg / pork belly (or a side of rice and gyoza) keeps it kid-simple.*

**Art-lover's option (Galiya):** Naruto is also home to the **Ōtsuka Museum of Art** — Japan's largest exhibition space, with 1,000+ full-size ceramic reproductions of the world's masterpieces (the Sistine Chapel ceiling recreated whole, the *Mona Lisa*, Monet's water lilies) that you can photograph and even touch, which makes it genuinely fun with a 6-year-old. It is a **2–4 h commitment**, so don't force it onto this already-full ferry day — either do a focused highlights loop on arrival, **save it for a relaxed Day-10 morning before riding into Iya**, or keep it as the rainy-day fallback. **Closed Mondays** — check the weekday.

> **Ferry logistics:** Nankai sails ~8×/day each way and is **first-come, first-served for motorcycles (no reservations)** — be at the ticket office 30+ min early; deck space is limited (~20 bikes). Check the day's timetable.
>
> **Whirlpool timing:** they're dramatic only near **spring tides (full/new moon)** and the daily slack swings — aim your Uzunomichi or boat slot at the day's best window.

**Stops along the way:**
- 📷 **Wakayama Castle** — Hilltop keep before the port
