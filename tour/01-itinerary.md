# 22-Day Itinerary & Daily Schedule

**Total ≈ 1,166 miles (1,877 km) over 22 days — an Osaka-anchored loop (start and finish in Osaka).** Distances and driving times are from the **Google Directions API** (real road routing through each day's stops).

**Getting to/from the loop:** the family flies **nonstop SEA ⇄ Tokyo** and rides the **Tōkaidō Shinkansen (~2h30m) Tokyo ⇄ Shin-Osaka** on each end (see [flights](02-flights.md)). A **Tokyo arrival night (Day 0)** buffers jet-lag before the train down to the Day-1 bike pickup, and a **Tokyo departure night** (night of Day 22) precedes the homebound flight (**Day 23**) — so the door-to-door span is ≈ 24 days.

Average riding time on the **13 transfer days is 2h42m** (1h45m–4h33m). No two long days run back-to-back, rest days stay light, and **Day 8 swaps a long road transfer for a ~2h15m Nankai ferry** across the strait to Shikoku. The loop opens down the **central Kii spine** — Kōyasan and the sacred Kumano interior on the paved **Route 168 pilgrim road** — before reaching the Pacific. There are no high alpine passes, so no November snow-closures or near-freezing dawns; but the **Kii interior & Pacific coast (Days 2–8) is the typhoon-exposed side of the loop**, and **Routes 168/311 can close after heavy rain**, so keep early-trip lodging cancellable (see [overview](00-overview.md) and the October notes).

## Day notes

### Day 0 — Tokyo Arrival Night
*SEA ✈ Tokyo (Haneda) → Shinagawa · 0 mi · no riding · Tokyo*

**Arrival bookend (not part of the riding loop).** The **nonstop SEA → Tokyo** flight (ANA/Delta to Haneda, JAL to Narita; ~10h30m) crosses the date line and lands **mid/late afternoon**. From Haneda, the **Keikyu Line** reaches **Shinagawa** (a Shinkansen stop) in ~15 min; overnight near the station for a gentle jet-lag reset. Easy dinner, early night, optional brief Tokyo look if energy allows. Forward the big luggage to Osaka by **takkyūbin** so tomorrow's train is light. See [flights](02-flights.md).

### Day 1 — Shinkansen to Osaka & Bike Pickup
*Tokyo 🚄 Shin-Osaka → Suita base · 0 mi · no riding · Kansai*

**Strictly no riding today.** A relaxed **morning Nozomi** Tokyo/Shinagawa → **Shin-Osaka** (~2h30m, ≈¥14,720/adult, child ~half) brings you in around midday. The bikes come from [Bike Rental Japan](https://bikerentaljapan.com/) in **Suita, northern Osaka** — there is no Tokyo counter, which is exactly why this trip is an Osaka loop. The afternoon is the rental handover only: paperwork and insurance, a full walk-around of the Africa Twin and CB400X, fitting and adjusting the child's gear, pairing the intercoms, loading luggage, and a slow **on-foot shakedown** to learn the controls. The first real ride is **Day 2 morning, fresh and rested.** Explore Osaka on foot, eat well, and have an early night.

> **Connection timing:** confirm the Suita branch's opening hours allow a same-afternoon collection after the Nozomi. If your flight lands late or the family is jet-lagged, the Day-0 Tokyo night absorbs it — never rush the pickup.

See [child-pillion safety](00-overview.md#child-pillion-safety-africa-twin) in the overview before Day 2 — fit and test the footpeg reach, backrest/grab bar and tandem belt while the bike is parked today.

**Stops along the way (all on foot / local):**
- 🎯 **Osaka Castle** — Moats, park and the keep, an easy first-evening stroll
- 🎯 **Kaiyukan Aquarium** — Whale sharks and a kid-pleasing arrival day
- 🍜 **Dōtonbori** — Welcome dinner under the neon — takoyaki & okonomiyaki

### Day 2 — Up to the Sacred Mountain
*Osaka → Kōyasan · 45 mi · 2h19m riding · Kōya, Wakayama*

The first real ride is gentle and short. Slip south out of the Kansai sprawl and pick up the paved **Route 480 / 370** approach that winds up into the forested highlands of **Kōyasan** — a UNESCO World Heritage monastic town founded in 816, set in a high basin at ~800 m. Arrive in good time to walk **Okunoin**, the lantern-lit cedar avenue of 200,000 tombs to Kōbō Daishi's mausoleum, then settle into a **shukubō** (temple lodging) for a **shōjin-ryōri** Buddhist-vegetarian dinner and a quiet mountain night.

**For the newer rider:** ride the first 30 minutes very gently, settling into the bike, the left-side traffic and the group spacing before any pace builds. The final climb is a winding two-lane mountain road — relaxed and well within the cap, with no schedule to beat. Re-check the child's gear at the foot of the climb. **October note:** Kōyasan is cooler than the lowlands and turns colour early — pack a warm layer for the child for the evening.

**Stops along the way:**
- ⛩️ **Okunoin** — Lantern-lit cedar avenue to Kōbō Daishi's mausoleum (an evening walk)
- 🛕 **Danjo Garan & Konpon Daitō** — The vermilion great pagoda, spiritual heart of the mountain
- 🍵 **Shukubō shōjin-ryōri** — Temple lodging and a Buddhist vegetarian dinner

[🗺️ Day route](https://www.google.com/maps/dir/?api=1&origin=Osaka,%20Japan&destination=Okunoin%20Cemetery,%20Koyasan,%20Wakayama,%20Japan&travelmode=driving)

### Day 3 — The Pilgrim Road (Route 168)
*Kōyasan → Hongū / Yunomine · 52 mi · 2h02m riding · Kumano, Wakayama*

Drop off the mountain and turn south down **Route 168**, the classic pilgrim road, following the **Totsukawa River** through deep forested gorges and **Totsukawa**, Japan's largest village by area. Pause to walk the swaying **Tanise suspension bridge** high over the river, then roll into the **Hongū** basin — the spiritual centre of the Kumano Kodō. Visit **Kumano Hongū Taisha**, head shrine of the three Kumano Sanzan, and the nearby **Ōyunohara** sandbank crowned by **Japan's largest torii** (33.9 m). Overnight at the ancient onsen hamlets of **Yunomine** or **Kawayu**.

**For the newer rider:** Route 168 is moderate, paved two-lane — winding but not technical, ridden at a calm pace with frequent valley pull-offs. **October / typhoon note:** this is the most landslide-prone corner of the loop — **Routes 168/311 can close after heavy rain** — so keep these interior nights cancellable and check road and river status before setting off.

**Stops along the way:**
- 🌉 **Tanise no Tsuribashi** — A 297 m swaying pedestrian suspension bridge over the Totsukawa
- ☕ **Totsukawa** — River-valley leg-stretch in Japan's largest village
- ⛩️ **Kumano Hongū Taisha & Ōyunohara** — Head Kumano shrine and Japan's largest torii

[🗺️ Day route](https://www.google.com/maps/dir/?api=1&origin=Okunoin%20Cemetery,%20Koyasan,%20Wakayama,%20Japan&destination=Yunomine%20Onsen,%20Tanabe,%20Wakayama,%20Japan&waypoints=Tanise%20no%20Tsuribashi%20Totsukawa|Kumano%20Hongu%20Taisha&travelmode=driving)

### Day 4 — Kumano Interior Rest Day
*Yunomine / Kawayu (light riding) · 20 mi · rest day · Kumano, Wakayama*

A slow day in the sacred interior. Soak in the tiny **Tsuboyu** at Yunomine — the only UNESCO World Heritage bath you can actually bathe in (reserve a 30-minute slot) — and **boil eggs** in the public hot spring. At **Kawayu**, scoop out your **own riverbed bath** in the warm gravel of the Ōto River. Walk the gentle **Hosshinmon-ōji → Hongū** final section of the Kumano Kodō for a manageable taste of the pilgrimage, and return to **Ōyunohara** for the great torii at golden hour.

**Stops along the way:**
- ♨️ **Tsuboyu, Yunomine** — Soak in the only World Heritage bath you can bathe in
- ♨️ **Kawayu Onsen** — Dig your own bath in the warm riverbed
- ⛩️ **Ōyunohara & Kumano Kodō walk** — Giant torii and a gentle pilgrim-trail stretch

[🗺️ Day route](https://www.google.com/maps/dir/?api=1&origin=Yunomine%20Onsen,%20Tanabe,%20Wakayama,%20Japan&destination=Kumano%20Hongu%20Taisha&waypoints=Tsuboyu%20Yunomine|Kawayu%20Onsen|Oyunohara%20Otorii&travelmode=driving)

### Day 5 — Down to the Sacred Coast
*Yunomine → Doro-kyō → Nachi-Katsuura · 52 mi · 1h52m riding · Wakayama*

A short, scenic descent from the mountains to the Pacific. Drop along the **Kitayama River** to **Doro-kyō**, where a **jet-boat** threads the glass-clear gorge between towering cliffs — a thrill for the child. Continue to the coast for the day's centrepiece: **Nachi Falls**, Japan's tallest waterfall, framed beside the vermilion pagoda of **Kumano Nachi Taisha**, reached up the cobbled **Daimon-zaka** path under giant cedars. Overnight at the tuna port of **Katsuura**, famous for seaside cave onsen.

**For the newer rider:** all paved, relaxed two-lane riding with low mileage. **October note:** still the typhoon-exposed side — check the forecast and road status, and keep lodging cancellable.

**Stops along the way:**
- 🚤 **Doro-kyō jet-boat** — Glass-clear gorge cruise between sheer cliffs (a kid highlight)
- 🥾 **Daimon-zaka** — Short cobbled pilgrim path under giant cedars (kid-friendly)
- 🍜 **Nachi Falls & Kumano Nachi Taisha** — Waterfall, pagoda and shrine, with lunch

[🗺️ Day route](https://www.google.com/maps/dir/?api=1&origin=Yunomine%20Onsen,%20Tanabe,%20Wakayama,%20Japan&destination=Kii-Katsuura,%20Wakayama,%20Japan&waypoints=Doro-kyo%20Gorge%20Kitayama|Nachi%20Falls&travelmode=driving)

### Day 8 — Ferry to Shikoku
*Shirahama → Wakayama → ferry → Naruto · 91 mi ride + ~2h15m ferry · 2h46m riding · Tokushima*

Rather than backtrack through Osaka, the loop crosses the water. Ride up to **Wakayama Port** (a castle stop on the way) and roll the bikes straight onto the **Nankai Ferry** for the ~2h15m sailing to **Tokushima** — a restful break and a small adventure for the child. Land beside **Naruto**, where huge tidal whirlpools churn under the Ōnaruto Bridge.

> **Ferry logistics:** Nankai sails ~8×/day each way and is **first-come, first-served for motorcycles (no reservations)** — be at the ticket office 30+ min early; deck space is limited (~20 bikes). Check the day's timetable.
>
> **Whirlpool timing:** they're dramatic only near **spring tides (full/new moon)** and the daily slack swings — aim your Uzunomichi or boat slot at the day's best window.

**Stops along the way:**
- 📷 **Wakayama Castle** — Hilltop keep before the port
- ⛴️ **Nankai Ferry** — ~2h15m crossing to Shikoku, bikes aboard
- 📸 **Naruto whirlpools** — Uzunomichi glass-floor walkway over the strait

[🗺️ Day route](https://www.google.com/maps/dir/?api=1&origin=Shirahama,%20Wakayama,%20Japan&destination=Naruto,%20Tokushima,%20Japan&waypoints=Wakayama%20Castle|Nankai%20Ferry%20Wakayama%20Port|Tokushima%20Port|Uzunomichi%20Naruto&travelmode=driving)

### Day 20 — Bridge to Awaji & Home
*Kobe → Awaji → Osaka · 64 mi · 1h45m riding · Kansai*

A short, scenic flourish to close the loop: cross the **Akashi Kaikyō Bridge** — for years the longest suspension span on earth — onto **Awaji Island**, loop its gentle, flower-filled north coast, then recross and drop into Osaka. Kept deliberately light so the family arrives unhurried, with the bikes due back the next day.

**Stops along the way:**
- 📸 **Akashi Kaikyō Bridge** — Ride the ~4 km span; photo stop at the Awaji end
- 🎯 **Nijigen no Mori** — Anime park (Naruto/Godzilla/Crayon Shin-chan) — a kid magnet
- 📷 **Awaji Hanasajiki** — Hillside flower fields over the Inland Sea

### Day 21 — Osaka & Bike Return
*Osaka (light riding) · 15 mi · rest day · Kansai*

Return the motorcycles at the Suita base and celebrate the journey. A relaxed Osaka day — castle, aquarium and the food streets of Dōtonbori.

**Stops along the way:**
- 🏯 **Osaka Castle** — Castle & park
- 🐋 **Kaiyukan** — Whale-shark aquarium, a top kids' day
- 🍜 **Dōtonbori** — Neon, street food and a celebration dinner

### Day 22 — Buffer & Shinkansen to Tokyo
*Osaka / Kyoto 🚄 Tokyo · 0 mi · rest day · Kansai → Tokyo*

Flexible buffer day that also **repositions to Tokyo** for the flight home. **Kyoto sits right on the Tōkaidō line**, so you can sightsee in the morning — **Fushimi Inari's** torii gates or a last Osaka food stroll — then board an **afternoon Nozomi at Kyoto (or Shin-Osaka) → Tokyo** (~2h30m) and **overnight near Haneda/Shinagawa**. The slack here also absorbs any earlier weather day. Have the travel cases **forwarded ahead** to the Tokyo hotel by takkyūbin so the train is light. See [flights](02-flights.md).

**Stops along the way:**
- 🎯 **Fushimi Inari, Kyoto** — Thousand vermilion torii gates (board the Shinkansen at Kyoto Station after)
- 🎯 **Osaka Castle** — A last castle & park stroll
- 📷 **Kaiyukan / Dōtonbori** — Aquarium or final food street

### Day 23 — Fly Home
*Tokyo ✈ Seattle (nonstop) · 0 mi · no riding · Tokyo*

**Departure bookend (not part of the riding loop).** From your Tokyo hotel, head to the airport and fly the **nonstop Tokyo → Seattle** leg (ANA/Delta from Haneda, JAL from Narita). You re-cross the date line and **land in Seattle the same calendar day**. The big luggage, forwarded by takkyūbin, is waiting — or carried with you for the flight. See [flights](02-flights.md).
