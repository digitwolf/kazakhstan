## This is the reference line

The tour is planned **anchor-first** — 5 fixed anchors with route **options** between them (see [03-anchors-and-options.md](03-anchors-and-options.md)). The table above is the **reference itinerary**: the default option through every segment. By day: **D3 = S1a** (Steppe Classic), **D4–5 = S2a** (Hot Springs & the Canyon), **D6 = A2 day loop** (Jeti-Oguz/Altyn-Arashan), **D7 = S3a** (the Glacier Road), **D9–10 = S4a split** (Eagle Hunters & 33 Parrots, Kochkor overnight), **D11–13 + 14 = S5a** (Grand Western Loop), **D15 = S6a** (Kolsai Morning & the Run Home). The anchor nights (A1 Basshi, A2 Karakol ×2, A3 south-shore yurt camp ×2 incl. the Day-8 rest day, A4 Song-Kol, A5 Saty) are fixed whichever options the group picks.

## Shape of the trip

- **Riding legs:** 120 + 250 + 250 + 280 + 110 + 190 + 200 + 130 + 240 + 153 + 261 + 325 + 300 = **2,809 km** over **13 riding days (incl. the training day)**; the **14-day tour body = 1 training day + 12 riding days + 1 rest day** — the same 14+2 shape as the Morocco reference tour. Average ≈ 216 km/riding day, longest **Day 14 (325 km, incl. the Karkara border)**, shortest **Day 6 (~110 km, the Karakol day loop) and Day 2 (~120 km, the training day)**.
- **Day 2 is the training day** — the first day of the tour body: a skills session on the DR650 (bike setup, off-road stance, gravel braking, sand basics) plus an easy ~120 km foothill/steppe loop out of Almaty — the same warm-up formula as the Morocco tour, dialing in the rental before the big days.
- **The hard block is Days 7–12:** three passes above 3,000 m (one above 4,000 m), two nights at ~3,000 m, and the roughest surfaces of the trip — but the **Day-8 rest day** at the south-shore yurt camp splits it after the 4,021 m opener. Days 2–6 build up to it; Days 13–15 are the scenic tarmac-and-canyon run home.
- **Two-night stays:** Karakol (D5–6, Jeti-Oguz + Altyn-Arashan day loop) and Issyk-Kul south shore (D7–8, the rest day). Altyn-Emel is **one night** — the Singing Dune and the Aktau mountains are ridden **en route to Chundzha on Day 4**, the operator's original 250 km leg.
- **Two border crossings, same post:** Kegen/Karkara southbound on **Day 5** (KZ → KG, clocks **+1 h**) and northbound on **Day 14** (KG → KZ, clocks **−1 h** — Kazakhstan unified on UTC+5 in March 2024; Kyrgyzstan is UTC+6). The operator handles the bike paperwork both ways; riders handle their own passports (see [02-getting-started.md](02-getting-started.md)).
- **Overnights:** hotel (Almaty ×2) → guest house (Basshi) → hot-springs resort (Chundzha) → hotel (Karakol ×2) → yurt camp (Issyk-Kul ×2) → guest house (Kochkor) → yurt camp (Song-Kol) → cabin camp (Suusamyr) → hotel (Bishkek) → hotel (Cholpon-Ata) → guest house (Saty) → hotel (Almaty). All twin-share, included; single supplement to confirm for the custom edition.
- **Pricing:** the operator's published 10-day tour is USD 3,000 + USD 500 deposit; this loop is a **custom extended edition — indicative ≈ USD 4,500, price to confirm with the operator**.
