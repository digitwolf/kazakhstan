# Trip Overview

A scenic, family-friendly **3-week motorcycle tour of Japan** for a family of three, run as a premium ADV-style **paved** touring trip — not a hard adventure ride or a mileage challenge.

## The family
Full profiles, riding backgrounds and a "what we like" planning checklist are in **[00-family.md](00-family.md)** — read it before planning.
- **Rider 1 — Ruslan (37)** — experienced rider (FJR → BMW R 1300 GS; a 2-week Morocco ADV trip with Bret Tkacs), on a large ADV motorcycle (ideally Honda Africa Twin), carrying the child + snacks, rain layers, tools, first-aid. Can ride big/long — he is *not* the limiting rider.
- **Rider 2 — Galiya (41)** — long-time pillion but a **newer solo rider**, on a sub-500cc bike (ideally Honda CB400X / NX400). The whole plan is built around her confidence; she rides near the front so her pace sets the group. A **big foodie**; loves Japanese art, Studio Ghibli/*Ponyo*, sushi and ramen.
- **Passenger — Aslan (6)** — son riding with Ruslan, in full fitting gear, with stops every 60–90 minutes. Can manage up to ~6h on a good day but **not long days back-to-back**. Likes Transformers, Mario/Nintendo, Minecraft, *KPop Demon Hunters*, and buying toys.

## Shape of the trip
- **An Osaka-anchored riding loop inside a ≈26-day door-to-door trip (Day 0–25)** — pick up *and* return the bikes at the rental base in **Suita, Osaka** (the chosen operator has no Tokyo counter, so this is a loop, not a one-way). Home base is **Seattle** — see [flights](02-flights.md).
- **Getting there/back:** fly **nonstop SEA ⇄ Tokyo** (Haneda HND on ANA/Delta, or Narita NRT on JAL; ~10h30m), then ride the **Tōkaidō Shinkansen Tokyo ⇄ Shin-Osaka** (~2h30m Nozomi) to/from the loop — **not** an Osaka-airport (KIX) ticket. The riding loop is bracketed by city days at each end: a **Tokyo arrival night (Day 0)** and a **full Tokyo museum day (Day 1 — Ghibli Museum, teamLab Planets, Nintendo TOKYO)** precede the **Day-2 train down + bike pickup**; at the far end the **bikes return on Day 22**, a **Super Nintendo World / USJ day fills Day 23**, and a **reposition-to-Tokyo day (Day 24)** precedes the **homebound flight (Day 25)**. So the door-to-door span is **≈ 26 days (Day 0–25)**. Forward the big luggage by **takkyūbin** so the train legs stay light.
- **≈ 1,166 miles (1,877 km)** total; the **13 transfer days average 2h42m** (1h45m–4h33m), comfortably inside the **4–6 hour cap** with no two long days back-to-back.
- **A temple night + six 2-night rest bases:** the loop opens with a **shukubō (temple-lodging) night at Kōyasan**, then settles at six 2-night bases — **Kumano interior (Yunomine/Hongū)**, Shirahama, Iya/Oboke, Shimanto River, Dōgo Onsen/Matsuyama, Onomichi/Setoda.
- **One ferry, no high passes:** Day 9 crosses to Shikoku by the ~2h15m Nankai ferry; the loop carries no alpine passes, so there are no November snow-closures — but the **Kii interior & Pacific coast (Days 3–9) is the typhoon-exposed side**, and **Routes 168/311 in the Kumano interior can close after heavy rain**, so keep early lodging cancellable.
- **Best season:** October–early November, or late April–May after Golden Week.

## Priorities (in order)
1. Safety for a newer rider  2. Comfort for the child  3. Scenic paved roads  4. Remote villages & rural Japan  5. Beautiful onsen towns  6. Kid-friendly stops  7. Good hotels & food  8. Moderate daily distances  9. Rest days in beautiful places  10. Avoiding urban traffic.

## Avoid
Dense city riding, narrow mountain roads after dark, wet twisties, weekend rider roads, tiny Google-Maps shortcuts, back-to-back long transfers, technical gravel. Season-wise: rainy season, August heat, winter mountains.

## Before you go (per rider)
Valid home motorcycle licence · **1949 Geneva International Driving Permit** (physical, stamped category "A") · passport · credit card · rental confirmation · **travel insurance covering motorcycle riding** · written confirmation the rental allows **passenger and child-passenger** use and a large bike.

## Bike rental (Osaka loop, large bike + child pillion)
Rebasing the trip to an **Osaka loop** is driven by real rental availability: a true one-way Tokyo→Osaka large-bike hire essentially isn't an off-the-shelf product (the biggest chain, Rental819, is loop-only), and the operator that stocks **both** planned bikes does so **only from Osaka**. So pick up and return in Osaka.

- **Bike Rental Japan (bikerentaljapan.com)** — single base at **4-10 Senrioka Shimo, Suita-shi, Osaka** (no Tokyo counter). It is the operator confirmed to stock **both** the **Honda Africa Twin CRF1000L** and the **Honda CB400X** (six units) from one counter, and is the most touring/point-to-point friendly. Min age 20; requires a physical 1949 Geneva IDP, category "A".

**Get all of this in writing before paying a deposit:**
1. The **explicit 22-day quote** for both bikes (which rate tier applies) and the **deposit/hold** amount.
2. **Passenger use is permitted, including a child passenger** on the Africa Twin (some rentals forbid pillions or under-age passengers outright), and that a small child can reach the rear footpegs.
3. A large bike (Africa Twin class) for Rider 1 **and** a sub-500cc (CB400X / NX400 class) for Rider 2 are both available for the dates.
4. **ETC card** included/available for each bike (for expressway tolls — used mainly on the Day-20 Himeji/Kobe run and the Day-21 Akashi Kaikyō bridge to Awaji; the early Kōyasan/Kumano days are ridden on the scenic toll-free Route 480/168).
5. **Luggage**: panniers/top-box fitted or allowed (an optional support-truck add-on exists), and where to leave the flight-day bags.
6. Insurance/excess, breakdown cover, and what counts as the licence + IDP they require.
7. The **Nankai ferry** (Day 9, Wakayama→Tokushima) is **first-come, first-served for motorcycles** — no booking; arrive 30+ min early.

## Child-pillion safety (Africa Twin)
The 6-year-old rides on the Africa Twin with Rider 1. Before Day 3, set up and test all of this **while the bike is parked** (do it on Day 2):

- **Footpeg reach.** A 6-year-old's legs may not reach the stock passenger pegs comfortably — fit **lowered/highway pegs or peg-lowering brackets** so feet sit flat and secure. Feet that dangle get tired and slip.
- **Backrest & grab points.** Add a **passenger backrest/top-box backrest** so the child can't slide rearward, plus a **proper grab rail or grab strap** at the waist — don't rely on the child holding the rider.
- **Tandem riding belt.** Use a **child–adult tandem safety belt** (harness linking the child to the rider) so a tired child can't lean away or doze off the bike.
- **Gear.** Full properly-fitted child helmet (correct shell size, not an adult hand-me-down), armoured jacket, gloves, boots; ear protection helps on longer legs.
- **Pace & limits.** Stops every **60–90 minutes**; watch for the child going quiet or fidgety (early fatigue). **A 5h+ day in the saddle is too long for a 6-year-old** — this is a core reason the loop's longest day is held to ~4h33m (Day 13) and why rest days are spaced through the route. Never ride the child after dark or when they're overtired.
