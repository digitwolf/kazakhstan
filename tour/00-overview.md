# Trip Overview — "From Desert Up to Glaciers" (the Loop Edition)

A **16-day guided ADV motorcycle loop across Kazakhstan and Kyrgyzstan** with **[Silk Off Road Tours](https://www.silkoffroadtours.com/motorcycle-tours-kazakhstan-kyrgyzstan)** — **starting and finishing in Almaty, no open-jaw** — from the singing dunes of the Kazakh steppe, over a chain of 3,000–4,000 m Tian Shan passes, to the glaciers above Issyk-Kul and the nomad pastures of Song-Kol, then home along the lake's north shore, back over the Karkara border and through the Kolsai Lakes.

## The goal

**Built for experienced ADV riders who value beautiful offroad — the roads are the product.** The anchors are the frame, but the reason to come is what's under the tires: desert pistes, a 4WD gorge track, a gravel road to 4,021 m, a stacked-hairpin climb to Song-Kol and a rocky serpentine off its rim (the full day-by-day riding record is **[04-roads.md](04-roads.md)**). For this rider it's also a **real ADV step-up**: after a guided Morocco training tour, this is the graduation ride — **desert to 4,000 m glaciers on the old Silk Road**, roughly 40% of it on gravel and dirt, on a light rental dual-sport instead of the big GS at home. The format is the proven "anchor-point adventure": a guided ride with a ride leader and support truck, fixed nightly anchors (hotels, guest houses, yurt camps), and genuinely adventurous riding between them — now with a **dedicated training day** before the big legs and a **real rest day** in the middle, Morocco-style. Because the participants for this departure **aren't fixed yet**, the tour is deliberately **designed anchor-first**: the must-see anchor nights are locked, and the connections between them are a menu of easier/harder **route options** the actual group will choose (see below).

## The flexible anchor model

The plan is **5 fixed anchors + route options between them** — Morocco-style. **Base: Almaty** (Day 1 arrival + Day 2 training day; Day 15 return night + celebration dinner; Day 16 departure). The anchors, pre-booked and non-negotiable:

- **A1 — Altyn-Emel / Singing Dune** (Basshi guest house)
- **A2 — Karakol** (2 nights — Jeti-Oguz/Altyn-Arashan day loop on the second day)
- **A3 — Issyk-Kul south shore yurt camp** — ★ **the rest-day anchor**, 2 nights, mid-trip
- **A4 — Song-Kol Lake yurt camp** (3,016 m)
- **A5 — Saty / Kolsai Lakes** (Lake Kaindy)

Each pair of anchors is connected by two or more **options** trading difficulty and pace (glacier passes vs. coast roads, desert pistes vs. a direct canyon day); the [16-day itinerary](01-itinerary.md) is the **reference line** — the default choice through the options. Days freed by faster options become extra anchor nights or weather buffer; the Day-2 training day doubles as the option-selector. Full detail, per-segment: **[03-anchors-and-options.md](03-anchors-and-options.md)**.

## The route in one paragraph

Fly into **Almaty** (Day 1, bike handover and welcome dinner), spend **Day 2 on a training day** — a DR650 skills session plus an easy ~120 km foothill/steppe loop — then ride north-east to **Altyn-Emel National Park** for a night in Basshi. The next leg rides the **Singing Dune** and the white-and-red **Aktau mountains** **en route to Chundzha** — the operator's original 250 km desert-piste day — ending with a soak at the **Chundzha hot springs**. Cross into Kyrgyzstan via **Charyn Canyon** and the quiet **Kegen/Karkara border**, and base **two nights in Karakol** with a day loop to **Jeti-Oguz** and the **Altyn-Arashan** hot-springs gorge. Then the big mountains: up **Barskoon gorge** past glaciers over **Suek Pass (4,021 m)** and down to **Issyk-Kul's south shore** for **two yurt-camp nights and the Day-8 rest day**; along the Terskey Alatau to **Kochkor** (lunch with an eagle-hunting family); over the **33 Parrots Pass** switchbacks to alpine **Song-Kol Lake (3,016 m)**; a rocky off-road day over **Moldo-Ashuu** and **Kara-Keche** into the vast **Suusamyr valley**; and over **Too-Ashuu Pass** down to **Bishkek**. The return leg makes it a loop: through the **Boom gorge** to **Cholpon-Ata** and its Bronze-Age **petroglyphs** on the lake's north shore, back over the **Karkara border** into Kazakhstan (clocks −1 h), a night in **Saty** for **Lake Kaindy's sunken forest** and the **Kolsai Lakes**, then the run home to **Almaty** for the celebration dinner and the flight out. **≈ 2,800 km**, a 14-day tour body — one training day, twelve riding days, one rest day — two border crossings, start = finish.

## Priorities

0. **The roads are the product** — this loop is chosen for riders who value beautiful offroad: when an option trades comfort for a better track (and the weather allows), the track wins. The per-day surface record lives in [04-roads.md](04-roads.md).
1. **Use the training day** — Day 2 exists to dial in the DR650 (levers, pressures, sag) and refresh off-road stance, gravel braking and sand basics *before* the big days. Arrive with nothing to prove on Day 3.
2. **Ride the step-up honestly** — take the gravel/dirt options (the desert pistes on Day 4 have an asphalt alternative; ride the pistes if conditions allow).
3. **Altitude discipline** — hydrate, layer, respect the 4,000 m day and the nights at 3,000 m; the Day-8 rest day is part of the acclimatization plan, not a luxury.
4. **The group experience** — hold the ride leader's structure; the support truck is the safety net, not a crutch.
5. **Central Asian culture at the anchors** — yurt life at Song-Kol and the south shore, the eagle-hunting family, Karakol's Orthodox church and Dungan mosque, the Cholpon-Ata petroglyphs, the hot springs.
6. **Eat the Silk Road** — lunches and dinners are excluded, which is a feature: every meal is a local pick (see the Food Trail below).
7. **Come home with margin** — no heroics on the rocky descents; a dropped DR650 is a deposit conversation, an injury ends the trip.

## The Silk Road Food Trail

Lunches and dinners are on the rider, so the route doubles as a food tour of Kazakh, Kyrgyz, Uyghur and Dungan cooking:

- **Beshbarmak** — the national dish of both countries: boiled meat (often horse in Kazakhstan, mutton in Kyrgyzstan) over broad hand-cut noodles with onion broth. Best at guest-house tables, not restaurants.
- **Plov** — Central Asian rice pilaf with mutton, carrots and cumin, cooked in a kazan. Everywhere from Almaty to Bishkek.
- **Laghman** — hand-pulled noodles with peppers and meat, a Uyghur specialty; the Chundzha area (Uyghur District) is the place for it.
- **Manty** — big steamed dumplings of mutton and onion, a roadside-café staple.
- **Shashlik** — skewered meat over coals; judge a stop by its smoke.
- **Ashlan-fuu in Karakol** — the town's famous Dungan cold noodle dish, spicy-vinegary starch noodles in broth; the classic version is at the Karakol bazaar stalls, pennies a bowl.
- **Kumis & boorsok at Song-Kol** — fermented mare's milk and fried dough pillows offered in the herders' yurts; accepting a bowl is the polite (and memorable) thing to do.
- **Lakeside fish at Issyk-Kul** — smoked and fried whole fish sold along both shores; the north-shore stands around Cholpon-Ata are the classic Day-13 stop.
