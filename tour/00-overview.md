# Trip Overview

A scenic, family-friendly **7-day Pacific Northwest motorcycle "first tour"** for a family of three — built to **grow a brand-new rider's skills and her love of touring**, not to chase mileage. It runs as a relaxed coast-and-mountains loop from home, on the family's **own** bikes, entirely on paved two-lanes off the freeways.

## The goal
The trip exists for one reason: **Galiya just earned her motorcycle endorsement**, and this is her **first multi-day tour as a solo rider**. Everything — the route, the daily distances, the road choices, the fuel stops — is shaped to make her first tour comfortable, confidence-building and joyful, with a memorable coast, a Fourth-of-July rest day, and a triumphant mountain ride home.

## The family
Full profiles, riding backgrounds and a "what we like" planning checklist are in **[00-family.md](00-family.md)** — read it before planning.
- **Rider 2 — Galiya** — the **newly-endorsed solo rider** this whole trip is built around, on her own **Kawasaki W230** (233cc single, happiest 55–65 mph, ~3.4-gal tank). She rides up front; **her confidence sets the pace**. A big foodie who loves the coast, seafood, lighthouses and art.
- **Rider 1 — Ruslan (37)** — seasoned rider (BMW R1300GS; a 2-week Morocco ADV trip), carrying **Aslan as pillion** plus the snacks, rain layers, tools and first-aid. He is *not* the limiting rider — he rides steady and keeps Galiya's pace.
- **Passenger — Aslan (6)** — rides with dad in full fitting gear, with stops every 60–90 minutes. Can manage up to ~6h on a good day but **not long days back-to-back**. Loves toys/souvenirs, animals, hands-on museums, the volcano and the cheese factory.

## Shape of the trip
- **A home loop from Woodinville, WA** (15402 NE 176th Pl, 98072) — **out down the Washington and Oregon coast, home over the Cascades.** The family rides their **own** motorcycles round-trip: **no rental, no flights, no passport or IDP.** The trip starts in the garage (see [getting started](02-getting-started.md)).
- **Dates:** depart **Wednesday 1 July 2026**, home **Tuesday 7 July 2026** — **7 days**. The single **rest day is Saturday 4 July** in Yachats, built around the village's **La De Da Parade and bay fireworks**.
- **One ferry, no freeways:** Day 1 opens by rolling the bikes onto the short **Edmonds–Kingston ferry** (Washington State Ferries — no motorcycle reservation; arrive early) to skip Seattle traffic, then drops to the quiet **Hood Canal** shore. From there the route stays on **coastal and mountain two-lanes the whole way** — deliberately **off all Interstates and high-speed highways** for the new rider.
- **≈ 970 riding miles over 7 days.** Distances and drive times are from the **Google Directions API** (real road routing). The **average riding day is ≈ 3h40m**; the **longest day is Day 5** (Yachats → Mount St. Helens, ~217 mi / ~4.1h), the one true coast-to-Cascades transfer. Every day stays inside the **4–6h comfort cap**, with **no two long days back-to-back** and breaks every 60–90 minutes for Aslan.
- **One 2-night rest base:** **Yachats**, the trip's emotional high point and "nice hotel" goal — two unhurried nights with the bikes parked over the Fourth of July. Every other night is a single-night stop (Westport, Cannon Beach, the Mount St. Helens gateway at Castle Rock/Silver Lake, and the Rainier gateway at Packwood). Astoria is an afternoon stop en route on Day 2.
- **The ride home is the reward:** Day 7 returns *through* **Mount Rainier National Park** — Paradise, Reflection Lakes, Narada Falls — and over **Chinook Pass (SR-410)**, the new rider's graduation ride, before the descent through Enumclaw to the driveway.
- **Best timing:** early July is the sweet spot — the coast is dry and lively for the Fourth, the Cascade wildflowers are coming on at Paradise, and **Chinook Pass normally clears of snow and reopens by early July** (always confirm the WSDOT pass report).

## Priorities (in order)
1. Safety & confidence for a brand-new solo rider  2. Comfort for the child  3. Scenic paved roads (no freeways)  4. Standout coast food  5. Lighthouses, coast & wildlife  6. Kid-friendly stops every day  7. Good lodging  8. Moderate daily distances  9. A real rest day over the Fourth  10. Avoiding city/Interstate traffic.

## Avoid
Freeways and Interstates of any kind, high-speed highways, long back-to-back transfers, riding after dark, pushed-hard wet twisties, gambling on the W230's fuel range, and Seattle traffic (the ferry exists to skip it).

## Child-pillion safety (BMW R1300GS)
The 6-year-old rides on the GS with Ruslan. Set up and test all of this **while the bike is parked** before Day 1, then confirm it on the gentle Day-1 Hood Canal opener before the longer days:

- **Footpeg reach.** A 6-year-old's legs may not reach the stock passenger pegs — fit **lowered/highway pegs or peg-lowering brackets** so feet sit flat and secure; dangling feet get tired and slip.
- **Backrest & grab points.** Add a **passenger backrest** so he can't slide rearward, plus a **proper grab rail or grab strap** at the waist — don't rely on him holding the rider.
- **Tandem riding belt.** Use a **child–adult tandem safety belt** so a tired child can't lean away or doze off the bike.
- **Gear.** A properly-fitted child helmet (correct shell size, not an adult hand-me-down), armoured jacket, gloves, boots; ear protection helps on longer legs.
- **Pace & limits.** Stops every **60–90 minutes**; watch for him going quiet or fidgety (early fatigue). Keep the long Day-5 transfer steady and broken into short hops, and **never run two long days back-to-back** — which is exactly why the Fourth-of-July rest day sits between the coast days and the long inland transfer.

Valid Washington/Oregon **motorcycle endorsement** (Galiya — newly earned) · current registration & insurance on both bikes · pre-trip service (tires, chain/final drive, brakes, lights) · **intercoms paired** · **Mount Rainier park entry/timed-entry sorted** · confirmed **secure motorcycle parking + passenger/child rules** at each lodging · the WSDOT Chinook Pass report checked before Day 7. No passport, IDP or rental paperwork — these are the family's own bikes (see [getting started](02-getting-started.md)).
