# Trip Overview

A scenic, family-friendly **3-week motorcycle tour of Japan** for a family of three, run as a premium ADV-style **paved** touring trip — not a hard adventure ride or a mileage challenge.

## The family
- **Rider 1** — experienced, on a large ADV motorcycle (ideally Honda Africa Twin), carrying the child + snacks, rain layers, tools, first-aid.
- **Rider 2** — newer rider, on a sub-500cc bike (ideally Honda CB400X / NX400). The whole plan is built around her confidence; she rides near the front so her pace sets the group.
- **Passenger** — 6-year-old son riding with Rider 1, in full fitting gear, with stops every 60–90 minutes.

## Shape of the trip
- **21 days**, picking up bikes near **Tokyo/Yokohama** and dropping near **Osaka/Kansai** (one-way rental). Home base is **Seattle** — see [flights](02-flights.md).
- **≈ 1,439 miles (2,316 km)** total; daily rides usually **4–6 hours max** (no two long days back-to-back).
- **Five 2-night rest bases:** Hakone, Iya/Oboke, Shimanto River, Dogo Onsen/Matsuyama, Onomichi/Setoda.
- **Best season:** October–early November, or late April–May after Golden Week.

## Priorities (in order)
1. Safety for a newer rider  2. Comfort for the child  3. Scenic paved roads  4. Remote villages & rural Japan  5. Beautiful onsen towns  6. Kid-friendly stops  7. Good hotels & food  8. Moderate daily distances  9. Rest days in beautiful places  10. Avoiding urban traffic.

## Avoid
Dense city riding, narrow mountain roads after dark, wet twisties, weekend rider roads, tiny Google-Maps shortcuts, back-to-back long transfers, technical gravel. Season-wise: rainy season, August heat, winter mountains.

## Before you go (per rider)
Valid home motorcycle licence · **1949 Geneva International Driving Permit** · passport · credit card · rental confirmation · **travel insurance covering motorcycle riding** · written confirmation the rental allows **passenger and child-passenger** use and a large bike. Don't assume one-way rental is available — confirm first.

## Bike rental (one-way Tokyo → Kansai, large bike + child pillion)
One-way large-bike rental with a child passenger is the single biggest thing to lock down — sort it before booking flights. Two operators worth approaching first:

- **Rental819 (rental-bike.jp)** — Japan's largest motorcycle-rental network, with Honda Africa Twin and other big ADV bikes and many branches in both the Tokyo and Osaka/Kansai areas. Ask specifically whether they will do a one-way Tokyo-pickup / Kansai-drop and what the drop fee is.
- **Hertz Ride Japan** — foreigner-friendly, English support, large ADV/touring bikes out of Tokyo; confirm one-way drop options and child-pillion policy.

**Get all of this in writing before paying a deposit:**
1. **One-way drop** is allowed Tokyo → Osaka/Kansai, and the **one-way drop fee** in writing.
2. **Passenger use is permitted, including a child passenger** (some rentals forbid pillions or under-age passengers outright).
3. A large bike (Africa Twin class) for Rider 1 **and** a sub-500cc (CB400X / NX400 class) for Rider 2 are both available for the dates.
4. **ETC card** included/available for each bike (for expressway tolls — essential on Days 8 and 19).
5. **Luggage**: panniers/top-box fitted or allowed, and where to leave the flight-day bags.
6. Insurance/excess, breakdown cover, and what counts as the licence + IDP they require.

## Child-pillion safety (Africa Twin)
The 6-year-old rides on the Africa Twin with Rider 1. Before Day 2, set up and test all of this **while the bike is parked** (do it on Day 1):

- **Footpeg reach.** A 6-year-old's legs may not reach the stock passenger pegs comfortably — fit **lowered/highway pegs or peg-lowering brackets** so feet sit flat and secure. Feet that dangle get tired and slip.
- **Backrest & grab points.** Add a **passenger backrest/top-box backrest** so the child can't slide rearward, plus a **proper grab rail or grab strap** at the waist — don't rely on the child holding the rider.
- **Tandem riding belt.** Use a **child–adult tandem safety belt** (harness linking the child to the rider) so a tired child can't lean away or doze off the bike.
- **Gear.** Full properly-fitted child helmet (correct shell size, not an adult hand-me-down), armoured jacket, gloves, boots; ear protection helps on longer legs.
- **Pace & limits.** Stops every **60–90 minutes**; watch for the child going quiet or fidgety (early fatigue). **A 5h+ day in the saddle is too long for a 6-year-old** — this is a core reason Day 8 was reshaped and why rest days are spaced through the route. Never ride the child after dark or when they're overtired.

## High-level route
Seattle ✈ → Tokyo/Yokohama → Hakone → Izu → Fuji Five Lakes → Kiso Valley (Magome/Tsumago) → Awaji → Iya/Oboke → Kochi → Shimanto → Uchiko/Uwajima → Dogo Onsen/Matsuyama → Shimanami Kaido → Onomichi/Setoda → Kurashiki → Himeji/Kobe → Osaka/Kansai ✈ Seattle
