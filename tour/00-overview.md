# Trip Overview

A scenic, family-friendly **3-week motorcycle tour of Japan** for a family of three, run as a premium ADV-style **paved** touring trip — not a hard adventure ride or a mileage challenge.

## The family
Full profiles, riding backgrounds and a "what we like" planning checklist are in **[00-family.md](00-family.md)** — read it before planning.
- **Rider 1 — Ruslan (37)** — experienced rider (FJR → BMW R 1300 GS; a 2-week Morocco ADV trip with Bret Tkacs), on a large ADV motorcycle (ideally Honda Africa Twin), carrying the child + snacks, rain layers, tools, first-aid. Can ride big/long — he is *not* the limiting rider.
- **Rider 2 — Galiya (41)** — long-time pillion but a **newer solo rider**, on a sub-500cc bike (ideally Honda CB400X / NX400). The whole plan is built around her confidence; she rides near the front so her pace sets the group. A **big foodie**; loves Japanese art, Studio Ghibli/*Ponyo*, sushi and ramen.
- **Passenger — Aslan (6)** — son riding with Ruslan, in full fitting gear, with stops every 60–90 minutes. Can manage up to ~6h on a good day but **not long days back-to-back**. Likes Transformers, Mario/Nintendo, Minecraft, *KPop Demon Hunters*, and buying toys.

## Shape of the trip
- **An Osaka-anchored riding loop inside a ≈25-day door-to-door trip (Day 0–24)** — pick up *and* return the bikes at the rental base in **Suita, Osaka** (the chosen operator has no Tokyo counter, so this is a loop, not a one-way). Home base is **Seattle** — see [flights](02-flights.md).
- **Getting there/back:** fly **nonstop SEA ⇄ Tokyo** (Haneda HND on ANA/Delta, or Narita NRT on JAL; ~10h30m), then ride the **Tōkaidō Shinkansen Tokyo ⇄ Shin-Osaka** (~2h30m Nozomi) to/from the loop — **not** an Osaka-airport (KIX) ticket. The riding loop is bracketed by city days at each end: a **Tokyo arrival night (Day 0)** and a **full Tokyo museum day (Day 1 — Ghibli Museum, teamLab Planets, Nintendo TOKYO)** precede the **Day-2 train down + bike pickup**; at the far end the **bikes return on Day 21**, a **Super Nintendo World / USJ day fills Day 22**, and a **reposition-to-Tokyo day (Day 23)** precedes the **homebound flight (Day 24)**. So the door-to-door span is **≈ 25 days (Day 0–24)**.
- **Luggage plan:** because bikes can't carry suitcases, forward the main case **rest-base to rest-base** by **takkyūbin** — it leapfrogs ahead from each 2-night base to the next (Osaka → Shirahama → Iya → Dōgo/Matsuyama → Onomichi → Osaka) while the family rides only a soft overnight bag + gear between bases; a heavier base-camp case can simply wait in Osaka storage for the whole loop. (Full chain, exceptions and bookend forwards in the [itinerary luggage plan](01-itinerary.md) and [touring-practical](../research/touring-practical.md#luggage-forwarding-takkyūbin--takuhaibin--the-key-tool-for-a-moto-family).)
- **≈ 1,171 miles (1,884 km)** total; the **12 transfer days average 2h56m** (1h45m–4h33m), comfortably inside the **4–6 hour cap** with no two long days back-to-back.
- **Six 2-night rest bases:** a **quick daytime stop at Kōyasan** (no temple-lodging overnight — the family isn't after a deep spiritual stay) opens the ride on Day 3, which runs straight on to the first base; the loop then settles at six 2-night bases — **Kumano interior (Yunomine/Hongū)**, Shirahama, Iya/Oboke, Shimanto River, Dōgo Onsen/Matsuyama, Onomichi/Setoda.
- **One ferry, no high passes:** Day 8 crosses to Shikoku by the ~2h15m Nankai ferry; the loop carries no alpine passes, so there are no November snow-closures — but the **Kii interior & Pacific coast (Days 3–8) is the typhoon-exposed side**, and **Routes 168/311 in the Kumano interior can close after heavy rain**, so keep early lodging cancellable.
- **Best season:** October–early November, or late April–May after Golden Week.

## Priorities (in order)
1. Safety for a newer rider  2. Comfort for the child  3. Scenic paved roads  4. Remote villages & rural Japan  5. Beautiful onsen towns  6. Kid-friendly stops  7. Good hotels & food  8. Moderate daily distances  9. Rest days in beautiful places  10. Avoiding urban traffic.

## Avoid
Dense city riding, narrow mountain roads after dark, wet twisties, weekend rider roads, tiny Google-Maps shortcuts, back-to-back long transfers, technical gravel. Season-wise: rainy season, August heat, winter mountains.

## Before you go (per rider)
Valid home motorcycle licence · **1949 Geneva International Driving Permit** (physical, stamped category "A") · passport · credit card · rental confirmation · **travel insurance covering motorcycle riding** · written confirmation the rental allows **passenger and child-passenger** use and a large bike.

## Bike rental (Osaka loop, large bike + child pillion)
Rebasing the trip to an **Osaka loop** is driven by real rental availability: a true one-way Tokyo→Osaka large-bike hire essentially isn't an off-the-shelf product (the biggest chain, Rental819, is loop-only), and the operator that stocks **both** planned bikes does so **only from Osaka**. So pick up and return in Osaka.

- **Bike Rental Japan (bikerentaljapan.com)** — single base at **4-10 Senrioka Shimo, Suita-shi, Osaka** (no Tokyo counter). It is the operator confirmed to stock **both** the **Honda Africa Twin CRF1000L** and the **Honda CB400X** (six units) from one counter, and is the most touring/point-to-point friendly. Min age 20; requires a physical 1949 Geneva IDP, category "A".

**Get all of this in writing before paying a deposit:**
1. The **explicit 22-day quote** for both bikes (which rate tier applies) and the **deposit/hold** amount.
2. **Passenger use is permitted, including a child passenger** on the Africa Twin (some rentals forbid pillions or under-age passengers outright), and that a small child can reach the rear footpegs.
3. A large bike (Africa Twin class) for Rider 1 **and** a sub-500cc (CB400X / NX400 class) for Rider 2 are both available for the dates.
4. **ETC card** included/available for each bike (for expressway tolls — used mainly on the Day-19 Himeji/Kobe run and the Day-20 Akashi Kaikyō bridge to Awaji; the early Kōyasan/Kumano days are ridden on the scenic toll-free Route 480/168).
5. **Luggage**: panniers/top-box fitted or allowed (an optional support-truck add-on exists), and where to leave the flight-day bags.
6. Insurance/excess, breakdown cover, and what counts as the licence + IDP they require.
7. The **Nankai ferry** (Day 8, Wakayama→Tokushima) is **first-come, first-served for motorcycles** — no booking; arrive 30+ min early.

## Child-pillion safety (Africa Twin)
The 6-year-old rides on the Africa Twin with Rider 1. Before Day 3, set up and test all of this **while the bike is parked** (do it on Day 2):

- **Footpeg reach.** A 6-year-old's legs may not reach the stock passenger pegs comfortably — fit **lowered/highway pegs or peg-lowering brackets** so feet sit flat and secure. Feet that dangle get tired and slip.
- **Backrest & grab points.** Add a **passenger backrest/top-box backrest** so the child can't slide rearward, plus a **proper grab rail or grab strap** at the waist — don't rely on the child holding the rider.
- **Tandem riding belt.** Use a **child–adult tandem safety belt** (harness linking the child to the rider) so a tired child can't lean away or doze off the bike.
- **Gear.** Full properly-fitted child helmet (correct shell size, not an adult hand-me-down), armoured jacket, gloves, boots; ear protection helps on longer legs.
- **Pace & limits.** Stops every **60–90 minutes**; watch for the child going quiet or fidgety (early fatigue). **A 5h+ day in the saddle is too long for a 6-year-old** — this is a core reason the loop's two biggest days are held to ~4h33m (Day 12) and ~4h32m (Day 3, the combined Kōyasan→Yunomine opener), each followed by a rest day, with rest days spaced through the route. Never ride the child after dark or when they're overtired.

## The Ramen Trail: a foodie thread for Galiya
The loop happens to thread **four of Japan's famous regional ramen cities**, so we make it an explicit, celebrated thread for **Galiya** (the foodie, who loves ramen/noodles above all — see [00-family.md](00-family.md)). Each is a genuinely *different* local bowl, eaten where it's made, and each falls on a day already on the route — no detours required. **Notes & honesty:** the famous **Shūkaen (朱華園, est. 1947)** — for decades *the* Onomichi-ramen marquee — **closed permanently in 2019**; its exact flavour is carried on by the founder's wife and daughter at the successor shop **Shu (朱)**, so we point there and to the classic **Tsutafuji** instead. For **Aslan (6)**, every stop has a kid-simple out — a plain bowl (no raw egg / pork belly), a side of rice, or gyoza. The **Day-8 lunch + dinner are a single "two-ramen" day**, so pace appetites accordingly.

**Bookend & off-route awareness:** the Tokyo bookend (Days 0–1) is a chance to add classic **Tokyo shoyu ramen** as an optional fifth bowl. Japan's other headline styles — **Sapporo miso, Hakata/Fukuoka tonkotsu, Kitakata, Kishimen** — are **off this Osaka loop** (Hokkaidō and Kyūshū aren't on the route), so they're flagged for awareness, not promised.
