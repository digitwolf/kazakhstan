# The Family — who this trip is for

The whole tour is designed around these three people, and around one fact above all:
**Galiya just got her motorcycle endorsement and this is her first real tour as a solo
rider.** Read this before any planning decision — the **riders' skill/pace** sets the route
and daily limits, and **what each person likes** should steer destinations, stops, food and
the website's framing. (Roles map to `00-overview.md`: Rider 1 = Ruslan, Rider 2 = Galiya,
Passenger = Aslan.)

## Galiya — Rider 2, the newly-endorsed solo rider this trip is built around
- Born in **Kazakhstan**; in **Seattle since 2008**. Years of experience **as Ruslan's pillion**, but she **just earned her motorcycle endorsement** — this is her **first multi-day tour on her own bike**, and the entire trip exists to build her touring skills and a love of touring.
- Rides her own **Kawasaki W230** — a friendly, classic-styled **233cc single** that is happiest at **55–65 mph** and carries a small **~3.4-gallon tank**. **She rides up front and her confidence sets the group pace.**
- **Hard rule that shapes the whole route:** as a new rider on a small-displacement bike, **avoid all freeways, Interstates and high-speed highways.** Keep to paved coastal and mountain two-lanes at relaxed speeds, no white-knuckle riding, no long slogs, no riding after dark, and **plan fuel around the W230's short range** (top off early and often).
- **Big foodie** — the coast's seafood is hers: **Dungeness crab, fresh oysters, fish & chips, clam chowder**, and the Tillamook cheese-and-ice-cream stop. Also loves the **Oregon coast itself**, **lighthouses**, coastal art and galleries, and quiet beautiful places to simply stop.
- **Planning implication:** she is the rider the *safety/pace* rules protect, and the traveller whose *interests* (coast seafood, lighthouses, scenery, art) should be weighted most when choosing stops and where to eat. Build days she can ride comfortably and finish proud of.

## Ruslan — 37, Rider 1 (carries Aslan; the seasoned one)
- Born in **Ukraine**; in the **Seattle** area ~10 years. **Strong, seasoned rider** on a **BMW R1300GS** — his longest trip was a **2-week adventure ride in Morocco with Bret Tkacs**. He can ride long and technical — he is **not** the limiting rider.
- On this tour he carries **Aslan (6) as pillion** on the GS, with the snacks, rain layers, tools and first-aid. His job is to ride steady at the back, keep Galiya's pace, and make the long coast-to-Cascades transfer easy for everyone.
- Likes **riding quality** — the road as the experience: the Hood Canal shoreline, the Three Capes loop, Spirit Lake Highway, Stevens Canyon Road and the Chinook Pass switchbacks home. Also drawn to the **volcano story, the Tillamook Air Museum and the Columbia River maritime history**.
- **Planning implication:** lean on him for the scenic/riding priorities and the dad-and-son experiences, but **always cap the day to Galiya and Aslan**, not to what he could do solo.

## Aslan — 6, Passenger (rides with dad)
- Born in the **US**; speaks **English and Russian**. Rides pillion on the GS in full fitting gear.
- Loves **toys and souvenirs** (a souvenir from every stop), **animals and wildlife**, **hands-on museums**, the **volcano**, and the **cheese factory**. Specific magnets on this route: sea lions, the aquarium touch tanks, the Astoria Column gliders, the Tillamook Creamery and Air Museum, the Mount St. Helens visitor center, and roadside elk.
- Can manage **up to ~6 hours of riding on a good day, but not long days back-to-back** — keep individual days within the comfort cap, **stop every 60–90 minutes**, and keep every day breakable if he tires. Full child-pillion safety setup per `00-overview.md`.
- **Planning implication:** weave in **a kid hook on every day and a souvenir/toy stop wherever possible**; pair the one rest day (the 4th of July in Yachats) with tidepools, sea lions and fireworks so it lands for him too.

## What we like — a checklist for planning decisions
Use this as a scoring lens: a strong day/stop hits several of these for *more than one* person.

### Safety & pace (Galiya leads — the new rider)
- **No freeways, no Interstates, no high-speed roads.** Coastal and mountain two-lanes only, ridden at a relaxed, confidence-building pace.
- **Fuel discipline** for the W230's small tank and short range — top off early, never gamble on the next station on a remote stretch (Hood Canal, the Coast Range, US-12 to Packwood).
- Days inside the **4–6h cap**, **no two long days back-to-back**, breaks every 60–90 min, no riding after dark or in pushed-hard wet twisties.

### Food & drink (Galiya leads — she's the foodie)
- **Coast seafood above all** — Dungeness crab, oysters, fish & chips, clam chowder, fresh-off-the-boat catch, eaten at the dock where possible. The **[Coast Food Trail](00-overview.md#the-coast-food-trail)** (Westport → Astoria → Tillamook → Yachats) is the explicit thread.
- **Tillamook cheese & ice cream** as the marquee midday treat; coastal brewpubs and bakeries for relaxed family dinners and breakfasts.
- Good food is a first-order reason to choose a town to overnight in — not an afterthought.

### Coast, art & scenery (Galiya leads)
- **Lighthouses** (Westport Light, Heceta Head, Cape Meares), **headlands and tidepools**, **dramatic coast** (Thor's Well, Cape Perpetua), and quiet, beautiful places to stop.
- Coastal towns with galleries and historic character (Astoria's Victorian riverport, the village of Yachats).

### Aslan (6) — keep him engaged
- **Toy & souvenir stops** at every opportunity (he loves buying toys) — call out gift shops, the Creamery store, aquarium and museum shops.
- **Animals & wildlife:** sea lions (Westport, Astoria, Sea Lion Caves), tidepools, the petting farm at Blue Heron, roadside elk near St. Helens and Packwood, aquarium touch tanks.
- **Hands-on museums & the big set-pieces:** the Astoria Column gliders, Columbia River Maritime Museum, Tillamook Air Museum, the Mount St. Helens visitor center, and the volcano story.
- **A kid hook on every day, and the rest day too:** the 4th of July in Yachats pairs the parade and fireworks with tidepools and sea lions.

### Riding quality (Ruslan can do more; Galiya + Aslan set the limits)
- **Scenic, flowing, paved roads** are the point — Hood Canal (Hwy 106), the Three Capes Loop, Alsea River road, Spirit Lake Highway (SR-504), Stevens Canyon Road and **Chinook Pass (SR-410)** as the graduation ride home.
- **But cap to the others:** Galiya's new-rider confidence and Aslan's "up to ~6h but not back-to-back" tolerance govern every day's length.

### Practical / comfort
- Lodging with **secure motorcycle parking** and confirmed **passenger/child** rules (always "confirm before booking").
- **Book the coast far ahead** — Yachats and the whole central coast sell out for the **4th of July**.
- Two nights in Yachats as the trip's rest base and emotional high point; everything else single-night.

### How agents should use this
- **tour-expert:** protect Galiya's new-rider pace and fuel range above all; weight coast seafood, lighthouses/scenery and Aslan's wildlife/toy/hands-on stops when choosing or adding stops; keep days inside the cap with no two long days in a row. When two options are close, pick the one that serves the most family interests at once.
- **website-builder:** surface these interests in framing/emphasis when rendering (highlight foodie picks, lighthouses/scenery, kid wildlife & toy stops, and the "new rider, no freeways" character) — but never invent travel facts; if the content isn't in `tour/`, ask the tour-expert to add it first.
