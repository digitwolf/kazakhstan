# Tillamook

> The cheese-and-ice-cream heart of the Oregon Coast — a green dairy valley with a giant blimp hangar full of aircraft and a scenic loop of dramatic capes.

- **Region:** Oregon Coast · Tillamook County
- **Itinerary:** Day 3 · midday stop (Fri Jul 3, en route to Yachats)
- **Stop type:** stop
- **Ride to here:** 45 mi (Cannon Beach down the coast, Day 3)

## About
Tillamook is the lush, cow-dotted dairy valley that gives Oregon its most famous **cheese and ice cream**, and on this trip it is the marquee midday stop — a tasty, kid-joyful break that anchors the long Day-3 ride down the coast to Yachats. Riding in from Astoria past **Cannon Beach**, Garibaldi, and Rockaway Beach, you trade headlands and surf for a wide green floodplain where dairy herds graze under the Coast Range and the smell of the sea gives way to the smell of fresh-churned cheese.

For this family it is almost too good to be true. **Galiya** the foodie gets a working cheese factory with free tastings and legendary scoops; **Ruslan** and **Aslan** get the **Tillamook Air Museum**, an enormous WWII wooden blimp hangar packed with vintage aircraft; and **Aslan** also gets a petting farm, ice cream, and a shop full of toys and souvenirs. Just west of town, the **Three Capes Scenic Loop** strings together a lighthouse, a famous twisted tree, and a beach with dory boats and a giant dune — easy, low-speed detours that suit a brand-new rider.

## Things to see & do
- **Tillamook Creamery** — The big one: a free self-guided **cheese-factory tour** on a glassed-in walkway over the production floor, **cheese tasting**, and the famous **Tillamook ice cream** scoop counter, plus a café and gift shop (**the headline foodie + kid win of the day**).
- **Tillamook Air Museum** — Housed in **Hangar B**, a gigantic WWII wooden blimp hangar — one of the **largest wooden structures in the world** — filled with vintage and warbird aircraft; the sheer scale of the building alone is jaw-dropping (**a guaranteed Ruslan + Aslan win**).
- **Blue Heron French Cheese Co.** — A barn-red farm shop with brie and other cheeses, a tasting room and wine, jams and gifts, plus a small **petting farm** with goats and friendly animals out back (**easy kid stop**).
- **Cape Meares (lighthouse + the Octopus Tree)** — On the Three Capes Loop: a squat, picturesque **lighthouse** on the cliffs and the **Octopus Tree**, a giant candelabra-shaped Sitka spruce, with seabird views over Three Arch Rocks (**short walks, big payoff**).
- **Cape Kiwanda / Pacific City** — A sandstone headland with colorful **dory boats** launched straight off the beach, a towering **sand dune** kids love to climb, and **Pelican Brewing** right on the sand looking at Pacific City's own offshore Haystack Rock (**foodie + kid combo**).
- **Cape Lookout** — The middle cape: a forested headland trail and a long sandy spit, an optional leg-stretch if time allows on the loop.
- **Cannon Beach · Haystack Rock** — The day's earlier coastal stop on the way in from Astoria: the iconic 235-ft sea stack with tide pools and puffins, framed by a long flat beach (**classic photo + tide-pool stop**).
- **Garibaldi & Rockaway Beach** — Small fishing-port and beach towns you pass riding into Tillamook Bay — good for a quick coffee, a leg stretch, or beach-and-train photos.

## What to eat
- **Tillamook ice cream** — Scoops at the Creamery counter (waffle cones, sundaes, the works) — the single most-requested treat of the day and an automatic crowd-pleaser for Aslan.
- **Tillamook cheese & curds** — Sample squeaky fresh cheese curds and the famous cheddar at the Creamery tasting bar; grab a snack-pack to carry on the bike for Yachats.
- **Pelican Brewing — Pacific City** — Beachfront brewpub at Cape Kiwanda with chowder, fish tacos, burgers, and house beers, all with a Haystack Rock view (**foodie + family favorite**).
- **Tillamook seafood / chowder** — A bowl of Oregon-coast clam chowder or fish-and-chips in town or at the bay (the **Garibaldi** docks are known for fresh-off-the-boat seafood).
- **The Creamery café** — Grilled cheese, mac-and-cheese, and simple kid plates right at the Creamery — an easy, no-fuss lunch before pushing south.
- **Blue Heron tastings** — Brie, smoked cheeses, jams and a glass of local wine for the grown-ups while Aslan visits the petting animals.

## Where to stay
No overnight here — Tillamook is purely a midday stop on the Day-3 ride from Astoria down to the Yachats base, timed for cheese, ice cream, the air museum, and a cape or two before the afternoon push. You could easily overnight in Tillamook or nearby Pacific City if you wanted to split this long coastal day into two and slow the pace for the new rider, but the plan is to soak up the creamery and capes, then ride on and sleep in Yachats.
