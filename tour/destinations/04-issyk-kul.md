# Issyk-Kul South Shore

> The world's second-largest alpine lake — a warm, never-freezing inland sea reached the hard way, over three passes and past the glaciers of the Barskoon gorge, with a yurt camp waiting on the beach.

- **Region:** Issyk-Kul south shore, Kyrgyzstan
- **Itinerary:** Days 7–8 · 2 nights (yurt camp, Tosor/Tamga area) — Day 8 is the tour's rest day
- **Stop type:** stay
- **Ride to here:** 190 km (Karakol over Sarimonok, Barskoon & Suek passes, Day 7)

## About
**Issyk-Kul** ("warm lake") is the world's second-largest alpine lake after Titicaca — about 180 km long, ringed by the snow walls of the Terskey and Kungey Alatau, sitting at 1,607 m, slightly saline and famously **never freezing**, even in the Central Asian winter. The south shore is the quiet side: no big Soviet-era resorts, just pebble beaches, red-rock canyons and small villages like **Tosor** and **Tamga**, where the tour's yurt camp stands close to the water.

Getting here is the day the whole tour is named for. From Karakol the Day-7 route climbs into the **Barskoon gorge** and onto the high mining road toward Kumtor, crossing **Sarimonok Pass (3,126 m)**, **Barskoon Pass (3,754 m)** and finally **Suek Pass (4,021 m)** — the highest point of the trip, with **glaciers hanging beside the road** and a real chance of snow flurries in any month. Then comes the payoff: a 2,400-metre descent from the ice down to a warm lake you can swim in before dinner. Desert-to-glacier-to-beach in a single riding day.

And then — nothing. **Day 8 is the tour's one full rest day**, spent right here at the yurt camp: no alarm, no kilometres, just the lake. It's placed deliberately, because the block that follows — **Days 9–12**, Kochkor, the 33 Parrots Pass to Song-Kol, the big Moldo-Ashuu/Kara-Keche off-road day to Suusamyr and Too-Ashuu into Bishkek — is the hardest sustained riding of the loop. Rest, swim, let the support crew and mechanic do their work, and start it fresh.

## Things to see & do
- **The three-pass Barskoon road** — The ride *is* the sight: gravel switchbacks built for the Kumtor gold mine, climbing from spruce forest past **Sarimonok (3,126 m)** and **Barskoon (3,754 m)** to **Suek (4,021 m)**, with marmots on the verges and glacier tongues above the road (**the tour's highest point — ride it behind the leader, weather can turn fast**).
- **Barskoon waterfalls** — A short walk from the gorge road reaches the famous falls; Yuri **Gagarin** holidayed here after his flight, and a monument to him stands among the spruce.
- **Swim in Issyk-Kul** — The camp is steps from a pebble beach; the mildly salty water is genuinely swimmable in July and the classic way to wash off a 4,000-metre day (**bring sandals — the pebbles are sharp**).
- **Yurt-camp evening** — Sunset over the lake with the Kungey Alatau glowing pink across the water, dinner in the communal yurt, and a sky full of stars once the generator goes off.
- **Skazka (Fairy Tale) Canyon** — Eroded red-and-orange rock formations a short hop along the south-shore road toward Tosor — a quick, photogenic leg-stretch if the group passes it on the Day-9 ride to Kochkor.
- **Rest day (Day 8): swim, nap, repeat** — The one day of the loop with zero kilometres. Sleep in, swim off the camp's beach, read on the pebbles, walk the shore — deliberate recovery before the hard Days 9–12 block over the 3,000-m passes to Song-Kol, Suusamyr and Bishkek.
- **Rest day: banya** — The south-shore camps and nearby Tamga guest houses can fire up a **banya** (steam bath) — the definitive fix for a week of riding shoulders; book it with the hosts in the morning (small cash cost).
- **Rest day: horseback to a jailoo (optional)** — Camps arrange half-day horse treks up to a nearby **jailoo** summer pasture with a local horseman — a gentle preview of Song-Kol's herding world (**agree the price first, typically $10–15/hour; skip it if you'd rather save your legs**).
- **Rest day: laundry & bike checks** — Hand a bag to the support crew for washing, and give the mechanic time with your DR650 — chain, tyres, air filter after the dusty mining road — so the bike starts the Song-Kol block as fresh as you do.

## What to eat
- **Fresh lake fish** — The Issyk-Kul classic: whole fried or smoked fish sold in the shore villages and served at the camps — ask for the local **chebak** or whitefish, straight from the lake.
- **Yurt-camp dinner** — Home-style Kyrgyz table: **lagman** or **shorpo** soup, fresh **lepyoshka** bread, mountains of apricot jam and tea from the samovar (dinners are at your own expense — carry som in cash, there are no card machines on the south shore).
- **Honey & apricots** — Tamga and Barskoon are orchard villages; roadside stands sell jars of honey and dried apricots that travel well in a pannier.
