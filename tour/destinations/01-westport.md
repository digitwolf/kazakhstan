# Westport

> A salty fishing-and-surf town on Grays Harbor's south jetty — the family's first night on tour, and their first taste of the Pacific.

- **Region:** Washington Coast · Grays Harbor County
- **Itinerary:** Day 1 · 1 night (Wed Jul 1)
- **Stop type:** stop
- **Ride to here:** 157 mi (Woodinville via the Edmonds–Kingston ferry & Hood Canal, Day 1)

## About
Day 1 is built for confidence, not mileage records. From Woodinville the family rolls down to **Edmonds** and rides the bikes straight onto the **Edmonds–Kingston ferry** — a relaxed half-hour crossing of Puget Sound that doubles as Galiya's first ferry on two wheels. From Kingston the route stays on quiet backroads and skirts the calm, fjord-like shore of **Hood Canal on Highway 106**, a low-traffic, easy-cornering road with water on one side and forest on the other. After Shelton the country opens up and the air turns salty as the route reaches the coast and the working harbor town of **Westport**.

Westport sits on a sandy spit guarding the south side of **Grays Harbor**, and it wears two hats: it's one of Washington's busiest **sport-fishing and charter ports**, and it's the most reliable **surf town** on the state's coast. For a brand-new rider it's an ideal first night — flat approaches, an easy grid of streets, no pressure — and for the family it delivers the big payoff of standing at the Pacific on the very first evening. Expect **boats, sea lions, fresh seafood, a tall lighthouse, and a huge open beach** all within a few blocks of each other.

## Things to see & do
- **Westport Light / Grays Harbor Lighthouse** — at 107 ft, the **tallest lighthouse in Washington** (and third-tallest on the West Coast); built in 1898, it's reached by a short trail from Westport Light State Park and is the town's landmark photo stop
- **Westhaven State Park & the surf** — Washington's go-to surf beach on the south jetty; watch surfers and **kitesurfers** ride the break, walk the dunes, and let everyone touch the Pacific on arrival evening **[KID]**
- **Westport Maritime Museum** — housed in a 1939 Nantucket-style former Coast Guard station; lighthouse lenses, whale and sea-life skeletons, and fishing-history exhibits make an easy, low-key stop **[KID]**
- **Westport Aquarium** — a small, hands-on local aquarium right by the docks with touch tanks and resident sea creatures; a perfect quick hook for a tired 6-year-old **[KID]**
- **The marina, docks & fishing fleet** — wander the working waterfront, watch charter boats unload salmon, tuna, and crab, and look for the **sea lions** that haul out near the basin **[KID]**
- **Westport Viewing Tower (Observation Tower)** — climb the tall waterfront tower for a sweeping look over the harbor, the jetty, and the boats coming and going
- **The South Jetty & long beach** — walk out toward the jetty and along the wide, hard-packed sand; great for driftwood-hunting, beach-combing, and brown-pelican spotting **[KID]**
- **Float Park & Fishermen's Memorial** — a quiet harborside spot honoring the town's fishing heritage, good for stretching legs after the ride
- **Fresh-off-the-boat seafood** — Westport calls itself a salmon capital, and the dockside markets and chowder counters are a destination in themselves for Galiya the foodie **[FOOD]**

## What to eat
- **Bennett's Fish Shack** — a Westport institution for beer-battered **fish & chips**, clam chowder, and a kid-friendly menu, steps from the harbor **[FOOD]**
- **Half Moon Bay Bar & Grill** (at the Inn Resort) — waterfront views with fresh local seafood, burgers, and easy options for Aslan **[KID]**
- **Merino's Seafood Market** — buy fresh and cooked **Dungeness crab, oysters, and smoked fish** right off the dock; great for a casual picnic dinner by the water **[FOOD]**
- **Westport Winery Garden Resort & Sea Glass Grill** — just outside town; smoked-salmon chowder, garden art, and dessert wines for a foodie detour **[FOOD]**
- **Aloha Alabama BBQ & Bakery** — a beloved local spot mixing barbecue and from-scratch baking; reliable comfort food and treats for a hungry kid **[KID]**
- **Blue Buoy Restaurant** — a long-running diner-style breakfast spot for hearty eggs, pancakes, and coffee before Day 2's ride **[FOOD]**
