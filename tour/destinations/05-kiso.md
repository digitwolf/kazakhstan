# Kiso Valley — Magome & Tsumago  木曽路 馬籠・妻籠

> Car-free Edo-era post towns on the old Nakasendo highway.

- **Region:** Kiso Valley · Nagano / Gifu
- **Itinerary:** Day 7 (overnight; Day 8 departs after a morning walk)
- **Stop type:** stop
- **Ride to here (direct):** 134 mi

## About
The Kiso Valley carries the old **Nakasendo**, the Edo-period highway that linked Kyoto and Edo through the mountains. **Magome** and **Tsumago** are its best-preserved post towns (*juku*): stone-paved streets closed to traffic by day, wooden inns, water wheels, and not an overhead wire in sight.

It replaces the old Nagoya city night with something far more in keeping with this trip — quiet, rural, deeply atmospheric, and reached by gentle paved valley roads rather than expressway sprawl. A calm post-town evening is also the perfect reset after the longer Day 8 transfer to come.

## Things to see & do
- Walk **Tsumago-juku**'s preserved main street (through-traffic banned 9–17)
- **Magome-juku**'s steep stone slope with views to Mt. Ena
- A sample stretch of the gentle **Magome–Tsumago Nakasendo trail** (full walk ≈ 8 km / 3h)
- **Narai-juku**, the longest of the post towns — a leg-stretch on the Day 7 ride in
- Optional: **Nezame-no-toko** gorge near Kiso-Fukushima

## What to eat
- **Gohei-mochi** — Flattened rice cakes brushed with sweet walnut-miso and grilled; kids love them.
- **Kiso soba** — Cold Shinshu buckwheat noodles, a mountain staple.
- **Sansai & river fish** — Wild mountain vegetables and salt-grilled ayu or iwana.
- **Oyaki** — Griddle-baked buns stuffed with vegetables or sweet bean.
