# Saty & the Kolsai Lakes

> The loop's last night: a one-street village in a Tian Shan valley, an alpine lake in the spruce forest, and — on the final morning — the ghostly sunken forest of Lake Kaindy.

- **Region:** Saty / Kolsai Lakes, Kazakhstan
- **Itinerary:** Day 14 (1 night; Day-15 morning Kolsai/Kaindy, then the ~300 km run home to Almaty)
- **Stop type:** end
- **Ride to here:** ~325 km (Cholpon-Ata → around Issyk-Kul's eastern tip → Karkara border back into Kazakhstan → Kegen → Saty; Day 14)

## About
Day 14 closes the international part of the loop. From Cholpon-Ata the route runs east along Issyk-Kul's north shore, rounds the lake's eastern tip, and climbs back into the high **Karkara valley** — the same green border pastures the tour crossed nine days earlier, now taken in the opposite direction. Stamped back into Kazakhstan at Karkara, the road drops through Kegen and turns up a mountain valley to **Saty**, a one-street Kazakh village of log houses, haystacks, and satellite dishes that has become the base camp for **Kolsai Lakes National Park** — about 325 km for the day.

Saty earns its place as the final overnight. Fifteen minutes up the valley lies **Kolsai Lake 1** (Lower Kolsai, ~1,800 m): a kilometer-long ribbon of blue-green water between steep spruce slopes, deep (up to ~80 m), cold, and absurdly photogenic — the "pearls of the Tian Shan" start here. And on the **Day-15 morning**, before the ~300 km run home to Almaty, comes the strangest sight of the whole trip: **Lake Kaindy**, reached by a ~12 km rough track off the Saty road, where the bleached trunks of a drowned spruce forest still stand upright in turquoise water — a lake created overnight when the **1911 Kebin earthquake** dammed the valley with a landslide. Park entry is a small per-person fee paid in cash at the checkpoint — the operator handles it. The night itself is pure village hospitality: a family guest house, bikes in the yard, dinner from the kitchen garden, and the Milky Way overhead.

## Things to see & do
- **Kolsai Lake 1 (Lower Kolsai, ~1,800 m)** — Ride the short valley road from Saty to the first of the three Kolsai lakes on the Day-14 evening or at first light: a 1-km-long, up-to-80-m-deep alpine lake pinned between forested ridges. Boardwalk paths, rowboats for rent, and trout in the water; the higher lakes (Kolsai 2 at ~2,250 m and Kolsai 3 near the Kyrgyz ridge) are hiking/horse territory beyond the road.
- **Lake Kaindy & the sunken forest (Day-15 morning)** — The finale: a **~12 km rough, rocky piste** off the Saty road — fords, ruts, proper DR650 terrain — climbs to a small lake at ~2,000 m where dozens of **Schrenk's spruce trunks stand dead-straight out of turquoise water**. The 1911 Kebin earthquake dropped a limestone landslide across the gorge; the river filled it, and the cold water has preserved the drowned forest for over a century. Walk the rim trail down to the shore before the day-trip crowds arrive, then point the bikes at Almaty.
- **The Karkara border, reversed** — Crossing back into Kazakhstan through the same high-pasture valley as Day 5 bookends the Kyrgyz chapters — yurts, herds, and snow peaks all still exactly where you left them.
- **Village-evening Saty** — Cows coming home at dusk, kids on horseback, hay wagons on the one street. Walk it end to end; it takes fifteen minutes and it's the last quiet of the trip.
- **Horseback option to the lakes** — Guest-house families arrange horses to Kolsai 1 or toward Kaindy for anyone who wants one Central-Asian ride done the original way before flying home.

## What to eat
- **Home-cooked guest-house dinner** — The village kitchens do the honest classics: soup or beshbarmak, plov, salads, potatoes from the garden — hot, generous, and served at one long table.
- **Fresh bread, cream & mountain honey** — Breakfast is baursaki or tandyr bread with kaymak (thick cream) and local honey from hives up the valley — Saty's quiet specialty.
- **Kymyz** — Fermented mare's milk, offered wherever horses graze; one last bowl before the city. Sour, fizzy, and the most Kazakh thing on the table.
- **Pack for the piste** — There are no cafés at Kaindy or the lakes; the guest house fills thermoses and wraps bread for the Day-15 morning run.
