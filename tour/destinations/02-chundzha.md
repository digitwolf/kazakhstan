# Chundzha Hot Springs

> Steaming thermal pools at the edge of the Uighur district — reached by riding straight through Altyn-Emel's dunes and badlands, with the best lagman in Kazakhstan on the table.

- **Region:** Chundzha (Shonzhy), Uygur District, Almaty Region, Kazakhstan
- **Itinerary:** Day 4 (1 night, hot springs)
- **Stop type:** stop
- **Ride to here:** 250 km (Basshi → Chundzha through Altyn-Emel: Singing Dune and Aktau on the morning pistes, then south across the Ili basin)

## About
Day 4 is the tour's desert day proper: a **250 km leg that leaves Basshi through Altyn-Emel National Park itself** — the Singing Dune and the Aktau chalk mountains on the morning's dirt-and-sand pistes — before dropping south across the **Ili basin** on open, near-empty tarmac to **Chundzha** (officially Shonzhy), the capital of Kazakhstan's **Uygur District** — with the whole evening left for soaking. This is a different Central Asia again: a green irrigated pocket in the semi-desert where the population is largely Uighur, the bazaars smell of cumin and fresh flatbread, and the roadside signs advertise one thing above all — hot springs.

Chundzha sits on a field of some **140 thermal springs**, with artesian wells pumping mineral-rich water that comes up hot year-round; resort pools run at a steady 36–50 °C. A whole strip of spa resorts has grown up around them, from simple pool-and-cabin operations to full complexes with indoor and outdoor pools, saunas, and plunge tubs. For a group of riders arriving with Altyn-Emel's dust still in their gear, it is the perfectly engineered overnight: park the DR650s, sink into the hot water under the open sky, then eat Uighur lagman until you can't. Chundzha is also the gateway to **Charyn Canyon** — tomorrow's first stop, less than an hour down the road.

## Things to see & do
- **Soak in the thermal pools** — The whole point. Outdoor pools at 36–50 °C; the mineral water is locally credited with easing joints and skin — exactly what a morning of dune and badland pistes orders, with the whole evening to enjoy it.
- **Pool-hop the resort zone** — Most complexes have several pools at different temperatures plus saunas and cold plunges; do the circuit slowly, with tea between rounds.
- **Chundzha town & bazaar** — A short ride into Shonzhy proper for the Uighur bazaar: flatbread ovens, spice stalls, melons in season — a small, real slice of the Uighur district.
- **Sunset over the steppe** — The resort zone sits between the Ketmen range and the Ili basin; the light show over the semi-desert at dusk is worth getting out of the pool for.
- **Stage Charyn Canyon** — Sleep here and you hit Charyn Canyon early on Day 5, before the day-trip crowds from Almaty arrive — the operator's routing does exactly this.
- **Bike care hour** — Guarded resort parking makes this the natural evening for chain lube, air-filter checks, and knocking the desert out of everything before the border day.

## What to eat
- **Lagman — the real thing** — Chundzha is in the Uighur heartland, and hand-pulled lagman here is the benchmark: chewy noodles slapped out to order under a fiery meat, pepper, and celery sauce. Order it at the resort or a roadside Uighur café and understand what the Almaty version was imitating.
- **Manty and samsa** — Steamed lamb dumplings and tandyr-baked meat pies, everywhere and excellent.
- **Shashlik by the pool** — The resorts grill skewers into the evening; lamb over coals plus hot-spring steam is the signature Chundzha combination.
- **Uighur tea and flatbread** — Fresh non (flatbread) from the tandyr with green tea — breakfast before the canyon.
