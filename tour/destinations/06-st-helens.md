# Mount St. Helens

> The volcano that blew its top in 1980 — eruption stories, recovering forests and big Cascade views, the family's overnight on the long turn for home.

- **Region:** Cascades · Cowlitz County, WA
- **Itinerary:** Day 5 · 1 night (Sun Jul 5)
- **Stop type:** stop
- **Ride to here:** 217 mi (Yachats via Corvallis & the Columbia crossing, Day 5)

## About

Day 5 is the trip's pivot. After four days hugging the Oregon coast you point the bikes **inland and north toward home**, climbing out of Yachats over the Coast Range to Alsea and Corvallis, then crossing the **Columbia River** back into Washington and rolling up into the Cascade foothills. At roughly **217 miles it's the longest day of the tour** — a true transfer day — so the plan is steady backroad pace, frequent breaks, and an easy arrival rather than heroics. **Galiya's comfort on her W230 sets the rhythm**; there's no need to rush a single mile of it.

The reward at the end is a real, recently-active **volcano**. You overnight at the mountain's southwest gateway — the small towns of **Castle Rock, Toutle and Silver Lake** along WA-504 — which is where the lodging, diners and the always-open visitor center all are. **Aslan gets the big payoff here**: the story of the morning of **May 18, 1980**, when Mount St. Helens lost 1,300 feet of its summit in a sideways blast, flattened forests for miles, and sent ash around the planet. Day 6 then runs the Spirit Lake Highway viewpoints before continuing on to Packwood.

## Things to see & do

- **Spirit Lake Memorial Highway (WA-504) drive** — The scenic spine of the monument: ~50 miles of smooth, sweeping two-lane climbing from Silver Lake toward the blast zone, with pull-offs framing the crater. A relaxed, low-stress ride for a new rider, all viewpoint-to-viewpoint. (Best ridden on Day 6.)
- **Mount St. Helens Visitor Center at Silver Lake** — The state-park center right on WA-504, **always open** and the must-do here: walk-in lava-tube model, a step-in scale model of the volcano, seismographs, and the 1980 eruption story told for all ages. **Aslan's favorite kind of place** — hands-on, kid-pitched, and a short boardwalk over Silver Lake's wetland with the mountain on the horizon.
- **The 1980 eruption story** — On May 18, 1980 a magnitude-5.1 quake triggered the largest landslide in recorded history and a lateral blast that leveled 230 square miles of forest. Great campfire-grade history for a six-year-old: the bulging north flank, the sideways explosion, the ashfall that darkened nearby towns.
- **Hoffstadt Bluffs (Mount St. Helens Visitor Center at Hoffstadt Bluffs)** — A privately-run overlook up WA-504 at about milepost 27 with sweeping valley views, eruption photo displays, a gift shop and helicopter flightseeing. A good leg-stretch and photo stop along the highway.
- **Coldwater Lake** — A lake **created by the eruption** when debris dammed Coldwater Creek; calm water, an accessible "Birth of a Lake" boardwalk trail, and reflected mountain views. A flat, easy stop the whole family can do.
- **Blast zone & the recovering forest** — From the upper viewpoints you can read the landscape: silvered downed logs, pumice plains, and decades of returning wildflowers, alder and elk — a living lesson in how a flattened forest comes back.
- **Elk & wildlife viewing** — The Toutle River valley and meadows along WA-504 are prime **Roosevelt elk** habitat, especially early and late in the day; deer and raptors too. Eyes up for a herd from a pull-off.
- **Johnston Ridge Observatory — CLOSED (do not drive there)** — Heads up: the famous Johnston Ridge Observatory and the upper end of WA-504 have been **closed since a May 2023 landslide and bridge washout** near milepost 49. Road rebuilding runs through fall 2026 and the observatory is not expected to reopen until ~2027. **Treat WA-504 as a roadside-viewpoint drive up to the closure point** — the Silver Lake, Hoffstadt Bluffs and Coldwater Lake stops are the highlights instead.

## What to eat

- **Patty's Place at 19 Mile House (Toutle)** — Longtime family-run roadhouse on the way up WA-504, known for elk and beef burgers, sweet-potato fries and homemade cobbler — a fitting end-of-a-long-day meal. (Recently rebranded; confirm current name/hours.)
- **Fire Mountain Grill at Hoffstadt Bluffs (Toutle)** — Hand-pressed burgers (the "Bigfoot"), salads and cobbler with a wall-of-windows view of the mountain. Seasonal — confirm it's open before counting on it.
- **Parker's Steakhouse & Brewery / local diners (Castle Rock)** — Castle Rock at the I-5 exit has straightforward American sit-down and diner options — easy, **kid-simple plates** (grilled cheese, burgers, fries) after 217 miles in the saddle.
- **Coffee in Castle Rock** — Grab espresso at a local drive-through stand or café on Huntington Ave before the morning push up the Spirit Lake Highway. **A foodie warm-up for Galiya** and caffeine for the riders.
