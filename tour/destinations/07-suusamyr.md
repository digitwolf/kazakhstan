# Suusamyr Valley

> Central Asia's largest summer pasture — a 150-km grass sea at 2,200 m, earned by the tour's biggest off-road day over Moldo-Ashuu and the coal road of Kara-Keche, and sweetened by the valley's famous honey.

- **Region:** Suusamyr Valley, Chüy Province, Kyrgyzstan · ~2,200 m
- **Itinerary:** Day 11 · 1 night (cabin camp)
- **Stop type:** stop
- **Ride to here:** 240 km (Song-Kol over Moldo-Ashuu 3,546 m & Kara-Keche 3,384 m, Day 11)

## About
The **Suusamyr Valley** is a vast high plateau — roughly 150 km long at about **2,200 m** — held between the Kyrgyz Alatau and the Suusamyr-Too ranges. It is celebrated as **the largest alpine pasture in Central Asia**: for centuries herders have driven millions of head of livestock up here for the summer, and in July the valley floor is an unbroken sea of grass, herds and scattered yurts under enormous skies. The other thing everyone knows Suusamyr for is **honey** — the valley's wildflower meadows feed apiaries whose white "Suusamyr honey" wins medals and turns up on tables across Kyrgyzstan.

Getting here is the most demanding riding day of the tour — 240 km, most of it off the asphalt (only Day 14's 325-km run back into Kazakhstan is longer, and that one stays on tarmac). From Song-Kol the route drops off the plateau on a **rocky off-road descent**, climbs the switchbacks of **Moldo-Ashuu Pass (3,546 m)**, then takes the gravel **coal road over Kara-Keche Pass (3,384 m)** — built to serve the open-cast coal mine below it — before following the boiling turquoise **Kokomeren river** gorge out into the open valley. The night is spent in a **cabin camp** ringed by panoramic mountains: real beds, hot food and a well-earned beer under the stars.

## Things to see & do
- **Moldo-Ashuu Pass (3,546 m)** — A ladder of gravel switchbacks with the whole Song-Kol plateau falling away behind — the classic photo stop of Day 11 (**loose surface, ride it patiently**).
- **Kara-Keche Pass (3,384 m) & the coal road** — A working mining road past the open-cast coal pits that heat half of Kyrgyzstan in winter; expect the occasional loaded truck and give it room.
- **The Kokomeren gorge** — The wild blue-green Kokomeren river crashes through a bare rock canyon alongside the road — one of Kyrgyzstan's most dramatic stretches of gravel riding.
- **The pasture itself** — Evening light across an ocean of grass, herds drifting home, and shepherd camps trailing smoke; walk out from the cabin camp and just stand in it.
- **Buy Suusamyr honey** — Roadside apiaries and village stands sell the valley's famous white honey by the jar — the best edible souvenir of the whole route (**a small jar survives a pannier fine**).

## What to eat
- **Suusamyr honey** — The valley's pride: pale, dense wildflower honey from apiaries grazing the same meadows as the horses — eat it on fresh bread with cream at breakfast.
- **Cabin-camp dinner** — Hearty highland cooking after the big day: **kuurdak** (fried meat and potatoes), **shorpo**, bread and tea; dinners are at your own expense, so keep som handy.
- **Kumis from the roadside** — Summer shacks along the valley road sell kumis and **maksym** (a tangy fermented grain drink) to travellers crossing between Bishkek and the south.
