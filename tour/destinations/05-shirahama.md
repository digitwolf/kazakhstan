# Shirahama  白浜

> A white-sand resort coast, panda safaris and seaside hot springs.

- **Region:** Nanki-Shirahama · Wakayama
- **Itinerary:** Days 7–8 · 2 nights
- **Stop type:** stay
- **Ride to here (direct):** 54 mi

## About
Rounding the southern tip of the Kii Peninsula, the coast softens into **Shirahama** ("white beach") — one of the **three oldest hot-spring resorts in Japan**, soaked in long enough to appear in the 8th-century *Nihon Shoki* chronicles, when emperors travelled here to bathe. Its crescent of dazzling white quartz sand (partly replenished with imported Australian sand) and turquoise water gives it a near-tropical feel. The ride in passes the photogenic line of sea pillars at **Hashigui-iwa** in Kushimoto and the sheer **Sandanbeki** cliffs.

It's the trip's first proper rest base: a beach, open-air onsen perched right over the Pacific surf, and **Adventure World** — a hugely popular zoo–safari–marine park that has successfully bred more giant pandas than anywhere outside China, with a drive-through safari and dolphin shows that make it the single biggest kid magnet of the whole loop. A relaxed, sunny pause before the ferry to Shikoku.

## Things to see & do
- **Adventure World** — giant pandas, a drive-through "Safari World" of lions and giraffes, plus dolphins and a marine park — easily a full, joyful day for a 6-year-old **[KID]**
- **Shirarahama beach** — brilliant white sand and gentle, shallow swimming, glowing at sunset **[KID]**
- **Sakino-yu onsen** — a rock-cut open-air bath right at the surf line, in use for 1,300+ years; waves crash a few metres below as you soak
- **Sandanbeki** — dramatic 50–60 m sea cliffs with an elevator down to a **sea cave** once used to hide pirate boats **[KID]**
- **Senjōjiki** — a vast tilted "1,000-tatami" rock platform sculpted by the waves, fun to clamber on **[KID]**
- **Engetsu Island** — the "round-moon" islet pierced by a natural sea arch; the classic Shirahama sunset shot
- **Hashigui-iwa** (en route at Kushimoto) — a 850 m row of ~40 pillar rocks marching out to sea toward Ōshima island
- **Tore-Tore Ichiba** — one of western Japan's biggest seafood market halls; watch a tuna-cutting show, then eat **[KID]**

## What to eat
- **Maguro & kaisen-don** — fresh local tuna and heaped seafood rice bowls at Tore-Tore market; catch the tuna-filleting demo.
- **Kumano-gyū / wagyū** — Wakayama-raised beef, grilled teppanyaki-style at the resort inns.
- **Ise-ebi (spiny lobster)** — the Kii coast's prized clawless lobster, in season from autumn — often the centrepiece of a ryokan kaiseki.
- **Umeboshi & ume products** — Nanki **ume** plums in everything from sweets to *umeshu*; this is Japan's plum heartland.
- **Mikan citrus** — Wakayama's famous mandarins and fresh juices.

## Where to stay
Shirahama is a genuine onsen town, so the trip leans into a **seaside onsen ryokan** with its own hot-spring baths over the Pacific — tatami rooms and futons that suit a child, plus easy parking (resort lots here are generous, unlike the old hamlets upstream).
