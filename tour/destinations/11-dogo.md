# Dogo Onsen / Matsuyama  道後温泉

> One of Japan's oldest hot springs beneath a hilltop castle.

- **Region:** Ehime · Matsuyama
- **Itinerary:** Days 15–16 · 2 nights
- **Stop type:** stay
- **Ride to here (direct):** 55 mi

## About
Matsuyama is Shikoku's largest city, crowned by one of Japan's twelve **original castle keeps**, with the ancient **Dōgo Onsen** at its edge — a hot spring mentioned in the 8th-century *Man'yōshū* and said to be among the **oldest in Japan, used for 3,000 years**. The landmark **Honkan** bathhouse (1894), a three-storey warren of wooden stairs, a private imperial bathing room and a *Botchan*-novel literary legend, is widely cited as an inspiration for the bathhouse in Studio Ghibli's *Spirited Away*. Bathe like a local: pay at the door, soak in the *Kami-no-yu*, then take green tea and a rice cracker in the tatami lounge upstairs.

Two nights here balance city comforts (laundry, dining, a proper room) with a classic onsen evening, and the gentle nostalgia of the **Botchan train** — a restored steam-style streetcar named for Natsume Sōseki's 1906 novel set in Matsuyama. A fitting reward before the Shimanami Kaidō.

## Things to see & do
- **Dōgo Onsen Honkan** — soak in the 1894 wooden bathhouse, a designated Important Cultural Property; pick the basic "Kami-no-yu" ticket or the tatami-lounge package with tea and dango **[KID]**
- **Matsuyama Castle** — ride a **ropeway or single-seat chairlift** up Katsuyama hill to a rare hilltop original keep with sweeping city-and-sea views; the climb and lift are a kid favourite **[KID]**
- **Botchan Ressha** — a coal-black, steam-style heritage tram that trundles between Dōgo and the city centre **[KID]**
- **Dōgo Haikara-dōri arcade** — a covered shopping street of onsen sweets, citrus, crafts and the **Botchan Karakuri clock** that performs on the hour **[KID]**
- **Ishite-ji** — an atmospheric, slightly mysterious pilgrimage temple (Temple #51) with a two-storey gate, a pagoda and a cave tunnel of statues
- **Asuka-no-Yu** — a newer annex bathhouse beside the Honkan, decorated in lavish Asuka-period style, with private family baths bookable (handy with a child)

## What to eat
- **Taimeshi (Matsuyama style)** — a whole **sea bream cooked together with the rice** in a clay pot, the fish lifted out and flaked back through; the cooked counterpart to Uwajima's raw version.
- **Botchan dango** — three-colour (green tea, egg, red bean) sweet dumplings on a skewer, named for the Sōseki novel; the classic post-bath treat. **[KID]**
- **Jakoten** — fried minced-fish cakes, an Ehime staple, good with local sake. **[KID]**
- **Mikan everything** — Ehime citrus as juice (some stations have a **"mikan juice tap"**), jelly and soft-serve. **[KID]**
- **Tai-meshi onigiri & sea-bream broth** — quick versions sold around the arcade.

## Where to stay
Dōgo is a true onsen town built around the Honkan, so the trip stays in a **traditional onsen ryokan within a short walk of the bathhouse** — tatami rooms, futons for the child, in-house hot-spring baths and kaiseki dinners. All three below have **their own parking**; if a ryokan lot is full, the city runs the **Dōgo Onsen municipal parking lots** a couple of minutes away.
