# Anchors & Options — the Flexible Plan

**The philosophy (Morocco-style "flexible anchor-point adventure"):** the participants for this departure aren't fixed yet, so the tour is designed the way the Morocco reference tour was — around **five fixed anchor points** that are 100% must-see and whose nights are **pre-booked and non-negotiable**, connected by **route options** the group chooses to fit its actual skill and appetite. The anchors are the trip; the connections are the dial. Whoever ends up in the group, the frame holds: same 16 days, same anchors, same rest day mid-trip — only the riding between them scales easier or harder. The existing [16-day itinerary](01-itinerary.md) remains the **reference itinerary**: the default line through the options, and the one all published/planned distances belong to.

## The base and the five anchors

**Base: Almaty** — start & finish. Day 1 arrival, bike handover and welcome dinner; **Day 2 training day** (skills session + easy foothill loop — and the option-selector, see [How to choose](#how-to-choose)); Day 15 return night and the celebration dinner; Day 16 departure.

| # | Anchor | Nights (pre-booked) | Why it's 100% must-see |
|---|--------|---------------------|------------------------|
| **A1** | **Altyn-Emel National Park** — guest house, Basshi | 1 | The Singing Dune and the white-and-red Aktau mountains: the Kazakh desert-steppe signature of the trip, and the terrain the DR650 was rented for. |
| **A2** | **Karakol** | **2** — the second day is the Jeti-Oguz/Altyn-Arashan day loop | The Tian Shan trailhead town: Jeti-Oguz red cliffs, the Altyn-Arashan gorge and hot springs, the Holy Trinity Church and Dungan Mosque, ashlan-fuu at the bazaar. A riding day with no packing. |
| **A3** | **Issyk-Kul south shore yurt camp** ★ **THE REST-DAY ANCHOR** | **2**, mid-trip | The one full no-riding day of the tour: lake swim, Skazka canyon, banya, laundry, bike checks. Placed to recover after the hardest connection and acclimatize between the high passes. |
| **A4** | **Song-Kol Lake yurt camp (3,016 m)** | 1 | The nomad heart of Kyrgyzstan — yurts, herders, kumis and boorsok at 3,000 m. No version of this trip skips Song-Kol. *(Bad-weather fallback: Kyzart village eco-hotel.)* |
| **A5** | **Saty / Kolsai Lakes** — guest house, Saty | 1 | Lake Kaindy's sunken forest and Kolsai Lake-1 — Kazakhstan's alpine finale before the run home. |

**Waypoint stops** (used by options, not anchors — booked only if the chosen option needs them): Chundzha hot springs, Kochkor, Suusamyr, Bishkek, Cholpon-Ata.

## The segments and their options

Distances marked *(estimate)* are planned estimates for non-reference options — confirm with the operator; all other figures are the reference itinerary's planned/operator legs.

### S1 — Almaty → Altyn-Emel (A1)

**Option (a) "Steppe Classic"** — *the reference itinerary's line (Day 3)*
- **Days:** 1 · **Distance:** 250 km (operator leg)
- **Surface/difficulty:** easy–moderate — tarmac with a mountain pass; a gentle opener the day after training — ADV Class 1 (Novice/Green), Class 2 (Basic/Yellow) only on the last graded gravel into Basshi.
- **Highlights:** the first taste of open steppe, a mountain-pass warm-up, the retro-museum tea stop.
- **Overnights en route:** none — straight to the Basshi guest house.

**Option (b) "Assy Plateau Traverse"**
- **Days:** 1 · **Distance:** ~280 km *(estimate)*
- **Surface/difficulty:** **hard**, weather-dependent — up the Turgen gorge on a rough, badly degraded track (4WD-grade on the Turgen side), across the high gravel plateau at ~2,500–2,750 m past the Assy-Turgen observatory, then dirt down past the Bartogay reservoir to rejoin the Kokpek road toward Basshi. Turns greasy and can close in rain; only for a group that sails through the Day-2 session — ADV Class 3 (Moderate/Orange), peaks Class 4 (Difficult/Red) on the Turgen-side track and anywhere after rain. *(Sources: [Caravanistan — Assy Plateau & Bartogay](https://caravanistan.com/kazakhstan/almaty-region/assy-plateau-bartogay-reservoir/), [Caravanistan forum, access via Turgen 2024](https://caravanistan.com/forum/viewtopic.php?t=16337).)*
- **Highlights:** the high summer-pasture plateau, the observatory at 2,750 m, Bartogay reservoir, real off-road on day one of the route proper.
- **Overnights en route:** none.

### S2 — Altyn-Emel → Karakol (A2)

**Option (a) "Hot Springs & the Canyon"** — *the reference itinerary's line (Days 4–5)*
- **Days:** 2 · **Distance:** 250 km + 280 km (operator legs)
- **Surface/difficulty:** moderate — desert pistes and sand patches through the park (an asphalt alternative exists inside the park if conditions are bad), then a mostly-tarmac border day — ADV Class 2 (Basic/Yellow) peaking Class 3 (Moderate/Orange) in the sand on the park day; Class 1–2 (Novice–Basic) on the border day.
- **Highlights:** the **Singing Dune** and **Aktau mountains** ridden en route; an evening soak at the **Chundzha hot springs**; **Charyn Canyon**; the quiet **Kegen/Karkara border** (KZ → KG, clocks +1 h); laghman country in the Uyghur District.
- **Overnights en route:** Chundzha hot-springs resort (1 night — waypoint stop).

**Option (b) "Canyon Direct"**
- **Days:** 1 · **Distance:** ~430 km *(estimate)* — Basshi → Kokpek → Charyn Canyon → Kegen border → Karakol
- **Surface/difficulty:** easy–moderate but **long** — tarmac-forward all day; the challenge is saddle time and the border queue, not the surface — ADV Class 1 (Novice/Green). Still sees the dune (a short park run in the morning) only if time allows — honestly, this option trades the full Singing Dune/Aktau piste day and the hot springs for a **freed day**.
- **Highlights:** Charyn Canyon with more time on-site; a full extra day banked for the anchors.
- **Overnights en route:** none. **The freed day** becomes an extra night at an anchor (A2 or A3) or a weather buffer for Song-Kol/Suek.

### S3 — Karakol → Issyk-Kul south shore (A3)

**Option (a) "The Glacier Road"** — *the reference itinerary's line (Day 7)*
- **Days:** 1 · **Distance:** 190 km (operator leg)
- **Surface/difficulty:** **hard** — the toughest reference day: hard gravel up Barskoon gorge over **Sarimonok Pass 3,126 m**, **Barskoon Pass 3,754 m** and **Suek Pass 4,021 m**, past glaciers, then down to the lake. High-altitude, cold, weather-dependent; the rest day lands immediately after for a reason — ADV Class 2 (Basic/Yellow) touching Class 3 (Moderate/Orange) on the upper switchbacks: the "hard" is altitude and weather, not terrain, and snow bumps it a full class.
- **Highlights:** the 4,021 m high point of the tour, glaciers at arm's length, the Barskoon waterfalls.
- **Overnights en route:** none.

**Option (b) "The Coast Road"**
- **Days:** 1 · **Distance:** ~130 km *(estimate)*
- **Surface/difficulty:** **easy** — south-shore tarmac along the lake with short, optional dirt spurs — Paved / ADV Class 1 (Novice/Green) on the spurs.
- **Highlights:** a second look at **Jeti-Oguz** from the road, the **Skazka (Fairy Tale) canyon** walk done en route instead of on the rest day, lakeside villages.
- **Overnights en route:** none. The honest trade: no glaciers, no 4,000 m — this is the bail-out if the group or the weather says no to Suek.

### S4 — Issyk-Kul south shore → Song-Kol (A4)

**Option (a) "Eagle Hunters & 33 Parrots"** — *the reference itinerary's line (Days 9–10, split)*
- **Days:** 2 split with a Kochkor overnight (200 km + 130 km, operator legs) — or 1 long day, ~270 km mixed *(estimate)*, for a group that wants the freed day.
- **Surface/difficulty:** moderate — tarmac and good gravel along the Terskey Alatau, then the **33 Parrots Pass (3,133 m)** gravel switchbacks up to the lake — ADV Class 2 (Basic/Yellow), peaks Class 3 (Moderate/Orange) on the loose hairpin insides and the meadow two-track to camp.
- **Highlights:** the **farmhouse lunch with an eagle-hunting family**, Kochkor's felt workshops, the switchback climb, first sight of Song-Kol.
- **Overnights en route:** Kochkor guest house (reference = split; skipped in the one-day variant).

**Option (b) "Tosor Pass Direttissima"**
- **Days:** 1 · **Distance:** ~110 km *(estimate)*
- **Surface/difficulty:** **hard — the hardest riding of the tour**: straight up over **Tosor Pass, 3,893 m**, on an exceptionally rough high-alpine gravel/single-track: fords, rock steps, mud, possible snow near the top even in September; steep loose descent on the far side, then west to the lake. Conditions vary sharply year to year — a strong-group, good-weather-window option only — ADV Class 4 (Difficult/Red), peaks Class 5 (Severe/Black) if the upper fords run high or snow lies near the top: **the terrain ceiling of the entire tour**. *(Sources: [Central Asia Guide — Tosor Pass](https://central-asia.guide/kyrgyzstan/destinations-kg/issyk-kul/tosor-pass/), [OunTravela — Tian-Shan/Tosor](https://ountravela.com/en/overlanding-off-road-trip/kyrgyzstan-travel-map/tian-shan-tosor-pass-arabel-engilchek/).)*
- **Highlights:** the wildest pass of the trip, near-zero traffic, the direct herders' line from the lake shore to the high plateau.
- **Overnights en route:** none. Skips the eagle hunters and Kochkor — the trade is culture for terrain.

### S5 — Song-Kol → Saty / Kolsai (A5)

**Option (a) "Grand Western Loop"** — *the reference itinerary's line (Days 11–14)*
- **Days:** 4 · **Distances:** 240 km (Song-Kol → **Moldo-Ashuu 3,546 m** & **Kara-Keche 3,384 m** → Suusamyr, operator leg) + 153 km (**Too-Ashuu Pass** → Bishkek, operator leg) + 261 km (Boom gorge → Cholpon-Ata) + 325 km (Tüp → Karkara border → Saty).
- **Surface/difficulty:** one **hard** rocky off-road day (the Song-Kol descent and the two passes), then moderate-to-easy tarmac: the Too-Ashuu tunnel and descent, Boom gorge, north-shore lake roads, the Karkara valley gravel to the border — ADV Class 3 (Moderate/Orange) on the off-road day, peaks Class 4 (Difficult/Red) on the Moldo-Ashuu rocky descent; the two tarmac days are Paved, the Karkara gravel Class 2 (Basic/Yellow).
- **Highlights:** the full western sweep — Suusamyr's vast pasture, Bishkek, the **Cholpon-Ata petroglyphs**, smoked lake fish on the north shore, the Karkara valley.
- **Overnights en route:** Suusamyr cabin camp, Bishkek hotel, Cholpon-Ata hotel (all waypoint stops).

**Option (b) "North Shore Direct"**
- **Days:** 2 · **Distances:** ~250 km *(estimate)* (Song-Kol → **Kalmak-Ashuu** descent → Kochkor → Balykchy → north shore → Cholpon-Ata) + 325 km (Cholpon-Ata → Karkara border → Saty, the reference Day-14 leg).
- **Surface/difficulty:** moderate then easy — the Kalmak-Ashuu track is the **best-graded of all the Song-Kol accesses** (a maintained gravel road, snow-cleared late May–October, but greasy after rain), then tarmac around the lake's east end and along the north shore — ADV Class 2 (Basic/Yellow) on the descent (Class 3 when greasy after rain), then Paved. *(Sources: [Nomad's Land — Kalmak-Ashuu pass](https://nomadsland.travel/en/before-you-go/kyrgyzstan/passes/kalmak-ashuu-pass), [OunTravela — Song-Kul road access](https://ountravela.com/en/overlanding-off-road-trip/kyrgyzstan-travel-map/lake-song-kul/).)*
- **Highlights:** still gets the petroglyphs and the north-shore fish stands; **frees 2 days**. The trade: no Suusamyr, no Too-Ashuu, no Bishkek.
- **Overnights en route:** Cholpon-Ata hotel (1 night). **The two freed days** become extra anchor nights (A3 or A4) and/or the Song-Kol/Suek weather buffer.
- **Naming note:** the Kalmak-Ashuu road is the **same Kochkor-side corridor S4a climbs** — its serpentine is what tour literature nicknames "33 parrots" (most sources put the pass at ~3,446 m; the operator's 3,133 m figure is kept in the reference table). Riding S4a up and S5b down repeats that road in opposite directions — if the group rode S4a in, weigh S4b (Tosor) or accept the repeat for the freed days.

### S6 — Saty → Almaty (base)

**Option (a) "Kolsai Morning & the Run Home"** — *the reference itinerary's line (Day 15)*
- **Days:** 1 · **Distance:** 300 km
- **Surface/difficulty:** easy — the Kaindy access track in the morning (rough but short), then tarmac Saty → Shelek → Almaty — ADV Class 1–2 (Novice–Basic); the Kaindy jeep track is the exception at Class 3 (Moderate/Orange).
- **Highlights:** the walk to **Kolsai Lake-1** (and **Lake Kaindy** if missed on arrival), then the celebration dinner in Almaty.
- **Overnights en route:** none.

**Option (b) — Charyn "Moon Canyon" detour note:** groups with time and energy can add the **Charyn "Moon Canyon" (Lunar Canyon) viewpoint** off the Shelek road — a short detour from the reference line, worthwhile if Day 5/S2 skipped or rushed Charyn. Not a separate route, just a flagged stop.

## The flex principle

**The 16-day frame and the anchor nights are FIXED; the options only trade difficulty and pace.** Days freed by faster options (S2b frees 1, S4a-in-one-day frees 1, S5b frees 2) are not spent going farther — they become **extra anchor nights or weather buffer**. Song-Kol (A4) and the Suek glacier road (S3a) are the weather-dependent cruxes of the trip; a buffer day parked at A2 or A3 is what lets the group wait out a storm instead of forcing a 4,000 m pass in snow.

## How to choose

1. **Ride the Day-2 training day as a group skill test** — it doubles as the **option-selector**. The ride leader watches everyone through the same drills (off-road stance, gravel braking, sand basics) and the ~120 km foothill loop. In [Tkacs ADV rating](04-roads.md) terms, the day maps each rider onto the terrain classes — Rookie, Intermediate or Proficient per class — and the group then picks options rated **at or below the highest class every rider handles at Intermediate or better**.
2. **The verdict picks S3 and S4** — the two segments where the options diverge most:
   - Everyone solid, standing naturally, relaxed in sand → **S3a Glacier Road** and **S4b Tosor** are on the table.
   - Mixed group or anyone tense on gravel → **S3a with the S3b Coast Road as the named bail-out**, and **S4a via Kochkor** (the split keeps the eagle hunters anyway — no loss).
3. **S1 is decided the night before Day 3** (weather on the Assy plateau decides it as much as skill); **S2 and S5** are pace choices, not skill choices — pick (b) variants only if the group prefers banking anchor nights/buffer over the Chundzha springs or the Bishkek loop.
4. **When in doubt, take the easier connection.** The anchors are guaranteed either way — that is the whole point of the design.
