# Studio Ghibli / Hayao Miyazaki / *Ponyo*

**Whose interest:** **Galiya** loves Hayao Miyazaki / Studio Ghibli films, **especially *Ponyo***.
This is one of the most weighted interests when choosing stops. Good news: the loop already lands
the single best real-world *Ponyo* site, plus a strong *Spirited Away*-adjacent onsen.

## On-route — the real Ghibli/*Ponyo* sites

### Tomonoura (鞆の浦) — the *Ponyo* town **[ON-ROUTE: Day 18 / Onomichi → Kurashiki]**
- **What & why it fits:** the small seaside fishing town on the Setouchi coast where **Hayao
  Miyazaki actually stayed (~two months, 2004–05) and conceived *Ponyo***. He lived in an old-style
  house on the harbour; the cliffside-house setting and stone-jetty harbour visibly inspired the
  film. This is **the** literal *Ponyo* pilgrimage — a direct hit for Galiya and a lovely,
  kid-friendly port stop for Aslan. **[KID]**
- **What to see:** the stone jetty and **jōyatō lighthouse** (the iconic harbour symbol), the
  hillside lanes, the old townscape (a preserved district), and views that match the film's
  fishing village. Pair with the historic harbour walk.
- **On the route already:** a Day-18 POI between Onomichi and Kurashiki. No change needed.
- **Caveats:** it's a *town*, not a Ghibli attraction — there's no official Ghibli museum here, so
  set expectations: this is atmosphere and film-location pilgrimage, not merch/rides. Quieter on
  weekdays. *Reconfirm any specific "Ponyo" café/exhibit before relying — these come and go.*
- Sources: https://allabout-japan.com/en/article/405/ · https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Ponyo

### Dōgo Onsen Honkan (道後温泉本館) — *Spirited Away* resonance **[ON-ROUTE: Day 15 / Matsuyama]**
- **What & why it fits:** the grand 1894 wooden bathhouse at Dōgo is **widely cited as a visual
  inspiration/resonance for the bathhouse in *Spirited Away*** (Studio Ghibli has never officially
  confirmed a single model, but the labyrinthine wooden bathhouse atmosphere is the closest
  real-world analogue and is marketed as such). Bathing here is both an onsen highlight (see
  [onsen-sento.md](onsen-sento.md)) **and** a Ghibli-adjacent experience for Galiya.
- **On the route already:** the centrepiece of the Day-15 Matsuyama rest day.
- **Caveat:** frame it honestly as "evokes / inspired by," not an official Ghibli site. Confirm
  current bathing arrangements (the Honkan has undergone phased restoration).
- Source: https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/D%C5%8Dgo_Onsen

## On/near Osaka — Ghibli shopping (easy bookend wins for Galiya)

### Donguri Republic (どんぐり共和国) — official Ghibli shops **[OSAKA BOOKEND: Days 1, 21]**
- **What:** the official Studio Ghibli retail chain — Totoro/Ponyo/Kiki plush, goods and
  collectibles. Branches sit in major Osaka shopping complexes (e.g. around Umeda / Namba /
  shopping malls). A reliable way to satisfy the Ghibli theme even without the park/museum.
- **Fit:** drops neatly into an Osaka bookend afternoon (Day 1 or 21), and Aslan gets a toy/plush
  souvenir out of it too — overlaps with [toys-anime-transformers.md](toys-anime-transformers.md).
- **Caveat:** confirm the specific branch/floor and hours near your Osaka base before relying.
- Source: https://benelic.com/donguri/ (official Ghibli goods / Donguri Republic)

## Off-route / bookend — the big Ghibli destinations (strict ticketing)

### Ghibli Park (ジブリパーク) — Aichi, near Nagoya **[OFF-ROUTE / SHINKANSEN-CORRIDOR BOOKEND]**
- **What:** the large Studio Ghibli theme park (opened Nov 2022) in **Moricoro Park, Nagakute,
  ~50 min east of central Nagoya** — areas include Ghibli's Grand Warehouse, Hill of Youth,
  Dondoko Forest (*Totoro*), Mononoke Village and Valley of Witches (*Kiki*/*Howl*). A major
  family-and-Galiya magnet.
- **Fit:** **off the Osaka loop**, but Nagoya sits **directly on the Tōkaidō Shinkansen line
  between Tokyo and Osaka** — so it's reachable as a deliberate stop on the Day-0→Day-1 or
  Day-22 train segments **if you add a Nagoya night**. It is *not* a quick stop: a satisfying
  visit is most of a day.
- **Ticketing (critical):** **advance, date-and-time, reservation-only — no walk-ins.** As of 2026
  Ghibli Park has **moved away from the lottery**; tickets release at **14:00 JST on the 10th of
  the month, two months ahead**, via **Lawson Tickets** and **Klook** (Klook is the English-friendly
  route). They sell out fast, especially weekends and the Grand Warehouse. **Book the instant the
  window opens.** Aichi residents get priority around the 27th.
- Sources: https://ghibli-park.jp/en/ticket/ · https://japanuncharted.com/aichi/ghibli ·
  https://blooloop.com/theme-park/news/ghibli-park-lottery-ticketing/

### Ghibli Museum (三鷹の森ジブリ美術館) — Mitaka, Tokyo **[TOKYO BOOKEND]**
- **What:** the original Miyazaki-designed museum in Mitaka (western Tokyo) — whimsical exhibits,
  the Catbus room (kids only), an exclusive short film, the rooftop robot soldier.
- **Fit:** only feasible from the **Tokyo bookend** (Day 0 or the Day-22/23 window), and only with
  planning — **advance date-specific tickets, sold via Lawson, released the 10th of the prior
  month; they sell out almost immediately.** No same-day entry. A half-day.
- **Caveat:** closed Tuesdays; strict timed entry; **buy the moment sales open**.
- Source: https://www.ghibli-museum.jp/en/

## Quick guidance for this family
- **The loop already nails the two best *free*, no-ticket Ghibli experiences for Galiya** —
  **Tomonoura (*Ponyo*, Day 18)** and **Dōgo Onsen (*Spirited Away* feel, Day 15)** — plus easy
  **Donguri Republic** shopping in Osaka. That's a strong Ghibli thread with zero route change.
- **Ghibli Park** is the dream upgrade but is an **add-a-Nagoya-night + book-tickets-2-months-out**
  decision on the Shinkansen corridor — flag it like Naoshima/USJ, not a casual stop.
- **Ghibli Museum (Tokyo)** is only worth it if you can secure the hard-to-get timed tickets for a
  Tokyo-bookend half-day; otherwise skip without guilt.
