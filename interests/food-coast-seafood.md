# Galiya's Coast Food Trail — seafood, cheese & coast breweries

Galiya is the **big foodie** of the family, and the first four days run straight down the coast
through its best eating — so the food is an explicit thread, not an afterthought. Every pick below
is a **regional specialty eaten where it's made**, and each falls on a day already on the route —
**no detours required.** Aslan always has an easy out (grilled cheese, plain fish & chips, a burger).
Re-confirm hours/seasons before relying on any single spot — coast kitchens keep their own hours.

## Day 1 — Westport: Dungeness crab & chowder

- **Dungeness crab** is the Pacific Northwest crab — sweet, meaty, often sold cooked off the boat. Westport is a working crab/fishing port, so this is the place to eat it at the source.
- **Also here:** Willapa/Grays Harbor **oysters**, **razor clams**, **clam chowder**, and **smoked salmon/tuna** for the road.
- **Picks:** **Merino's Seafood Market** (cooked crab, smoked fish to go) · **Bennett's Fish Shack** (sit-down chowder, fish & chips) · the marina has fish-and-chips windows with sea-lion views. [Aslan: fish & chips, popcorn shrimp.]
- Ref: https://www.westportwa.com/

## Day 2 — Astoria: albacore fish & chips

- **Bowpicker Fish & Chips** — the famous one: beer-battered **local albacore tuna** served from a converted gillnet boat parked on land. Cash-ish, lines, seasonal hours — go early.
- **Also:** **South Bay Wild** (boat-to-table catch), brewpub fare along the riverfront, and Astoria's growing coffee/bakery scene for breakfast before the ride.
- Ref: https://www.travelastoria.com/

## Day 3 — Tillamook: cheese & ice cream (the marquee stop)

- **Tillamook Creamery** — the centerpiece of the whole food trail and a kid magnet too: a **free self-guided factory tour** watching cheese being made, a **cheese-tasting** counter, the famous **ice cream** scoop counter, and a café. [Aslan: mac-and-cheese, grilled cheese, ice cream — and the gift shop.]
- **Don't miss:** the squeaky fresh **cheese curds**.
- **Optional nearby:** **Blue Heron French Cheese Company** (brie, a petting farm — another Aslan win) and **Pelican Brewing (Pacific City)** later on the Three Capes loop.
- Ref: https://www.tillamook.com/creamery

## Days 3–4 — Yachats: the rest-base table

- The two-night rest base is also the **foodie high point**. **Luna Sea Fish House** is dock-to-table fish & chowder run by a fisherman; **Ona Restaurant & Lounge** is the **splurge dinner** for Galiya (Pacific Northwest seafood, good wine). **Yachats Brewing** for a relaxed family meal + local beer.
- Slow chowder lunches and a nice dinner are exactly what a rest day over the Fourth is for.
- Ref: https://yachats.org/

## Coast breweries (relaxed family dinners)

The Oregon/Washington coast has a strong craft-beer scene; brewpubs double as kid-friendly dinners.

- **Pelican Brewing — Pacific City** (Day 3): brewing **right on the beach** at Cape Kiwanda, burgers + beer with a dune-and-Haystack view. [Aslan: burger, beach out front.]
- **Fort George Brewery — Astoria** (Day 2): a local institution; pizza + beer in a historic building.
- **Yachats Brewing** (Days 3–4): relaxed rest-base dinner with local pints.
- **Buoy Beer / Astoria riverfront** (Day 2): brewery on the water with sea lions audible below.

---

## Beyond the coast (Days 5–7): mountain-town fare

Once inland, the food shifts to hearty mountain-town comfort:

- **Mount St. Helens gateway (Days 5–6):** **elk & beef burgers** and diner fare — Patty's Place / Fire Mountain Grill area near Toutle.
- **Packwood (Day 6):** burger shacks — **Cliff Droppers** for big burgers before the Day-7 finale.
- **Enumclaw (Day 7):** a real meal back near home after the Chinook Pass descent.

[Aslan's easy out everywhere: a burger, grilled cheese, or plain fish & chips is on basically every menu on this route.]
