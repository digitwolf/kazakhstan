# Interests Library — places & experiences mapped to what this family loves

This library is **interest-driven**, not geography/logistics-driven. It answers a different
question than [`../research/`](../research/README.md): *"Given what Ruslan, Galiya and Aslan
actually love, where are the points of interest — and which ones are actually reachable on the
Osaka loop (or on the Tokyo/Osaka bookends)?"*

Read [`../tour/00-family.md`](../tour/00-family.md) for the full profiles. The seven interest
themes covered here come straight from that file's "what we like" checklist, plus a watch for
**KPop Demon Hunters / K-pop** tie-ins for Aslan.

Some POIs sit **on the loop** (actionable now); many are **off-route or bookend-only**
(listed so the family knows what exists and where). **Every entry carries a FIT tag:**

- **[ON-ROUTE: Day N / <stop>]** — already on the planned route on that day.
- **[NEAR-ROUTE: short detour from <stop>]** — a small, sane detour from a planned stop.
- **[TOKYO/OSAKA BOOKEND]** — feasible from the Day-0/Day-22 Tokyo nights or the Osaka
  start/finish days (Days 1, 21, 22), not the riding loop itself.
- **[OFF-ROUTE]** — iconic but not reachable without reworking the trip; listed for awareness.

**Caveat on all opening hours / ticketing:** Japanese attractions change hours, closed days and
(especially) reservation systems often. Treat every hour/price/closure here as **"reconfirm
before relying"**; sources are linked per item.

## Iconic off-route / bookend options (know they exist, where they are)

- **Honda Collection Hall** (Motegi, Tochigi) — free, the strongest factory collection; **off-route**, only as a dedicated Kanto day from the Tokyo bookend. See `../research/motorcycle-museums.md`.
- **Yamaha Communication Plaza** (Iwata) & **Suzuki Plaza** (Hamamatsu) — Tōkaidō corridor, **off the loop** entirely.
- **Ghibli Park** (Aichi, near Nagoya) & **Ghibli Museum** (Mitaka, Tokyo) — both **off-route/bookend** with strict advance ticketing (see [ghibli-miyazaki.md](ghibli-miyazaki.md)).
- **Super Nintendo World @ USJ Osaka** — technically reachable as an **Osaka bookend day** (Day 1 or Day 21 area), but it's a full theme-park day, not a quick stop (see [nintendo-mario.md](nintendo-mario.md)).
- **Naoshima art islands** — half-day from Kurashiki via Uno; documented trade-offs in `../research/naoshima-feasibility.md`.
- **Ghibli Museum / Pokémon Center Tokyo / Kiddyland / KPop Demon Hunters pop-ups** — all Tokyo-bookend options.

## Suggestions worth promoting into the itinerary later (owner decision)

These are *easy* on-route or near-route wins this library surfaced. They are **not** changes —
just flags for the tour-expert to consider on a later pass.

1. **Sanuki udon detour (Kagawa).** Galiya's love of noodles + Japan's most famous udon region sits **just off the Shikoku route**. A self-serve udon stop near Takamatsu/Kotohira could be woven into a Shikoku transfer day (e.g. an eastern detour) without adding a night. See [food-ramen-noodles.md](food-ramen-noodles.md).
2. **An Osaka "kid + foodie + Ghibli" bookend afternoon.** Day 1 and Day 21 are already Osaka. **Daimaru Umeda 13F stacks Nintendo Osaka + Pokémon Center + Pikachu Sweets café** in one building, and **Donguri Republic (Ghibli)** + **Den Den Town** are nearby — a single afternoon hits Aslan (Nintendo/toys), Galiya (Ghibli shop) and food at once. Worth naming explicitly in the Osaka day notes.
3. **USJ / Super Nintendo World as an optional added Osaka day.** The single biggest Mario magnet in Japan is in Osaka but needs a **whole day + app-based timed-entry / Express Pass**. If the family wants it, it's a +1-night decision on the Osaka end — flag it like Naoshima.
4. **Isamu Noguchi Garden Museum (Takamatsu)** — a near-route art option if a Kagawa/udon detour is ever added; reservation-only, by appointment.
5. **Donguri Republic shop note for the Tomonoura day.** Day 18 already visits the *Ponyo* town; pairing it with a Ghibli-shop stop in Osaka closes the loop on Galiya's Ghibli theme nicely.
