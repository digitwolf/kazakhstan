## On-route — the two that fit the loop (both already in the plan)

### Kawasaki Good Times World (カワサキワールド) — Kobe **[ON-ROUTE: Day 19 / Kobe]**
- **Where:** inside the Kobe Maritime Museum, Meriken Park, Kobe — right on the Day-19
  Kurashiki → Himeji → Kobe run; a 5-min walk from Kobe Harborland (the Day-19 evening stop).
- **What & why it fits:** Kawasaki Heavy Industries' corporate museum — a motorcycle gallery
  where you can **sit on several bikes** (a big win for Aslan), plus jet-skis, a riding simulator,
  a Shinkansen driver's cab, a helicopter and robots. The one major corporate moto museum
  directly on the loop, and a Kawasaki shrine for a Kawasaki/Yamaha/Honda-loving family. **[KID]**
- **Caveats:** **closed Mondays** (next day if Mon is a national holiday) + Dec 29–Jan 3;
  ~10:00–18:00, last entry 17:30; ¥900 adult / ¥400 school-age / **free under 6**. Paid bike lot
  at Meriken Park. **Confirm Day 19 is not a Monday.**
- Source: https://www.khi.co.jp/kawasakiworld/english/

### Shikoku Automobile Museum (四国自動車博物館) — Kōnan, Kōchi **[ON-ROUTE: Day 11 / Kōchi]**
- **Where:** Noichi-chō, Kōnan City, Kōchi — ~6 km past the Anpanman Museum stop, on the same
  Day-11 line into Kōchi. Effectively zero added distance.
- **What & why it fits:** small private museum, **~30 motorcycles + ~30 cars**, 1960s–80s classics
  and race machines — Honda, Yamaha and rarities. A fun 30–45 min for the moto family. **[KID]**
- **Caveats:** **closed Mon AND Tue** (open if a national holiday); 10:00–16:00; ¥800 adult /
  ¥300 elementary / **under-6 free**. **Confirm Day 11 weekday** — if Mon/Tue, shift to the
  Day-12 morning or skip.
- Source: https://lovemota.vistanet.co.jp/museum/

## On/near-route — bike culture, showrooms & dealers (worth a stroll, not a detour)

### Osaka — the Kansai bike scene **[OSAKA BOOKEND: Days 1, 21]**
- Osaka has dense moto retail — used-bike dealers, gear shops (RS Taichi, Kushitani, 2Rinkan
  superstores) and parts. Nothing is a single "must-see," but a gear/souvenir browse fits an
  Osaka evening for Ruslan. **2Rinkan** and **NAP'S**-style superstores in the Kansai suburbs are
  the easiest one-stop browse. *Confirm a specific branch's hours near the Suita base before relying.*
- **Practical tie-in:** the bikes themselves come from **Bike Rental Japan, Suita** (see
  `../research/motorcycle-rental.md`) — the handover day (Day 1) is itself a moto-culture moment
  (walk-around of the Africa Twin + CB400X).

### Kobe / Meriken Park area **[ON-ROUTE: Day 19]**
- Beyond Kawasaki World, Meriken Park and the Harborland waterfront are an easy, scenic evening
  for the family — no extra moto stop needed, the museum carries the theme.

### Note on "bike streets" / moto cafés
- Japan's famous rider gathering spots are mostly **Kanto** (e.g. Ueno's *Korin-chō* motorcycle-shop
  street in Tokyo; Daikoku PA in Yokohama Bay; the Okutama / Hakone weekend-rider roads) — all
  **off this loop or Tokyo-bookend only**, and the weekend-crowd roads are explicitly something the
  trip *avoids* for the newer rider. There is no single iconic "bike street" on the Kansai/Shikoku
  loop; the museums above are the anchor moto-culture stops. *(Reconfirm any café/meet before relying;
  these come and go.)*

## Off-route / bookend — the famous factory museums (listed for awareness)

All three big factory museums are on the **Tokyo–Nagoya Tōkaidō corridor**, which the Osaka loop
never touches. They are **not loop stops**; the only realistic path is a Tokyo-bookend side trip.
Full detail in [`../research/motorcycle-museums.md`](../research/motorcycle-museums.md).

| Museum | Location | Fit | Note |
|--------|----------|-----|------|
| **Honda Collection Hall** | Motegi, Tochigi (Mobility Resort Motegi) | **[OFF-ROUTE / TOKYO BOOKEND]** | **Free**; reopened Mar 2024; the strongest collection of the three. Full day round-trip from central Tokyo (~2.5–3 h each way) — adds a night. The best moto-museum upgrade *if* the family wants one. |
| **Yamaha Communication Plaza** | Iwata, Shizuoka | **[OFF-ROUTE]** | Free; limited weekend hours (open ~2nd & 4th Saturdays). Tōkaidō corridor; only fits a Tokyo→Osaka one-way (which this trip deliberately isn't). |
| **Suzuki Plaza** | Hamamatsu, Shizuoka | **[OFF-ROUTE]** | Free; **reservation required**; closed Mondays. Bikes, cars, and Suzuki's textile-loom origins. Same corridor — off the loop. |
| **Iwashita Collection** | Yufuin, Ōita (Kyushu) | **[OFF-ROUTE]** | ~600 bikes (incl. the only surviving Ducati Apollo) — Japan's greatest private hoard, but **in Kyushu**, entirely off this Honshu/Shikoku/Kansai loop. |

- **Honda Welcome Plaza Aoyama (Tokyo) is permanently CLOSED** (31 Mar 2025) — do **not** plan a
  Tokyo bookend around it. Source: `../research/motorcycle-museums.md`.

## Quick guidance for this family
- **The two on-route museums (Kawasaki World, Shikoku Auto Museum) are the realistic moto-culture
  highlights** and are already in the plan — just lock the weekdays (Kawasaki = not Monday;
  Shikoku = not Mon/Tue).
- If Ruslan really wants a factory museum, **Honda Collection Hall (Motegi)** is the one to bolt
  onto the Tokyo bookend — free, fully reopened, best collection — as a deliberate +1 night, not a
  loop reroute.
- Don't bend the loop toward Yamaha/Suzuki/Iwashita — they're geographically incompatible.
