## On-route — the two standout art museums (already in the plan)

### Ōhara Museum of Art (大原美術館) — Kurashiki **[ON-ROUTE: Day 18 / Kurashiki]**
- **What & why it fits:** **Japan's first museum of Western art** (founded 1930), in the willow-
  lined Bikan canal quarter. Holds genuine masterpieces — **El Greco, Monet, Gauguin, Matisse,
  Picasso** — plus modern Japanese art and crafts (Munakata, Kawai). A serious, world-class art
  collection in a beautiful, compact, child-manageable canal town. A direct hit for Galiya.
- **On the route already:** Kurashiki Bikan is the Day-18 base; the museum is right in the quarter.
- **Caveat:** **closed Mondays** (and New Year) — **confirm Day 18 is not a Monday.** Compact enough
  to enjoy with a 6-year-old in an hour or two.
- Source: https://www.ohara.or.jp/en/

### Ōtsuka Museum of Art (大塚国際美術館) — Naruto **[ON-ROUTE: Day 8 / Naruto]**
- **What & why it fits:** Japan's largest exhibition space — **over 1,000 full-size ceramic-board
  reproductions** of the world's masterpieces (the Sistine Chapel ceiling recreated in full, the
  *Mona Lisa*, *The Last Supper*, Vermeer, Van Gogh's sunflowers). Because they're durable ceramic
  copies you can **get close, photograph freely, even touch** — which makes it brilliant with a
  kid, and a fascinating "all the world's art in one walk" experience for Galiya. **[KID]**
- **Fit:** in **Naruto**, the Day-8 landing point (right by the whirlpools) — essentially on-route,
  though it's a big museum (a full 2–4 h if you do it justice). A great rainy-day / tired-rider
  option on the Shikoku arrival day.
- **Caveat:** **closed Mondays** (open holiday-Mondays, then closed next day); large — budget time
  or do a highlights loop. Pricey admission. Confirm the Day-8 weekday.
- Source: https://o-museum.or.jp/en/

## Near-route — strong art options if you flex a day

### Naoshima art islands (直島) **[NEAR-ROUTE: half-day from Kurashiki via Uno]**
- **What:** the Setouchi contemporary-art islands — **Yayoi Kusama's yellow & red pumpkins**,
  **Benesse House**, the **Chichū Art Museum** (Monet/Turrell/De Maria), **Lee Ufan Museum**, and
  the **Art House Project** in Honmura. A bucket-list art pilgrimage and a striking
  architecture-meets-art experience for Galiya.
- **Fit & trade-offs:** **not a long push and not a whole-day ride** — from **Kurashiki (Day 18)**
  the right port is **Uno (~40 min), then a ~20-min ferry; park the bikes and cross as foot
  passengers.** A satisfying visit is a **half-day**. But fitting it with **no extra night** forces
  dropping Day-19 stops (Bizen pottery / Kawasaki World) via a base-shift. **The Kusama pumpkins
  (outdoor, free) are the kid magnet** for Aslan; the indoor museums lean adult.
- **Monday trap:** Chichū, Lee Ufan, Benesse House and the Art House Project are **all closed
  Mondays** — a Monday visit closes nearly everything except the two outdoor pumpkins.
- **→ Full analysis (ferries, time budget, no-night options):**
  [`../research/naoshima-feasibility.md`](../research/naoshima-feasibility.md).

### Isamu Noguchi Garden Museum (イサム・ノグチ庭園美術館) — Takamatsu **[NEAR-ROUTE: Kagawa]**
- **What:** the preserved studio and stone-sculpture garden of **Isamu Noguchi** in Mure,
  Takamatsu — his Japan atelier, with finished and unfinished basalt/granite works left as he had
  them. A serene, world-class sculpture experience for an art lover.
- **Fit:** **near-route in Kagawa** — same direction as a potential **Sanuki udon detour** (see
  [food-ramen-noodles.md](food-ramen-noodles.md)). Not on the current line, but a natural pairing
  if a Kagawa/Takamatsu day is ever added.
- **Caveat (critical):** **reservation-only, by advance application**, with **fixed guided viewing
  times** (typically Tue/Thu/Sat) — **you must book ahead; no drop-ins.** Verify days/booking.
- Source: https://www.isamunoguchi.or.jp/english/

The loop already builds in **hands-on craft** experiences that double as art immersion for Galiya:
- **Awa indigo (aizome) dyeing** — Day 9 (Udatsu/Wakimachi).
- **Tosa washi paper-making** — Day 12 (Ino-cho).

These are detailed in `../tour/01-itinerary.md`; listed here so the art thread is visible in one

## Quick guidance for this family
- **The loop already gives Galiya two exceptional, child-friendly art museums** — **Ōtsuka
  (Naruto, Day 8)** and **Ōhara (Kurashiki, Day 18)** — both varied and both genuinely engaging for
  a 6-year-old (Ōtsuka especially, since you can touch/photograph everything). Just **avoid the
  Monday closures** on both.
- **Naoshima** is the dream art upgrade but trades off Day-19 stops or needs +1 night — decide
  deliberately via the feasibility file; the **pumpkins** are the kid-safe core if you do go.
- **Noguchi (Takamatsu)** is a beautiful near-route bonus *only* if a Kagawa/udon day is added, and
  **must be reserved ahead**.
- The **hands-on crafts** (indigo, washi, Bizen, towel) already give the trip a strong applied-art
  thread without any change.
