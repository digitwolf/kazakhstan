# Aslan's Wins — the kid stops, day by day

Aslan is 6. He loves **toys & souvenirs** (a souvenir from every stop), **animals & wildlife**,
**hands-on museums**, **big machines**, the **volcano**, and the **cheese factory**. The trip is
built so **every single day has a kid hook** — and the one rest day (the Fourth of July) is loaded
with them too. This file is the at-a-glance "what's in it for Aslan" guide, with the animal stops,
the hands-on museums, and where to find toys/candy/souvenirs. (Full safety/pace rules in
`tour/00-overview.md`.)

## Animals & wildlife (his favorite category)

- **Sea lions** — barking on the **Westport marina** (Day 1) and the **Astoria docks** (Day 2); then the big one: **Sea Lion Caves** near Yachats (Day 4), an elevator down into America's largest sea cave full of wild Steller sea lions.
- **Tide pools** — **Haystack Rock, Cannon Beach** (Day 3) and the **Yachats/Cape Perpetua** tide pools (Day 4): starfish, anemones, crabs, and **puffins** nesting on Haystack Rock at low tide. *(Keep him back from the wet rocks at Thor's Well/Devil's Churn — those are dangerous.)*
- **Oregon Coast Aquarium, Newport (optional, Day 3)** — walk-through ocean tunnel, sea otters, and **touch tanks**.
- **Roaming elk & deer** — near **Mount St. Helens** and especially **Packwood** (Day 6), where elk wander the town lawns at dusk; **marmots, chipmunks and deer** up at Paradise/Tipsoo (Day 7).
- **Blue Heron petting farm (Tillamook, optional Day 3)** — goats and farm animals next to cheese.

## Hands-on museums & big machines

- **Tillamook Air Museum (Day 3)** — vintage aircraft inside a colossal **WWII wooden blimp hangar**; the scale alone is a wow. (Dad win too — see `../research/motorcycle-museums.md`.)
- **Astoria Column (Day 2)** — climb the spiral stairs to the top and **launch balsa-wood gliders** (sold at the gift shop) out over the river — a perennial kid favorite.
- **Columbia River Maritime Museum (Day 2)** — board a lightship, see Coast Guard rescue boats, the bar-crossing simulator.
- **Mount St. Helens Visitor Center, Silver Lake (Day 5)** — **always-open**, hands-on, with a **walk-into volcano model** and the dramatic May 18, 1980 story. Aslan's kind of place.
- **World Kite Museum, Long Beach (Day 2, optional)** — kites everywhere on the world's-longest-beach.

## The volcano & the mountain (set-pieces)

- **Mount St. Helens** — a real, recently-active volcano with a blown-off top; the crater views from SR-504 viewpoints (Day 6) and the eruption story (Day 5) are genuinely thrilling for a 6-year-old.
- **Mount Rainier (Day 7)** — **snow to touch in July** (lingering roadside snowbanks), **Narada Falls** and **Myrtle Falls** waterfalls, wildflower meadows and mountain critters at Paradise/Tipsoo.

## Toys, souvenirs, candy & gift shops

A souvenir from every stop is the goal — these are the reliable spots:

- **Tillamook Creamery store (Day 3)** — ice cream, fudge, cheese, plush cows and toys.
- **Astoria Column gift shop (Day 2)** — the balsa gliders (the souvenir that's also the activity).
- **Cannon Beach (Day 3)** — toy/kite/candy shops in the village; **Bruce's Candy Kitchen** (saltwater taffy).
- **Long Beach, WA (Day 2)** — boardwalk arcade, **kite shops**, candy and beach-toy stores.
- **Seaside, OR (near Cannon Beach)** — old-school promenade arcade & candy if you pass through.
- **National-park & aquarium gift shops** — Mount St. Helens, the Oregon Coast Aquarium and Mount Rainier all have kid-friendly shops (plush animals, junior-ranger gear).
- **Junior Ranger programs** — Mount St. Helens and Mount Rainier offer free **Junior Ranger booklets/badges** — a souvenir he earns. Ref: https://www.nps.gov/mora/learn/kidsyouth/

## Keeping him happy on the bike

- He rides pillion on the GS in full fitting gear; **stops every 60–90 minutes** are built into the route.
- This route is generous with **frequent towns, beaches and viewpoints**, so a break is never far — use them.
- The **rest day (Day 4)** deliberately pairs tidepools + sea lions + the parade + fireworks so the downtime day lands for him too.
- The **long Day-5 transfer** is broken into short hops and follows the rest day so he isn't tired going in. (Full child-pillion safety setup in `../research/bikes-and-prep.md`.)
