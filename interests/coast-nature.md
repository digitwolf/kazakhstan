# Coast & Nature — lighthouses, scenery & wildlife (Galiya's quiet-beautiful list)

Galiya loves not just the coast food but the **coast itself** — lighthouses, headlands, dramatic
shoreline, and quiet beautiful places to simply stop. This is her scenery-and-nature list: the
**lighthouses**, the **dramatic coast and tide pools**, and the **wildlife** along the route, plus
the **alpine scenery** at the end. (The original Japan "art museums" file didn't translate to this
trip; the family's "beautiful places to stop" interest lives here instead, where the PNW actually
delivers it.) Re-confirm hours/access before relying on any single one.

## Lighthouses (a Galiya through-line)

The Washington/Oregon coast is one of the great lighthouse coasts in the U.S. — several sit right on
the route:

- **Grays Harbor Lighthouse, Westport (Day 1)** — the **tallest lighthouse in Washington**; tours when open.
- **Cape Meares Lighthouse (Day 3)** — short, squat and scenic on the Three Capes Loop, next to the gnarled **Octopus Tree**.
- **Yaquina Head / Yaquina Bay, Newport (Day 3, optional)** — a classic tall lighthouse with tidepools and a seabird colony.
- **Heceta Head Lighthouse (Day 4)** — **one of the most photographed lighthouses in America**, perched on a headland with its keeper's house below — the rest-day photo prize.
- *(Bonus from the bridge: the **Columbia River bar** light history at the Astoria Maritime Museum, Day 2.)*

## Dramatic coast & headlands

- **Cape Perpetua Scenic Area (Day 4)** — the highest viewpoint on the Oregon coast and the most dramatic shoreline of the trip: **Thor's Well** ("the drainpipe of the Pacific"), the **Spouting Horn**, and **Devil's Churn**. Best near high tide for the drama. *(Keep well back from the wet rocks — they are genuinely dangerous.)*
- **Haystack Rock, Cannon Beach (Day 3)** — the iconic 235-ft sea stack, framed by The Needles.
- **Cape Kiwanda / Pacific City (Day 3)** — sandstone headland, dory boats and a huge climbable dune.
- **Long Beach Peninsula (Day 2)** — the "world's longest beach," wide and wind-swept.
- **Hood Canal (Day 1)** — a quiet, fjord-like inlet — the calm, beautiful opener.

## Tide pools & beaches

- **Haystack Rock tide pools (Day 3)** and the **Cape Perpetua / Yachats tide pools (Day 4)** — starfish, anemones, crabs at low tide; check a tide table and go at **low tide**.
- The **804 Trail, Yachats (Day 4)** — a flat oceanfront path right from the village, perfect for a slow beautiful stroll.

## Wildlife

- **Sea lions** — Westport docks (Day 1), Astoria docks (Day 2), and **Sea Lion Caves** (Day 4, America's largest sea cave).
- **Seabirds** — **tufted puffins** nest on Haystack Rock in early summer; murres and cormorants at Cape Perpetua and Yaquina Head.
- **Gray whales** — a resident summer pod feeds off the central Oregon coast (Depoe Bay/Yachats area); scan the water from the headlands. Ref: https://www.whalespoken.org/
- **Elk & mountain wildlife** — roaming elk near Mount St. Helens and Packwood (Day 6); marmots, deer and chipmunks at Paradise/Tipsoo (Day 7).

## Alpine scenery (the inland reward)

- **Mount St. Helens crater views (Day 6)** — the blast-zone landscape and the recovering forest, a stark, beautiful place.
- **Paradise wildflower meadows (Day 7)** — early-July subalpine bloom, **Reflection Lakes** mirroring Rainier, and the waterfalls (**Narada**, **Myrtle**) — the prettiest stops of the whole trip and a fitting visual finale.
