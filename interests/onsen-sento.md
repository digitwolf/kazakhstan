# Traditional baths — onsen & sentō

**Whose interest:** **Galiya** specifically loves traditional Japanese baths. The loop is
exceptionally strong here — it threads through three of Japan's most distinctive bathing
experiences (a UNESCO-bathable spring, dig-your-own riverbed baths, and one of the oldest grand
bathhouses in Japan), plus onsen-ryokan nights throughout.

## On-route — the standout traditional baths (the headline thread)

### Yunomine Tsuboyu (湯の峰温泉 つぼ湯) **[ON-ROUTE: Days 3–4 / Kumano Interior]**
- **What & why it fits:** a tiny wooden hut over a natural rock pool in Yunomine — **the only bath
  registered as a UNESCO World Heritage site that you can actually bathe in.** A ~1,800-year-old
  spring on the Kumano Kodō pilgrimage; the water shifts colour through the day. This is the single
  most unique traditional-bath experience on the loop and a perfect match for Galiya.
- **How:** **private, reserved 30-minute slots** (one group at a time) — book at the Yunomine
  bathhouse on arrival; small fee. Also boil eggs/veg in the public **yuzutsu** hot spring nearby.
- **On the route already:** a Day-4 (Kumano interior rest day) centrepiece.
- **Caveat:** very small/hot; manage the 6-year-old's time in it. Reserve early in the day.
- Source: https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Yunomine_Onsen

### Kawayu Onsen — dig-your-own riverbed bath (川湯温泉) **[ON-ROUTE: Day 4 / Kumano Interior]**
- **What & why it fits:** at Kawayu, hot spring water bubbles up through the **gravel bed of the
  Ōtō River** — you **scoop out your own bath** in the riverbed (and in winter a giant "Sennin-buro"
  1,000-person riverbed bath is built). A playful, hands-in-the-gravel bath the whole family,
  including Aslan, can do together. **[KID]**
- **On the route already:** a Day-4 POI alongside Tsuboyu.
- **Caveat:** swimwear-friendly riverbed bathing; check river level/season (high water can submerge
  the spots).
- Source: https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Kawayu_Onsen

### Dōgo Onsen Honkan (道後温泉本館) **[ON-ROUTE: Day 15 / Matsuyama]**
- **What & why it fits:** one of **Japan's oldest and most famous bathhouses** — a grand 1894
  three-storey wooden building, an Important Cultural Property, with a *Botchan*/Sōseki literary
  legacy and a *Spirited Away*-bathhouse resonance (see [ghibli-miyazaki.md](ghibli-miyazaki.md)).
  A grand traditional-bathhouse soak — a top Galiya experience.
- **On the route already:** the Day-15 Matsuyama rest-day centrepiece, with the retro **Botchan
  Ressha** tram and Dōgo shopping arcade alongside.
- **Caveat:** the Honkan has undergone phased restoration — **confirm which baths/floors are open**
  and current entry tiers before relying.
- Source: https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/D%C5%8Dgo_Onsen

## A note on sentō (urban public bathhouses)
- Galiya's interest includes **sentō** (neighbourhood public bathhouses, not hot-spring-fed). The
  loop is heavy on *onsen* (natural hot springs) rather than classic city *sentō*. For a true
  retro **sentō** experience, the easiest fit is an **Osaka bookend** evening: Osaka has historic
  tiled sentō and the Kansai bathhouse tradition (and large "super sentō" bath complexes). *Confirm
  a specific bathhouse's hours, tattoo policy and family/child rules before relying.*
- **Tattoo caveat (all baths):** many baths refuse visible tattoos — use **private/kashikiri
  (family) baths, cover-stickers, or tattoo-friendly facilities**. See
  [`../research/touring-practical.md`](../research/touring-practical.md).

## Off-route famous onsen (awareness only)
- **Kusatsu, Kinosaki, Beppu/Yufuin (Kyushu), Noboribetsu (Hokkaidō), Gero, Hakone** — all famous
  onsen destinations **off this loop**. The on-route line-up (Yunomine UNESCO bath → Kawayu
  riverbed → Katsuura cave → Sakino-yu sea-edge → Dōgo grand bathhouse) is a genuinely
  world-class and *varied* bathing thread, so no off-route onsen is needed.

## Quick guidance for this family
- **The loop is already a dream onsen itinerary for Galiya** — and crucially, it's *varied*
  (UNESCO bathable spring, dig-your-own riverbed, cave, sea-edge, grand wooden bathhouse), which
  fits the "no two days feel the same" design principle perfectly.
- Lock the **Tsuboyu reservation** behaviour (arrive early, 30-min slots) and **reconfirm Dōgo
  Honkan's open baths** post-restoration.
- For a classic **sentō**, slot one into an Osaka bookend evening; sort the tattoo policy ahead.
