# Nintendo / Mario

**Whose interest:** **Aslan (6)** loves Mario, Nintendo Switch and Minecraft; **Galiya** enjoys
Nintendo/Mario too — so this is a **double win**. The great news: the loop is **Osaka-anchored**,
and Osaka holds the two biggest Nintendo magnets in Japan (Super Nintendo World + the Nintendo
Osaka flagship store).

## The big one — Super Nintendo World @ Universal Studios Japan **[OSAKA BOOKEND]**
- **What & why it fits:** the immersive Mario-world land inside **Universal Studios Japan (USJ),
  Osaka** — a walk-into-the-game Mushroom Kingdom with **Mario Kart: Koopa's Challenge** (AR ride),
  **Yoshi's Adventure** (gentle, great for a 6-year-old), the interactive **Power-Up Band** coin/
  key game, the Donkey Kong Country expansion, and Toadstool Café. The single biggest Mario
  experience on Earth and an enormous Aslan + Galiya magnet. **[KID]**
- **Fit:** USJ is in **Osaka (Konohana ward)** — reachable on an **Osaka bookend day** (the trip
  already has Osaka on Days 1, 21, and the Day-22 buffer). **But it is a whole-day theme park, not
  a quick stop.**
- **Ticketing (critical):**
  - Buy the **USJ Studio Pass online in advance** (dated).
  - **Super Nintendo World usually requires a timed-entry / Area Ticket** on busy days (weekends,
    holidays, peak seasons) — obtained free via the **USJ app** on the day, **or guaranteed** by
    buying an **Express Pass** (e.g. Express Pass 7) that includes SNW timed entry. On peak dates
    the app's free timed-entry runs out early; the Express Pass removes that risk.
  - **2026 is USJ's 25th-anniversary ("Discover U") year — expect heavy crowds all year**, which
    strengthens the case for an Express Pass on any 2026 date.
  - Hours typically ~9:00–21:00 (can open 8:00 / close later on peak dates) — check the official
    dated calendar.
- **Planning implication / suggestion:** treat USJ like Naoshima — a **+1-night Osaka decision**,
  not a casual add-on. If the family wants it, build a dedicated Osaka USJ day (easiest as an extra
  night on the Day-1 front end before the bikes, or the Day-21/22 back end). Flag in the README.
- Sources: https://www.usj.co.jp/web/en/us · https://www.kkday.com/en/blog/87038/universal-studios-japan-guide ·
  https://deeparrival.com/theme-parks/universal-studios-japan/park-hours/

## Nintendo Osaka — the flagship store **[OSAKA BOOKEND: Days 1, 21]**
- **What & why it fits:** the official **Nintendo OSAKA** store — Japan's Kansai flagship Nintendo
  shop, packed with Mario/Zelda/Splatoon/Animal Crossing merch, exclusive goods and big in-store
  displays. A guaranteed hit for Aslan (toys/souvenirs) and fun for Galiya — **no ticket needed**,
  just walk in.
- **Where (corrected):** **13th floor of Daimaru Umeda** (next to JR Osaka/Umeda Station) — **not**
  Shinsaibashi. Open **daily ~10:00–20:00**. *(The same building's 13F also has the Pokémon Center
  Osaka and a Pokémon café-style outlet — see below — making one Umeda stop a triple win.)*
- **Fit:** drops straight into an Osaka bookend afternoon (Day 1 or 21). **Strong README suggestion**
  to name explicitly in the Osaka day notes.
- Sources: https://railway-toku.jp/en/nintendo-osaka-daimaru/ · https://www.tripadvisor.com/Attraction_Review-g298566-d27133918-Reviews-Osaka_Nintendo-Osaka

## Pokémon Center Osaka **[OSAKA BOOKEND: Days 1, 21]**
- **What:** one of Kansai's largest **Pokémon Centers** — plush, cards, exclusive goods; a giant
  store with photo-op figures. Pokémon overlaps Aslan's Nintendo/character love perfectly.
- **Where:** also **13F Daimaru Umeda** (~5 min walk from JR Osaka Station), open **daily
  ~10:00–20:00** — i.e. **same building and floor as Nintendo Osaka.** One elevator ride = both.
- **Pokémon Café:** Osaka has had a character café in the same complex (reservation-based) — confirm
  current status/booking if you want the food experience. There's also a **Pokémon Center Osaka DX**
  (a separate, larger location in central Osaka) — verify which is operating before you go.
- Sources: https://www.pokemon.co.jp/shop/en/pokecen/osaka/ · https://osaka-info.jp/en/osaka/pop-culture/pop-culture-pokemoncenter-osaka/

## Retro games & arcades — Den Den Town **[OSAKA BOOKEND: Nipponbashi]**
- **What:** **Den Den Town (Nipponbashi)** is Osaka's electronics/otaku district (the Kansai answer
  to Akihabara). For Nintendo/Mario specifically:
  - **Super Potato** — the famous **retro-game shop** with a Mario-statue storefront, classic
    Famicom/Super Famicom/N64 carts and consoles — a nostalgia hit and very kid-pleasing. **[KID]**
  - Arcades and **gachapon (capsule-toy)** floors all over the district — cheap, fun toy spins for
    Aslan.
- **Fit:** an Osaka bookend stroll; pairs with the toys/figures browse (see
  [toys-anime-transformers.md](toys-anime-transformers.md)) and **Kuromon Ichiba** food market
  nearby. *Confirm Super Potato's current Osaka branch hours before relying.*
- Source: https://www.kansaibeyond.com/ (Den Den Town guides) · https://nintendo.fandom.com/wiki/Nintendo_Osaka

## Off-route / bookend (awareness only)
- **Nintendo Tokyo** (flagship, Shibuya PARCO) & **Pokémon Center Mega Tokyo / Pokémon Café Tokyo**
  — **[TOKYO BOOKEND]** options on the Day-0 or Day-22/23 Tokyo nights.
- **Nintendo Museum** (Uji, Kyoto Prefecture) — Nintendo's official museum of its history/products.
  **[NEAR-ROUTE-ISH / BOOKEND]:** Uji is near Kyoto, which sits on the Day-22 Shinkansen corridor —
  *but it is **reservation-only by advance lottery/timed ticket** and sells out far ahead.* If the
  family wants it, it's a deliberate Kyoto-area side trip with tickets booked well in advance, not a
  drop-in. **Verify current ticketing before counting on it.**

## Quick guidance for this family
- **Osaka is the jackpot:** **Nintendo Osaka + Pokémon Center on one floor of Daimaru Umeda** is a
  no-ticket, walk-in **double/triple win** that slots into Day 1 or Day 21 — make it an explicit
  Osaka-day note.
- **Super Nintendo World** is the dream but is a **full-day + timed-entry/Express-Pass** commitment
  — treat it as a +1-night Osaka decision (flag like Naoshima/Ghibli Park).
- **Super Potato (Den Den Town)** is the easy bonus for retro-Mario joy + cheap gachapon toys.
- **Nintendo Museum (Uji)** is tempting on the Kyoto corridor but needs advance reservations —
  don't assume walk-in.
