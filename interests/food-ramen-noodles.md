# Food — ramen & noodles, sushi, regional cuisine & markets

**Whose interest:** **Galiya** is the big foodie. Her favourites are **ramen and noodles**
(ramen, udon, soba) and **sushi**, plus regional specialties eaten where they're made and market
food halls. Good food is a first-order reason to choose a stop. Aslan also loves market browsing
and takoyaki-style street food.

## Ramen & noodles on the loop (the headline interest)

### Wakayama ramen (和歌山ラーメン / "chuka soba") **[ON-ROUTE: Days 2–8 / Wakayama]**
- **What:** a Wakayama-prefecture soy-and-pork-bone (shōyu-tonkotsu) style with **thin straight
  noodles**, blending western-Japan tonkotsu with eastern soy broth. Classic side: **haya-zushi**
  (pressed mackerel sushi) eaten while you wait — a noodle-*and*-sushi combo that suits Galiya.
- **Where on route:** the loop runs the length of Wakayama (Kōyasan → Kumano → Shirahama,
  Days 2–8). Best authentic bowls are in **Wakayama City** (passed Day 8 en route to the ferry)
  and around Tanabe/Shirahama. Pair the Day-8 Wakayama-port morning with a chuka-soba stop.
- Source: https://www.foodinjapan.org/kansai/wakayama/wakayama-ramen/

### Tokushima ramen (徳島ラーメン) **[ON-ROUTE: Day 8–9 / Tokushima]**
- **What:** dark soy/tonkotsu broth, often **topped with sweet-savoury pork belly and a raw egg**
  that poaches in the hot soup. A distinctive, hearty regional bowl.
- **Where on route:** Tokushima City and the Naruto/Tokushima area (Day 8 landing, Day 9 start).
  The Day-9 Tokushima riverside coffee/leg-stretch stop is a natural lunch tie-in.
- Source: https://www.japan.travel/en/japan-magazine/2201_from-tokushima-ramen-to-sanuki-udon-deep-inside-shikokus-noodle/

### Sanuki udon (讃岐うどん) — Kagawa **[NEAR-ROUTE: detour from the Shikoku legs]**
- **What:** **Japan's most famous udon** — square-edged, intensely chewy/al-dente noodles, often
  served self-serve cafeteria-style with a niboshi (dried-sardine) dashi. Kagawa is the "udon
  prefecture." This is arguably the single biggest *noodle* magnet in the country for Galiya.
- **Where:** Kagawa (Takamatsu / Kotohira area) — **not on the current route**, which stays in
  Tokushima/Iya/Kōchi/Ehime. A self-serve udon stop near **Kotohira** or **Takamatsu** is a
  feasible eastern detour off a Shikoku transfer day, or pairs with a Naoshima/Noguchi add-on.
- **Suggestion flag:** strong candidate to weave in later (see README suggestion #1) — high
  interest payoff, no extra night needed if routed onto an existing Shikoku day.
- Source: https://gurunavi.com/en/japanfoodie/2016/04/shikoku.html

### Onomichi ramen (尾道ラーメン) **[ON-ROUTE: Days 16–18 / Onomichi]**
- **What:** Seto-Inland-Sea soy-base ramen with **flat noodles**, pork back-fat and small-fish
  (niboshi) depth. Onomichi is a celebrated ramen town and the broth uses local Setouchi fish.
- **Where on route:** **Onomichi** is the Days 16–17 rest base and the Day-18 departure point —
  ideal for a sit-down ramen lunch or dinner. A perfect rest-day food activity.
- Source: https://japantravel.navitime.com/en/area/jp/destinations/A0634/

### Susaki nabeyaki-ramen **[ON-ROUTE: Day 12 / Susaki]**
- The Day-12 Susaki coffee stop is the home of **nabeyaki ramen** (served bubbling in a small
  earthenware/metal pot) — a quirky local noodle variant worth a quick try if timing allows.

### Soba note
- No single famous soba region sits on this loop (Japan's soba meccas — Nagano, Izumo, Tōhoku —
  are off-route). Good soba is still widely available; flag it opportunistically rather than
  routing for it.

## Sushi & seafood on the loop

### Kōchi — katsuo no tataki & Hirome Market **[ON-ROUTE: Day 11 / Kōchi]**
- **Katsuo no tataki** (seared skipjack tuna/bonito, straw-flame-seared) is **Kōchi's signature
  dish** — one of Japan's great regional foods. Eat it at **Hirome Market** (ひろめ市場), a lively
  covered food hall of stalls where you assemble a meal and share tables — great for the family,
  and already a Day-11 POI. A top foodie + kid + market hit in one. **[KID]**
- Source: https://gurunavi.com/en/japanfoodie/2016/04/shikoku.html

### Uwajima taimeshi **[ON-ROUTE: Day 14 / Uwajima]**
- **Taimeshi** — fresh sea-bream sashimi over rice with a raw-egg-and-soy sauce (Uwajima style) —
  already the Day-14 lunch. A regional sushi-adjacent specialty eaten where it's made.

### Setouchi seafood & oysters **[ON-ROUTE: Days 16–19 / Setouchi]**
- The Inland Sea is rich seafood country — sea bream, anago (conger eel), and oysters in season.
  Setoda, Onomichi and the Shimanami islands all serve it fresh.

### Tuna at the markets **[ON-ROUTE: Day 6 / Shirahama]**
- **Tore-Tore Ichiba (とれとれ市場)** near Shirahama (already a Day-6 stop) is a giant seafood
  market famous for **maguro (tuna)** — buy sashimi/sushi and eat on-site. Katsuura (Day 5) is a
  major tuna port too.

## Osaka — the food capital itself **[ON-ROUTE: Days 1, 21; bookend Day 22]**
Osaka is *kuidaore* ("eat yourself broke") — the loop's anchor city is also its food capital:
- **Takoyaki** (octopus balls) and **okonomiyaki** (savoury pancake) — Osaka's defining street
  foods, all over Dōtonbori. **[KID]**
- **Kushikatsu** (deep-fried skewers, "no double-dipping") — Shinsekai district.
- **Kuromon Ichiba** for fresh seafood/wagyu (above).
- A relaxed first-night and last-night food crawl is built into Days 1 and 21 already.

## Off-route famous noodle/food regions (awareness only)
- **Hakata tonkotsu ramen** (Fukuoka, Kyushu) — off-route.
- **Sapporo miso ramen** (Hokkaidō) — off-route.
- **Izumo / Nagano soba** — off-route.
- **Toyama / Kanazawa sushi** (Hokuriku) — off-route.
These are listed so the family knows the loop trades national breadth for Kansai/Shikoku/Setouchi
depth — but the on-route noodle line-up (Wakayama → Tokushima → (Sanuki near-route) → Onomichi)
is genuinely strong for a noodle lover.
