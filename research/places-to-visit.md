# Places to Visit — coast, volcano & mountain highlights (PNW first tour)

The must-see stops along the 7-day loop, in route order. **[KID]** marks stops with a clear hook
for Aslan (6); **[FOOD]** marks Galiya's coast-food picks (full detail in
[`../interests/food-coast-seafood.md`](../interests/food-coast-seafood.md)). Everything here sits
**on or just off the planned route** — no detours that break the new-rider day caps. Re-confirm
hours/closures before relying on any single one.

---

## Day 1 — Hood Canal to Westport (WA coast)

- **Edmonds–Kingston ferry** — a Puget Sound crossing as the trip's opener; Olympics on the horizon, bikes load first. [KID]
- **Hood Canal shore (WA-106)** — quiet fjord-side spots for a leg-stretch and photos.
- **Westport** — working fishing-and-surf town. **Westport Maritime Museum** and the **Grays Harbor Lighthouse** (tallest in Washington); sea lions and the fishing fleet on the marina docks. [KID]
- **Fresh Dungeness crab off the dock** — the first link of Galiya's Coast Food Trail. [FOOD]
- Refs: https://www.westportwa.com/ · https://westportmaritimemuseum.com/

## Day 2 — Across the Columbia to Astoria (OR coast)

- **Willapa Bay / Raymond** — oyster country and the quirky roadside metal "Wildlife Heritage" sculptures. [KID]
- **Long Beach Peninsula** — the "world's longest beach," boardwalk, kite shops and the **World Kite Museum**. [KID]
- **Astoria–Megler Bridge** — the soaring 4.1-mile crossing into Oregon.
- **Astoria** — oldest American settlement west of the Rockies. **The Astoria Column** (climb it, fly balsa gliders from the top), **Columbia River Maritime Museum**, the riverfront trolley, sea lions barking on the docks, and *Goonies* filming locations around town. [KID]
- **Bowpicker fish & chips** — legendary albacore, served from a converted gillnet boat. [FOOD]
- Refs: https://www.travelastoria.com/ · https://astoriacolumn.org/ · https://www.crmm.org/

## Day 3 — Capes, Cheese & Haystack Rock (OR coast)

- **Cannon Beach · Haystack Rock** — the iconic 235-ft sea stack, tide pools and nesting puffins at low tide. [KID]
- **Tillamook Creamery** — free self-guided cheese-factory tour, tastings and the famous ice cream — the foodie + kid centerpiece. [KID][FOOD]
- **Tillamook Air Museum** — vintage aircraft inside a vast WWII wooden blimp hangar (see [`motorcycle-museums.md`](motorcycle-museums.md)) — the dad-and-Aslan win. [KID]
- **Three Capes Loop** — Cape Meares lighthouse + the Octopus Tree; the dory boats and big climbable dune at **Cape Kiwanda / Pacific City**. [KID]
- **Oregon Coast Aquarium, Newport (optional)** — walk-through ocean tunnel, sea otters, touch tanks if time allows. [KID]
- Refs: https://www.tillamook.com/creamery · https://tillamookair.com/ · https://aquarium.org/

## Day 4 — Yachats rest day (OR coast)

- **La De Da Parade + bay fireworks** — Yachats' beloved come-as-you-are Fourth of July. [KID]
- **Cape Perpetua Scenic Area** — **Thor's Well** ("drainpipe of the Pacific"), the Spouting Horn and Devil's Churn; the highest viewpoint on the Oregon coast. *(Keep Aslan well back from the wet rocks — they're dangerous.)*
- **Heceta Head Lighthouse** — one of the most photographed lighthouses in the U.S.
- **Sea Lion Caves** — an elevator down into America's largest sea cave, full of wild Steller sea lions. [KID]
- **804 Trail & tidepools** — flat oceanfront stroll and low-tide tidepooling right from the village. [KID]
- **Clam chowder & Dungeness crab** at the rest base (Luna Sea; Ona for the splurge). [FOOD]
- Refs: https://www.fs.usda.gov/recarea/siuslaw/recarea/?recid=42283 · https://oregonstateparks.org/index.cfm?do=park.profile&parkId=110 · https://www.sealioncaves.com/

## Day 5 — Coast to Volcano (Cascades)

- **Alsea River road (OR-34)** — the scenic Coast Range crossing; **Alsea Falls** is a short side option.
- **Corvallis** — valley lunch, fuel and a leg-stretch.
- **Mount St. Helens Visitor Center at Silver Lake (SR-504)** — always-open, hands-on eruption story with a walk-in volcano model and the May 18, 1980 timeline — Aslan's kind of place. [KID]
- Refs: https://parks.wa.gov/find-parks/state-parks/seaquest-state-park/mount-st-helens-visitor-center · https://www.fs.usda.gov/mountsthelens

## Day 6 — The Blast Zone & US-12 (Cascades)

- **Spirit Lake Highway (SR-504) viewpoints** — sweeping crater views from the pull-offs up the blast zone (upper road closed since 2023 — see scenic-routes.md).
- **Hoffstadt Bluffs** — valley overlook, eruption photo displays, leg-stretch.
- **Coldwater Lake** — a lake *born* of the eruption; the flat, easy "Birth of a Lake" boardwalk. [KID]
- **Packwood** — roaming elk and deer on the town lawns at dusk; quiet pre-finale evening. [KID]
- Refs: https://www.fs.usda.gov/mountsthelens · https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Coldwater_Lake

## Day 7 — Paradise & Chinook Pass home (Cascades)

- **Paradise (Mount Rainier NP)** — subalpine wildflower meadows in early July; the short, mostly-paved walk to **Myrtle Falls** framed by the mountain; the historic Paradise Inn and visitor center. [KID]
- **Reflection Lakes** — roadside tarns that mirror the whole mountain on a calm morning.
- **Narada Falls** — a 168-ft tiered waterfall right beside the road, often with a rainbow. [KID]
- **Chinook Pass & Tipsoo Lake (SR-410)** — the alpine high point at 5,430 ft and the new rider's graduation moment (confirm the pass is open). [KID]
- **Wildlife** — marmots, deer and chipmunks at Paradise/Tipsoo; lingering roadside snowbanks Aslan can touch in July. [KID]
- Refs: https://www.nps.gov/mora/planyourvisit/the-road-to-paradise.htm
