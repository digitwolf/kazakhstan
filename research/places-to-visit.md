# Best Places & Regions to Visit — Honshu + Shikoku + Kansai Loop

Reference of must-see towns, castles, onsen, coasts and national parks along a
Tokyo/Yokohama → Honshu → Shikoku → Osaka/Kansai route. **[KID]** flags places that are
especially good with a child pillion / family.

---

## Kantō departure region (Tokyo / Yokohama / Mt. Fuji)

- **Hakone** — onsen resort town, Lake Ashi, Owakudani volcanic valley, Fuji views, pirate-ship cruise, open-air sculpture museum. **[KID]** Excellent first/last stop; very family-friendly. Increasingly tattoo-tolerant onsen area.
- **Mt. Fuji Five Lakes (Fujigoko)** — Lake Kawaguchiko/Yamanakako for classic Fuji reflections, Fuji-Q area. **[KID]**
- **Izu Peninsula** — coastal onsen towns (Atami, Itō, Shimoda), Jōgasaki coast, Pacific beaches; pairs with the Izu Skyline ride. **[KID]** beaches + onsen.
- **Nikko** (north of Tokyo) — Toshogu Shrine (UNESCO), Lake Chūzenji, Kegon Falls, Irohazaka. Strong autumn-foliage destination. **[KID]** waterfalls + shrines.
- Source: https://en.japantravel.com/guide/autumn-in-japan-your-seasonal-guide/71788

---

## Central Honshu / Japan Alps (if routing inland)

- **Matsumoto** — Matsumoto Castle ("Crow Castle"), one of Japan's finest original keeps. **[KID]**
- **Kamikōchi / Norikura / Kiso Valley (Nakasendō post towns: Magome–Tsumago)** — alpine scenery and preserved Edo-era streets. **[KID]** the post-town walks.
- **Kusatsu Onsen** — one of Japan's top hot-spring towns (the *yubatake* hot-water field).
- Season/closure context: https://www.japan-guide.com/e/e2273.html

---

## Chūgoku / Setouchi (the Honshu→Shikoku bridge corridor)

- **Onomichi** — atmospheric hillside port town; Honshu end of the Shimanami Kaido; temple walk, cats, ramen. **[KID]**
- **Hiroshima & Miyajima (Itsukushima)** — Peace Memorial; the "floating" torii of Itsukushima Shrine; tame deer on Miyajima. **[KID]** deer + ferry.
- **Kurashiki (Bikan district)** — preserved canal-side merchant quarter. **[KID]** boat rides.
- **Mt. Daisen (Tottori)** — sacred volcano, hiking, autumn colour; Tottori Sand Dunes nearby. **[KID]** dunes.
- Source: https://www.japan-guide.com/e/e3478.html

## Shikoku

Note: attractions are scattered and mountain roads slow — plan 2–3 bases, not one.
(https://www.japanhighlights.com/japan/shikoku/travel-map)

- **Shimanami Kaido** — the island-hopping bridge route itself is a top attraction (see scenic-routes.md). **[KID]**
- **Matsuyama (Ehime)** — **Dōgo Onsen**, said to be Japan's oldest hot spring (1,000+ years; the Honkan bathhouse inspired *Spirited Away* imagery), nostalgic Dōgo Haikara street; **Matsuyama Castle** (original keep, hilltop, ropeway). **[KID]** castle ropeway + onsen town.
- **Iya Valley (Tokushima)** — dramatic remote gorge; **Iya-no-Kazurabashi** vine suspension bridge; Oboke/Koboke gorge river cruises; thatched-roof scarecrow village (Nagoro). **[KID]** vine bridge is a thrill (older kids).
- **Naruto Whirlpools (Tokushima)** — one of the world's largest tidal whirlpools, seen from sightseeing boats or the Uzunomichi walkway under the Ōnaruto Bridge. **[KID]** boat trip.
- **Kotohira-gū (Konpira-san) (Kagawa)** — hilltop shrine reached by 1,368 stone steps; panoramic views. (Strenuous climb — palanquin historically; optional with a child.)
- **Kōchi** — Kōchi Castle (original keep), Katsurahama beach; gateway to Niyodo Blue, Shimanto River and Capes Muroto/Ashizuri. **[KID]** castle + beach.
- **Takamatsu (Kagawa)** — Ritsurin Garden (one of Japan's finest landscape gardens); Sanuki udon. **[KID]** garden + boat.
- **Art islands (Naoshima/Teshima)** — Setouchi contemporary-art islands, reachable by ferry from Takamatsu/Uno. **[KID]** outdoor art (Yayoi Kusama pumpkins). _Fit-to-loop feasibility (zero added days vs +1 night, ferry, half-day budget, Monday closures) is worked out in **[naoshima-feasibility.md](naoshima-feasibility.md)** — documented, not yet in the plan._
- Sources: https://www.japan.travel/en/my/the-latest-itinerary/shikoku-itinerary-iya-valley-dogo-onsen/ · https://www.lonelyplanet.com/articles/top-things-to-do-in-shikoku

## Kansai (Osaka / Kyoto / Nara / Hyōgo / Wakayama / Mie)

### Cities & culture
- **Kyoto** — Fushimi Inari (thousand torii), Kinkaku-ji, Arashiyama bamboo grove & monkey park, Gion. **[KID]** Inari hike, monkey park, bamboo grove.
- **Osaka** — Osaka Castle & park, Dōtonbori, Kaiyukan Aquarium, **Universal Studios Japan**. **[KID]** USJ + aquarium are top family draws.
- **Nara** — Nara Park free-roaming (bow-happy) deer, Tōdai-ji Great Buddha. **[KID]** the deer are a classic kid highlight.
- **Himeji** — **Himeji Castle**, Japan's finest original castle (UNESCO, "White Heron"), ~15 min walk from Himeji Station; adjacent Kōko-en garden. **[KID]**
- **Kobe** — harbour city, Mt. Rokkō, Nada sake district.
- **Kawasaki Good Times World (Kobe)** — Kawasaki Heavy Industries' museum inside the Kobe Maritime Museum at **Meriken Park**, a short walk from Kobe Harborland. Sit-on motorcycle gallery, jet-skis, a riding simulator, Shinkansen cab, helicopter and robots. **[KID]** **The one major corporate motorcycle museum that sits on the Osaka-anchored loop** (Honda/Motegi, Yamaha/Iwata, Suzuki/Hamamatsu are all Tōkai/Kantō, off-route). *Closed Mondays (next day if Mon is a holiday) + Dec 29–Jan 3; ~10:00–18:00, last entry 17:30; ¥900 adult / ¥400 school-age / free under 6; paid Meriken Park parking.* Sources: https://www.khi.co.jp/kawasakiworld/english/ · https://www.khi.co.jp/kawasakiworld/english/how/
- Sources: https://livejapan.com/en/in-kansai/in-pref-osaka/in-usj_nanko-port/article-a2000786/ · https://www.thebackpackadventures.com/2019/07/10-kid-friendly-places-in-kansai-region.html

### Onsen
- **Arima Onsen (Hyōgo)** — one of Japan's oldest hot-spring towns, easy from Kobe/Osaka. **[KID]**
- **Shirahama Onsen (Wakayama)** — Pacific resort with white-sand beach, Sakino-yu seaside open-air bath, Sandanbeki cliffs, Adventure World (pandas + safari). **[KID]** beach + animal park.
- **Katsuura / Yunomine / Kawayu (Wakayama)** — onsen along the Kumano Kodō; Kawayu lets you dig your own riverbed bath. **[KID]**
- Sources: https://www.thebackpackadventures.com/2019/07/10-kid-friendly-places-in-kansai-region.html · https://www.japan-experience.com/all-about-japan/kumano/attractions-excursions/kii-peninsula

### Kii Peninsula coast & sacred sites (great Kansai loop add-on)
- **Kumano Kodō / Kumano Sanzan** — UNESCO pilgrimage trails and grand shrines (Hongū, Hayatama, Nachi). Short family-friendly walk options. **[KID]** (short sections)
- **Nachi Falls** — Japan's tallest single-drop waterfall (133 m), beside Nachi Taisha and the iconic three-storied pagoda. **[KID]**
- **Ise (Mie)** — Ise Grand Shrine (Jingū), Japan's most sacred Shinto site; Okage Yokochō old street.
- **Ise-Shima / Toba** — Mikimoto Pearl Island, Meoto-iwa "wedded rocks," and the **Pearl Road** (~24 km scenic coast drive Toba↔Shima with sea lookouts). **[KID]** aquarium (Toba) + pearls.
- **Wakayama Castle** — castle keep with artifacts and a small free zoo nearby. **[KID]**
- The Kii coast (Mie + Wakayama, ~350 km around) gives cliffs, coves and Pacific views — keep to the coastline for the best scenery.
- Sources: https://www.japan-experience.com/all-about-japan/kumano/attractions-excursions/kii-peninsula · https://biketourjapan.com/cycling-the-kii-peninsula-japans-pacific-coast-cycling-road-mie-wakayama-800-and-nara/

## National parks touched by this loop
- **Fuji-Hakone-Izu National Park** (start region) — Fuji, Hakone calderas, Izu coast/islands. **[KID]**
- **Setonaikai (Seto Inland Sea) National Park** — Japan's first national park; the Shimanami/Setouchi island seascape. **[KID]**
- **Yoshino-Kumano National Park** (Kii Peninsula) — sacred mountains, Kumano coast, Nachi Falls.
- **Chūbu-Sangaku (Northern Alps)** — if routing inland through Nagano/Gifu.
