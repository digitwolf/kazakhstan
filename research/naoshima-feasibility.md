# Naoshima art island — feasibility for the loop (documented option, NOT yet in the plan)

Captured for a later decision. The user asked whether **Naoshima** (Setouchi contemporary-art
island — Yayoi Kusama pumpkins, Benesse House, Chichū/Lee Ufan museums, Art House Project)
can be added **without adding nights**, whether it needs a dedicated day, and whether it
requires a long 5–6 h push. Verified June 2026; **reconfirm ferry times, reservations and
closed days before relying on them.** No itinerary change has been made.

## TL;DR
- **It is NOT a long push and NOT a whole-day ride.** From **Kurashiki (already the Day-18 base)**
  the correct port — **Uno Port (Tamano)** — is a **~40-min flat ride**, then a **~20-min ferry**.
  The "5–6 h push" only applies to the wrong port (Takamatsu, back on Shikoku = backtracking).
- **A satisfying visit is a half-day (~4–5 h on-island)**, not a dedicated day — *if* you skip or
  pre-book Chichū.
- **Leave the bikes parked at Uno; cross as foot passengers.** The island is toured by shuttle /
  rental bike; taking a big bike on/off a small ferry twice buys nothing.
- **Zero added days IS possible — but not free.** The only sound no-night method (base-shift, below)
  forces dropping **Bizen-yaki pottery and/or Kawasaki Good Times World** and compressing Himeji on
  Day 19. The clean alternative is **+1 night near Uno**, which is outside the "no added days" brief.
- **Monday trap (critical):** Chichū, Lee Ufan, Benesse House and the Art House Project are all
  **closed Mondays** — a Monday visit closes essentially everything except the two outdoor pumpkins.

## Ferries & geography
| Route | Crossing | Frequency | Bikes? |
|---|---|---|---|
| **Uno Port (Tamano, Honshū) → Miyanoura** | **~20 min** car ferry (+ passenger boat) | **~12/day**, ~06:10–20:25 | Yes (car ferry), but **park & walk on** instead |
| Takamatsu (Shikoku) → Miyanoura | ~50 min car ferry / 25-min fast boat | several/day | Car ferry yes; fast boat passenger-only |

- **Uno is the Honshū-side gateway, just south of Okayama/Kurashiki** — on the same shore the loop
  already uses (the Day-18 Washūzan / Seto-Ōhashi viewpoint looks out over this exact water).
- Distances to **Uno Port**: **Kurashiki ~25 km / 35–45 min**; Okayama ~30–40 min;
  **Onomichi/Setoda ~120 km / 1h45m+ (wrong base).**

## Time budget
- **Half-day MVP (~3.5–4.5 h, good with a 6-yr-old):** both **Kusama pumpkins** (outdoor, free,
  the kid magnet) + **Benesse House** grounds + **one** indoor museum (Lee Ufan is the quicker pick) + lunch.
- **Full day:** Chichū + Lee Ufan + Benesse House + Art House Project (Honmura) + both pumpkins —
  fills a day mostly via timed-entry queues and shuttle waits.
- **Chichū breaks the half-day:** needs **date-and-time online reservations ~3+ weeks ahead**
  (Mar–Nov peak). A relaxed half-day means either pre-committing to one fixed slot or skipping Chichū
  (its dark, silent, no-photo galleries are largely lost on a young child anyway).

## Closures (the same trap as the other museum stops)
- Chichū, Lee Ufan, Benesse House Museum, Art House Project: **closed Mondays** (open a holiday-Monday,
  then closed the next day). Hours: Mar–Sep 10:00–18:00 (last 17:00), Oct–Feb 10:00–17:00 (last 16:00).
- Also an annual **Jan–Mar maintenance closure** (irrelevant to the Oct / late-Apr–May windows).
- **Count weekdays from the actual trip start date** to the candidate day before locking in.

## No-added-days options (honest trade-offs)
- **(a) Convert the Day-17 Onomichi/Setoda rest day in place — POOR.** Onomichi→Uno is ~1h45m each
  way; a "rest day" becomes 3.5 h of riding + ferry + island. Breaks the rest-day-stays-light rule.
- **(b) Bolt a half-day onto Day 18 (Onomichi→Kurashiki) — NO.** Day 18 is already ~90 mi / 3h15m with
  Fukuyama, Tomonoura (*Ponyo*), Washūzan, Kurashiki Bikan; inserting a ferry + island blows the
  comfort cap or guts the kid-loved Tomonoura stop.
- **(c) Base-shift — the only sound no-night method.** Sleep the **Day-18 night near Uno/Tamano**
  instead of Kurashiki (same one night, ~25 km east; keep Tomonoura + Kurashiki Bikan as Day-18
  afternoon stops). **Day-19 morning:** short hop to Uno, **park bikes**, foot-ferry, half-day MVP
  (Chichū only if pre-booked), back early afternoon, then ride on to Himeji/Kobe.
  - **Cost:** Day 19 is already full (Bizen pottery → Himeji → Kawasaki World → Kobe). The island
    morning means **dropping Bizen-yaki and/or Kawasaki World and compressing Himeji.** You trade two
    hands-on / moto-culture experiences (both strong fits for *this* family) for contemporary art.
- **(d) +1 night near Uno (outside the brief).** The clean fix: Naoshima gets its own unhurried day
  and Day 19 keeps Bizen + Kawasaki + a full Himeji.

## Recommendation
The trip-killer assumptions are both false (short ferry, half-day — not a long push or a dedicated
day). But **zero added days is not free**: the base-shift sacrifices the Day-19 craft and/or
moto-museum. For this child-paced, weather-flexible trip:
- If the family values Bizen-yaki + Kawasaki World over adult-leaning contemporary art → **+1 night**
  or **skip** Naoshima (it's more an adult art-pilgrimage than a 6-yr-old highlight).
- If they want it with no extra night → **base-shift (c)**, foot-ferry from Uno, and accept dropping
  the Day-19 stops — and ensure the chosen day is **not a Monday**.
