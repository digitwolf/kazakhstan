# Seasonal Notes — Riding Japan in October

October is one of the best months to tour Japan: warm but no longer hot/humid, clearer
skies, and decreasing rainfall versus September. Two things to plan around: **tail-end
typhoon risk** (mostly early October) and **autumn-foliage timing + early mountain-pass
closures/cold**. Sources at the bottom; verify live forecasts close to travel.

---

## Weather overview
- October is widely rated one of the most pleasant touring months — comfortable daytime temps, clearer skies, less rain than the summer/September peak. Good for outdoor sightseeing and riding.
- Source: https://planjapansmart.com/japan-in-october/ · https://www.agatetravel.com/japan/weather-in-october.html

## Typhoon risk
- **Early October is still within the active typhoon window**; risk drops markedly through the month and the season largely ends by **late October into November** (more summer-ish, lower-risk weather).
- Typhoons most affect **southern/Pacific-facing areas**: Okinawa/Kyushu first, then can track up the Pacific coast (Shikoku's south coast, Kii Peninsula, Tōkai). Shikoku and the Kii coast are the more exposed parts of this loop.
- **Planning implications for the tour:**
  - Front-load flexible / coastal-exposed days (Shikoku Pacific capes, Kii coast) toward **mid-late October** if possible.
  - **Book cancellable lodging** and check forecasts daily in the first half of the month.
  - A typhoon doesn't only bring the storm day — heavy rain + wind can close bridges (Shimanami wind limits), trigger mountain landslide closures, and leave wet roads for a day or two after.
- Sources: https://selfguidejapan.com/blog/japan-typhoon-season-2026 · https://www.japanhighlights.com/japan/typhoon-season · https://www.jrpass.com/blog/japan-s-typhoon-season-what-to-expect-and-how-to-prepare

## Autumn foliage (kōyō) timing
Foliage sweeps from north/high-elevation downward through autumn:
- **Early–mid October:** Hokkaidō and the **high mountains/passes** (Japan Alps, Norikura, Tateyama, high Tōhoku). So the high alpine roads peak *during* this trip's window — but those are also the roads closing for winter soonest.
- **Late October–early November:** mid-elevation Tōhoku, **Nikko / Irohazaka**, higher Chūbu.
- **Mid–late November:** **Tokyo, Osaka, Kyoto lowlands**; Shikoku mountains (Iya Valley) and Kii peninsula generally peak **mid–late November**.
- **Net for an October tour:** expect peak colour mainly at **altitude** (mountain passes, Nikko-area, Alps); the lowland cities and most of Shikoku/Kansai will still be green-to-early-turning. Plan high-mountain rides early in the trip to catch both foliage *and* the passes before they close.

## Mountain-pass closures & cold (the big October constraint)
Many of Japan's best high roads close for winter — several **in November**, so an October trip is near the *end* of their season. Re-check official dates yearly.
- **Snow-closure (typically mid/late Nov → Apr):** Venus Line, Norikura (also access-restricted), Shiga-Kusatsu Rt 292, UFO Line / Shikoku Karst, Bandai-Azuma Skyline, Zaō Echo Line, Hachimantai Aspite Line, Irohazaka.
- **October realities at altitude:** foggy mornings, wet roads, **early frost/ice** especially late October — dangerous 2-up. Dress the child for genuine cold (alpine roads can be near-freezing at dawn even when valleys are mild).
- **Volcanic-gas/alert closures** (independent of snow): Shiga-Kusatsu near Mt. Shirane, Aso crater roads — can shut at short notice; check active-volcano advisories.
- Sources: https://farwayout.com/top-20-best-motorcycle-roads-in-japan/ · https://www.japan-guide.com/e/e2273.html

## October ride-planning checklist
- [ ] Watch the 5–10 day typhoon outlook in **early October**; keep lodging cancellable.
- [ ] Do **high-altitude/foliage roads early** in the trip — they close for snow soonest.
- [ ] Pack genuine cold-weather layers for the child (alpine passes ≈ 0–10 °C at altitude/dawn) plus rain gear.
- [ ] Expect **lowland foliage to still be green** — set expectations; peak colour is at elevation.
- [ ] Re-check each named pass's official closure date and any volcanic-alert status before routing.
- [ ] Buffer/rest days to absorb a rained-out or wind-closed day (Shimanami bridge wind limits, landslides).
