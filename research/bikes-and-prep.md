# Bikes & Pre-Trip Prep — the family's own two machines

_There is no rental on this trip._ The family rides **their own bikes from the garage** — so the
"operator research" of a rental tour is replaced by **pre-trip mechanical prep, the child-pillion
setup, and rider-comms**. This is the get-the-bikes-ready checklist for a brand-new rider's first
multi-day tour, with a 6-year-old on the back of the second bike. Do the setup work **weeks ahead**,
not the night before, and **test the child-pillion rig parked, then on the gentle Day-1 Hood Canal
opener**, before the longer days.

---

## The two bikes

### Galiya — Kawasaki W230 (the bike the whole trip is paced to)
- **233cc air-cooled single**, classic-styled, friendly and light — happiest at **55–65 mph**, with a **~3.4-gal tank** (short range — the trip's binding fuel constraint, see [`touring-practical.md`](touring-practical.md)).
- It is **right-sized for a new rider** on no-freeway two-lanes; the whole route is chosen so the W230 is never out of its comfort zone (no Interstates, no high-speed slogs).
- **Implication:** her bike sets the group pace and the fuel cadence. Prep it to be utterly reliable and easy.

### Ruslan — BMW R1300GS (carries Aslan + the load)
- Seasoned-rider ADV bike (Ruslan's done a 2-week Morocco trip on the type). Carries **Aslan as pillion** plus the snacks, rain layers, tools and first-aid.
- It is **not** the limiting bike — but it now carries a child, so its **passenger ergonomics and luggage** are the prep focus, not its capability.

---

## 1. Pre-trip service (both bikes, weeks ahead)

Book a service or do it yourself **with time to fix anything found** — not in the last days.

- **Tires:** check tread depth, age (date code) and **set pressures** (solo for the W230; **2-up/loaded pressures** for the GS carrying Aslan + gear — usually a few psi higher rear). A fresh rear on the GS if it's marginal — you don't want to change a tire mid-tour. Coast roads can be wet; good tread matters.
- **Chain & final drive:** the **W230 is chain-driven** — clean, lube and set slack to spec; replace a stretched/worn chain & sprockets before the trip. The **GS is shaft-driven** — check final-drive oil/condition per service schedule.
- **Brakes:** pad thickness, fluid level/age, no spongy feel — important for the Day-7 mountain descents (Stevens Canyon, Narada, Chinook Pass).
- **Fluids & filters:** oil + filter, coolant on the GS, air filter; the W230 is air-cooled so watch oil condition.
- **Lights & electrics:** all lights, signals, brake light, horn; **battery health** (a weak battery is the classic trip-killer).
- **Controls & cables:** clutch/throttle free play, levers, fasteners torqued; lube cables.
- **Fuel system:** the W230's small tank means you'll fill often — make sure the cap, petcock/pump and gauge/odometer-trip are reliable so range planning is trustworthy.
- **Carry:** tire-repair plug kit + mini pump/CO2, basic tool roll, fuses, spare levers if you have them, zip ties, a quart of the right oil, first-aid kit, tow strap. Ruslan carries the shared kit on the GS.

## 2. Child-pillion setup on the GS (do this first, test it parked)

The 6-year-old rides on the GS with Ruslan. Set up and **test every item while the bike is parked**,
then confirm on the gentle Day-1 Hood Canal stretch before the longer days. (Mirrors `tour/00-overview.md`.)

- **Footpeg reach.** A 6-year-old's legs may not reach the stock passenger pegs — fit **lowered/highway pegs or peg-lowering brackets** so his feet sit flat and secure; **dangling feet get tired and slip.** This is the #1 fit issue on a tall GS.
- **Backrest & grab points.** Add a **passenger backrest** so he can't slide rearward, plus a **proper grab rail or grab strap at the waist** — don't rely on him gripping the rider.
- **Tandem riding belt.** Use a **child–adult tandem safety belt** so a tired or dozing child can't lean away from the bike.
- **Seat & comfort.** A comfortable passenger seat / pad; he's on it for hours over a week.
- **Gear (his own, fitted).** A **properly-fitted child helmet** (correct shell size — *not* an adult hand-me-down), armoured jacket, gloves, boots; **ear protection** helps on longer legs; a **warm + waterproof layer** for the cold coast mornings and the Day-7 alpine pass (kids chill fast — see [`touring-practical.md`](touring-practical.md)).
- **Pace & limits.** Stops every **60–90 min**; watch for him going quiet/fidgety (early fatigue). Keep the long Day-5 transfer steady and in short hops, and **never two long days back-to-back** — exactly why the Fourth-of-July rest day sits between the coast days and the inland transfer.

## 3. Rider comms (intercoms)

- **Pair both helmets' intercoms before Day 1** and test them at speed on a short ride. For a new rider, **continuous voice contact is a safety and confidence multiplier** — Ruslan can coach Galiya through unfamiliar moments (the ferry roll-on, the Astoria Bridge wind, the Chinook switchbacks) and they can manage pace, fuel stops and Aslan's breaks in real time.
- Include **Aslan in the intercom** if his helmet supports it (or a simple listen-only setup) so he can say when he needs a stop and stay engaged — a big comfort and safety win for a 6-year-old.
- Charge all units the night before each riding day; carry chargers.

## 4. Luggage & load (mostly the GS)

- Pack **light on the W230** — keep it nimble and unintimidating for the new rider; put the bulk (tools, first-aid, rain layers, shared kit) on the GS.
- **Distribute weight low and forward** on the GS and re-check 2-up tire pressures with the load on.
- **Secure parking nightly:** every lodging must be confirmed for **secure motorcycle parking + passenger/child rules** before booking ("confirm before booking" — see `tour/00-family.md`). Bring **disc locks/covers** for peace of mind.

## 5. Documents & legal (per bike / rider)

- Valid **WA/OR motorcycle endorsement** (Galiya's newly earned), current **registration + insurance** on both bikes, carried on each bike.
- **No passport, IDP, visa or rental paperwork** — own bikes, domestic loop (see [`touring-practical.md`](touring-practical.md)).
- **Mount Rainier timed-entry reservation** booked ahead for Day 7 (see touring-practical.md).

## Pre-departure quick checklist

- [ ] Both bikes serviced (tires, **W230 chain**, brakes, fluids, lights, battery) with time to fix issues
- [ ] 2-up/loaded tire pressures set on the GS
- [ ] Child-pillion rig fitted & **tested parked**: footpegs, backrest, grab strap, tandem belt
- [ ] Aslan's fitted helmet + armoured gear + **warm/waterproof layer**
- [ ] Intercoms paired & tested (rider-to-rider, ideally include Aslan)
- [ ] Tool roll, tire-repair kit + pump, first-aid, spare oil on the GS
- [ ] Light pack on the W230; load balanced on the GS
- [ ] Endorsement / registration / insurance on each bike
- [ ] Mount Rainier timed-entry reservation booked (Day 7)
- [ ] Test the whole setup on the **Day-1 Hood Canal** opener before committing to the longer days
