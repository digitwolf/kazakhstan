# Motorcycle Rental in Japan — Operator Research & Start-Location Verification

_Research date: 2026-06-05. For the 21-day family motorcycle tour (2 bikes: newer rider + child pillion).
Current site plan assumes: **Honda Africa Twin (with child pillion) + Honda CB400X**, picked up near
**Tokyo/Yokohama**, one-way drop in **Kansai/Osaka**. This document verifies whether that is realistic._

## TL;DR / Recommendation

- **The "pick up in Tokyo, one-way drop in Osaka on big bikes" assumption is the weak link.** The major
  reputable chains do **not** offer one-way large-bike drop-off: Rental819 (Japan's biggest network) is
  **explicitly loop-only**, and Japan Bike Rentals (Tokyo/Yamanashi) is loop-only too. A true one-way
  Tokyo→Osaka on an Africa Twin is essentially **not a standard product**.
- **Best single-operator fit = [Bike Rental Japan](https://bikerentaljapan.com/) (Osaka / Suita).** It is the
  only operator confirmed to stock **both** planned bikes — the **Honda Africa Twin CRF1000L** *and* the
  **Honda CB400X (6 units)** — from one counter, and it is the operator most associated with flexible
  point-to-point / delivery arrangements. **But it has a single base in Osaka, not Tokyo.**
- **Correct start/end location (verdict):** Based on real rental availability, the operationally correct base
  is **Osaka**, not Tokyo. Two clean ways to honour that:
  1. **Osaka-anchored loop** (start and end Osaka) — simplest, cheapest, no one-way fee. Most of the existing
     route (Days 5–21: Awaji, Shikoku, Setouchi, Himeji/Kobe) already clusters around Osaka; only Days 1–4
     (Tokyo/Hakone/Izu/Fuji) sit far east.
  2. **Keep the Tokyo→Osaka geographic flow but pay Bike Rental Japan to deliver the bikes to Tokyo and
     collect them in Osaka** (delivery/one-way is fee-based and **must be quoted and confirmed in writing**).
- **Loop vs one-way verdict:** Plan it as a **loop (or delivery-assisted one-way), not an off-the-shelf
  one-way rental.** If you keep a Tokyo base, you'd have to use Rental819 and ride **back** to Tokyo (loop),
  which conflicts with the current Osaka ending.
- **Critical rider-eligibility flag:** A "newer rider" may legally be **barred from carrying the child
  pillion**. Japan requires the licence to have been held **≥1 year to carry any passenger on ordinary
  roads, and ≥3 years to carry a passenger on expressways** (bike must be >125cc — both planned bikes pass).
  Confirm the rider's licence tenure before committing to the child-pillion plan.

### Cross-operator rules (apply to all)

- **IDP:** Japan only accepts the **1949 Geneva Convention** IDP, **physically printed**, **stamped category
  "A" for motorcycles**, carried with the home licence. The **1968 Vienna Convention IDP is NOT valid in
  Japan.** (Some EU countries + Taiwan/Switzerland use an official Japanese translation instead.) Sources:
  bikerentaljapan.com/license, livejapan.com, mototoursjapan.com.
- **Carrying a passenger (the child-pillion question):** Japanese law sets **no explicit minimum passenger
  age**, but:
  - The bike must be **built for two** (rear footpegs) and the **passenger must be able to reach the
    footpegs** — a real concern for a small child on a **tall Africa Twin**. ⚠️
  - **Ordinary roads:** rider must have held the licence **≥1 year**, bike >50cc.
  - **Expressways:** rider **≥20**, licence held **≥3 years**, bike **>125cc**.
  - Both planned bikes clear the displacement bars; the binding constraint is the **rider's licence tenure**
    and the child's **size/footpeg reach**. Sources: livejapan.com, JAF, japan-guide.com, theninjarider.com.

## 3. Why the current "Tokyo pickup → Osaka one-way drop" plan needs revising

1. **The chosen/flagged operator (Bike Rental Japan) is in Osaka, not Tokyo.** There is no Tokyo counter to
   pick up from. So either the bikes start in Osaka, or BRJ must **deliver** them to Tokyo for a fee.
2. **The big reputable chain that *does* have Tokyo branches (Rental819) refuses one-way** — bikes must come
   back to the originating shop. So a Tokyo pickup with a Rental819 forces a **loop back to Tokyo**, which
   contradicts the current Osaka ending.
3. **Net:** an off-the-shelf one-way **Tokyo→Osaka large-bike** rental basically doesn't exist among the
   reputable operators. Realistic structures are: **(a) Osaka loop**, or **(b) BRJ delivery-assisted
   one-way** (confirm cost), or **(c) Tokyo loop via Rental819** (return to Tokyo).

## 4. Recommendation

**Primary:** Use **Bike Rental Japan (Osaka)** — it's the only operator confirmed to supply **both exact
bikes** (Africa Twin CRF1000L + CB400X) from one counter and is the most touring/point-to-point friendly.
- **If they confirm (in writing) a reasonable delivery/one-way fee:** keep the existing Tokyo→Osaka route,
  have them **deliver the two bikes to Tokyo/Yokohama and collect in Osaka**.
- **If one-way is unavailable or too expensive:** **rebase the tour to an Osaka loop** (start & end Osaka).
  The route is already Kansai/Shikoku/Setouchi-heavy, so this is a small restructure of only the first
  ~4 eastern days (Tokyo/Hakone/Izu/Fuji).

**Fallback:** **Rental819 Tokyo** if a Tokyo base is non-negotiable — best branch network, English booking,
both bike classes stocked — but accept it as a **loop returning to Tokyo** (no one-way).

**Correct start/end location:** **Osaka** is the operationally correct base given real availability.
**Loop (or delivery-assisted one-way), not a standard one-way rental.**

## 5. Must-confirm-in-writing before booking

1. **Bike Rental Japan:** Do you offer **one-way / delivery** (deliver to Tokyo, collect in Osaka) for a
   21-day hire of an Africa Twin + CB400X, and **what is the fee**?
2. **Exact 21-day quote** for both bikes (which rate tier applies) + the **deposit/hold** amount.
3. **Child-pillion policy:** does the operator permit a **child passenger on the Africa Twin**, any minimum
   age/height, and can a small child **reach the rear footpegs**? (Operator policy + the footpeg-reach law.)
4. **Rider eligibility:** confirm the rider's licence has been held **≥1 year (ordinary roads) / ≥3 years
   (expressways)** — otherwise the **child pillion is not legal**, especially on expressways.
5. **IDP:** physical **1949 Geneva** permit, **category "A" stamped**, carried with home licence.
6. **Availability of two specific bikes** across the exact 21-day window (book early — popular touring bikes
   sell out in spring/autumn; reservations open ~4 months ahead).
