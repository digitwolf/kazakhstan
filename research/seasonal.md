# Seasonal Notes — Riding Japan in Fall vs. Spring

The two recommended windows for this tour (per `tour/00-overview.md`) are **October–early
November** and **late April–May after Golden Week**. Both are among the best touring months
in Japan — warm-but-not-hot, relatively dry, and good for outdoor sightseeing — but they
behave like mirror images on the two things that actually constrain this route:
**typhoon risk** and **high mountain-pass status**.

- **Fall (Oct–early Nov):** autumn foliage peaks *at altitude* during the trip, but early
  October still carries tail-end typhoon risk on the exposed Shikoku/Kii coasts, and the
  best high passes are *closing* for winter (many in November).
- **Spring (late Apr–May after Golden Week):** essentially **no typhoon risk**, warming and
  drying toward late May, but the high passes are *re-opening* from winter snow — the same
  roads, just on the way up instead of down — and a post–Golden-Week trip largely **misses
  the lowland cherry blossoms** (late March–early April).

Temperature tables below use standard climatological averages (daytime high / overnight
low). Sources at the bottom; **always verify live forecasts and official pass-opening/closure
dates close to travel** — these shift year to year.

---

# FALL WINDOW — October to early November

## Weather overview (fall)
- October is widely rated one of the most pleasant touring months — comfortable daytime temps, clearer skies, less rain than the summer/September peak. Good for outdoor sightseeing and riding.
- Source: https://planjapansmart.com/japan-in-october/ · https://www.agatetravel.com/japan/weather-in-october.html

## Temperatures by region (fall: early-Oct → late-Oct)
Daytime high / overnight low. Lowlands are mild; the headline risk is **cold at elevation**.

| Region (loop area) | Early October | Late October |
|---|---|---|
| Tokyo (bookend) | 22/16 °C · 72/61 °F | 18/12 °C · 64/54 °F |
| Osaka / Kansai | 23/17 °C · 73/63 °F | 18/12 °C · 64/54 °F |
| Kii Peninsula coast (Shirahama) | 24/17 °C · 75/63 °F | 19/13 °C · 66/55 °F |
| Shikoku coast (Kōchi, Matsuyama) | 24/17 °C · 75/63 °F | 19/13 °C · 66/55 °F |
| Setouchi (Okayama, Kurashiki, Kobe) | 23/16 °C · 73/61 °F | 18/11 °C · 64/52 °F |

- **Elevation caveat (fall):** Iya Valley / Ōboke mornings can be **5–10 °C**; high passes are
  **near-freezing (0–10 °C) at dawn**, with **early frost/ice risk in late October**. Dress the
  child for genuine cold at altitude even when the valleys are mild.
- Source: https://www.timeanddate.com/weather/japan/osaka/climate · https://weatherspark.com/y/143399/Average-Weather-in-Osaka-Japan-Year-Round · https://www.japan-guide.com/e/e2273.html

## Typhoon risk (fall — the big fall constraint)
- **Early October is still within the active typhoon window**; risk drops markedly through the month and the season largely ends by **late October into November** (more summer-ish, lower-risk weather).
- Typhoons most affect **southern/Pacific-facing areas**: Okinawa/Kyushu first, then can track up the Pacific coast (Shikoku's south coast, Kii Peninsula, Tōkai). Shikoku and the Kii coast are the more exposed parts of this loop.
- **Planning implications for the tour:**
  - Front-load flexible / coastal-exposed days (Shikoku Pacific capes, Kii coast) toward **mid-late October** if possible.
  - **Book cancellable lodging** and check forecasts daily in the first half of the month.
  - A typhoon doesn't only bring the storm day — heavy rain + wind can close bridges (Shimanami wind limits), trigger mountain landslide closures, and leave wet roads for a day or two after.
- Sources: https://selfguidejapan.com/blog/japan-typhoon-season-2026 · https://www.japanhighlights.com/japan/typhoon-season · https://www.jrpass.com/blog/japan-s-typhoon-season-what-to-expect-and-how-to-prepare

## Autumn foliage (kōyō) timing (fall)
Foliage sweeps from north/high-elevation downward through autumn:
- **Early–mid October:** Hokkaidō and the **high mountains/passes** (Japan Alps, Norikura, Tateyama, high Tōhoku). So the high alpine roads peak *during* this trip's window — but those are also the roads closing for winter soonest.
- **Late October–early November:** mid-elevation Tōhoku, **Nikko / Irohazaka**, higher Chūbu.
- **Mid–late November:** **Tokyo, Osaka, Kyoto lowlands**; Shikoku mountains (Iya Valley) and Kii peninsula generally peak **mid–late November**.
- **Net for an October tour:** expect peak colour mainly at **altitude** (mountain passes, Nikko-area, Alps); the lowland cities and most of Shikoku/Kansai will still be green-to-early-turning. Plan high-mountain rides early in the trip to catch both foliage *and* the passes before they close.
- Sources: https://www.japan-guide.com/e/e2273.html · https://matcha-jp.com/en/1332 · https://en.japantravel.com/guide/autumn-in-japan-your-seasonal-guide/71788

## Mountain-pass closures & cold (fall: roads CLOSING for winter)
Many of Japan's best high roads close for winter — several **in November**, so an October trip is near the *end* of their season. Re-check official dates yearly.
- **Snow-closure (typically mid/late Nov → Apr):** Venus Line, Norikura (also access-restricted), Shiga-Kusatsu Rt 292, UFO Line / Shikoku Karst, Bandai-Azuma Skyline, Zaō Echo Line, Hachimantai Aspite Line, Irohazaka.
- **October realities at altitude:** foggy mornings, wet roads, **early frost/ice** especially late October — dangerous 2-up. Dress the child for genuine cold (alpine roads can be near-freezing at dawn even when valleys are mild).
- **Volcanic-gas/alert closures** (independent of snow): Shiga-Kusatsu near Mt. Shirane, Aso crater roads — can shut at short notice; check active-volcano advisories.
- Sources: https://farwayout.com/top-20-best-motorcycle-roads-in-japan/ · https://www.japan-guide.com/e/e2273.html

## Fall ride-planning checklist
- [ ] Watch the 5–10 day typhoon outlook in **early October**; keep lodging cancellable.
- [ ] Do **high-altitude/foliage roads early** in the trip — they close for snow soonest.
- [ ] Pack genuine cold-weather layers for the child (alpine passes ≈ 0–10 °C at altitude/dawn) plus rain gear.
- [ ] Expect **lowland foliage to still be green** — set expectations; peak colour is at elevation.
- [ ] Re-check each named pass's official closure date and any volcanic-alert status before routing.
- [ ] Buffer/rest days to absorb a rained-out or wind-closed day (Shimanami bridge wind limits, landslides).

# SPRING WINDOW — late April to May (after Golden Week)

## Weather overview (spring)
- Late spring is the other prime touring window: warming, drying, and — critically for this
  exposed coastal loop — **no typhoon season** to plan around. The catch is that high passes
  are still **coming out of winter snow**, and the cherry blossoms have largely finished in
  the lowlands by the time Golden Week ends.
- Source: https://www.japan-guide.com/e/e2273.html · https://www.timeanddate.com/weather/japan/osaka/climate

## Temperatures by region (spring: late-April → May)
Daytime high / overnight low. Late April is still cool, especially mornings; late May is the warm, dry sweet spot.

| Region (loop area) | Late April | May |
|---|---|---|
| Tokyo (bookend) | 19/10 °C · 66/50 °F | 23/15 °C · 73/59 °F |
| Osaka / Kansai | 20/11 °C · 68/52 °F | 25/16 °C · 77/61 °F |
| Kii Peninsula coast | 20/12 °C · 68/54 °F | 24/16 °C · 75/61 °F |
| Shikoku coast (Kōchi, Matsuyama) | 21/12 °C · 70/54 °F | 25/16 °C · 77/61 °F |
| Setouchi (Okayama, Kurashiki, Kobe) | 20/10 °C · 68/50 °F | 24/15 °C · 75/59 °F |

- **Elevation caveat (spring):** Iya Valley / mountain mornings in **late April ≈ 3–8 °C** — still
  cold-layer territory for the child at altitude. Warms quickly through May.
- Source: https://www.timeanddate.com/weather/japan/osaka/climate · https://weatherspark.com/y/143399/Average-Weather-in-Osaka-Japan-Year-Round · https://wanderlog.com/weather/3/5/osaka-weather-in-may

## Typhoon risk (spring — its big advantage)
- **There is effectively no typhoon risk in late April–May.** The Pacific typhoon season ramps
  up later (peaking late summer/early autumn), so the **exposed Shikoku south coast and Kii
  Peninsula coast — the trip's most weather-vulnerable legs in October — are far safer in
  spring.** This is the single strongest argument for the spring window for this particular loop.
- The trade-off is the **June tsuyu (rainy season)**, which begins roughly early-to-mid June in
  western Japan — so the spring window must end *before* tsuyu sets in (i.e. stay in May).
- Sources: https://selfguidejapan.com/blog/japan-typhoon-season-2026 · https://www.japan-guide.com/e/e2273.html

## Mountain-pass status (spring: roads RE-OPENING — the mirror of fall)
The high passes are the same constraint as fall, **in reverse**: instead of closing for winter,
they are **re-opening from snow, typically late April to mid-May**. A late-April trip may find
some still shut; by mid-to-late May most are open.
- **Typical re-opening (late Apr → mid-May, varies yearly with snowpack):** Venus Line, Shiga-Kusatsu Rt 292, UFO Line / Shikoku Karst, Bandai-Azuma Skyline, Zaō Echo Line, Hachimantai Aspite Line, Norikura (also access-restricted). Some Alpine roads open even later.
- **Action:** **re-check each named pass's official OPENING date** before routing a spring trip —
  do not assume a road is open just because the lowlands are warm. A later trip (mid-late May)
  greatly reduces the chance of a snow-closed pass.
- **Volcanic-gas/alert closures** (Shiga-Kusatsu near Mt. Shirane, Aso) apply year-round — check advisories regardless of season.
- Sources: https://farwayout.com/top-20-best-motorcycle-roads-in-japan/ · https://www.japan-guide.com/e/e2273.html

## Cherry blossoms & Golden Week (spring-specific)
- **Lowland sakura peak late March–early April** in Tokyo/Osaka/Kansai, so a **post–Golden-Week
  trip largely MISSES the city cherry blossoms** — expect fresh greenery instead, with perhaps
  only **late high-elevation/late-variety blossoms** lingering in the mountains. If city sakura
  is a priority, that's an early-April trip, which carries its own (cooler, wetter, passes-shut) trade-offs.
- **Golden Week (≈ Apr 29 – May 5)** is one of Japan's busiest travel periods: **crowds, traffic,
  and scarce/expensive lodging.** This is exactly why the recommendation is "**after** Golden Week."
- **Sweet spot: mid-to-late May** — warm, dry, passes open, before the June tsuyu rainy season,
  and clear of Golden Week congestion.
- Sources: https://www.japan-guide.com/e/e2011.html · https://www.japan-guide.com/e/e2273.html

## Spring ride-planning checklist
- [ ] Travel **after Golden Week** (after ~May 5) to avoid peak crowds and lodging scarcity.
- [ ] Aim **mid-to-late May** for the best combo of warmth, dry weather and open passes.
- [ ] **Re-check each named pass's official OPENING date** — late April can still be snow-closed at altitude.
- [ ] Pack cold morning layers for the child at elevation (Iya mornings ≈ 3–8 °C in late April).
- [ ] Set expectations: **city cherry blossoms are mostly over** post–Golden-Week; greenery (+ maybe late high-elevation blossoms) instead.
- [ ] Finish the trip **before June tsuyu** (rainy season) sets in.

# Spring vs. Fall — which to choose?

| | **Fall (Oct–early Nov)** | **Spring (late May, post-GW)** |
|---|---|---|
| Foliage / flowers | **Autumn kōyō at altitude** (peak on high passes) | Greenery; lowland sakura already over |
| Typhoon risk | **Yes, early October** (exposed Shikoku/Kii coast) | **None** — strongest reason to pick spring for this coastal loop |
| High passes | **Closing** for winter (many in Nov) — ride them early | **Re-opening** from snow (late Apr–mid May) — go mid/late May to be safe |
| Temperatures | Mild, cooling through the month | Warmest/driest by late May |
| Crowds | Generally fine (avoid foliage hotspots on weekends) | Avoid Golden Week; otherwise fine |

**Bottom line:**
- Choose **fall** if **autumn foliage at altitude** is the goal — accept early-October typhoon
  risk on the exposed coasts (front-load coastal days late in the month, keep lodging
  cancellable) and ride the high passes early before they snow-close.
- Choose **late-May spring** for the **warmest, driest, typhoon-free** riding with passes open —
  accepting that you'll **miss the city cherry blossoms** and should confirm each pass's
  opening date and finish before June's rainy season.

For *this* loop (a newer rider + child pillion on the typhoon-exposed Shikoku/Kii coasts),
**late-May spring is the lower-risk weather choice**; **fall wins only if autumn colour is the priority.**
