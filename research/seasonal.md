# Seasonal Notes — the PNW in early July (Jul 1–7)

Early July is close to the ideal week for this loop: long daylight, dry mountain passes, and the
coast at its liveliest for the Fourth. The two things to actually *plan* around are **coastal fog
and wind** out west and **alpine pass opening + crowds** in the Cascades. Verify the live forecast
and the WSDOT pass report before departure — mountain weather and snowpack vary year to year.

---

## The good news (why early July works)

- **Long daylight.** Around the solstice the PNW gets **~15.5–16 hours of daylight** (sunrise ~5:20, sunset ~9:00). Plenty of margin to ride the day caps without ever pushing into the dark — which the plan forbids anyway.
- **Dry, settled inland weather.** The Willamette Valley and Cascades are usually warm and dry by early July; the rainy season is over. Good for the Day-5 transfer and the Rainier/Chinook finale.
- **Passes are open.** Chinook Pass (SR-410) normally clears of snow and reopens **by late May–early July**; Paradise is plowed and open. Wildflowers are coming on at Paradise. *(Always confirm — see below.)*
- **Coast at its best for the Fourth.** Dry-ish, festive, and Yachats does its **La De Da Parade + bay fireworks** on Jul 4 — the rest day is built around it.

## The two things to plan around

### 1. Coast fog, wind & cool temps (Days 1–4)
- The Oregon/Washington coast runs **cool and often foggy/overcast even in July** — think **mid-50s to mid-60s °F**, frequent morning fog, and a persistent afternoon onshore wind. It can be sunny inland and gray on the beach the same hour.
- **Wind** matters on the **Astoria–Megler Bridge** (Day 2) and exposed headlands — ride a calm, central line.
- **Implication:** dress for **cold and damp on the coast** even in summer (layers + rain shell, heated grips welcome). Don't expect "summer" beach weather; expect Pacific-cool.

### 2. Alpine passes & crowds (Days 6–7)
- **Chinook Pass (SR-410)** is a **seasonal pass closed all winter.** It typically reopens late May–early July depending on snowpack, but in a heavy year it can open late. **Confirm the WSDOT pass report before Day 7;** the SR-7/Enumclaw lowland route is the fallback.
- **Altitude is cold.** Paradise (~5,400 ft) and Chinook Pass (5,430 ft) can be **30+ °F colder** than the coast, with snowbanks lingering into July and chilly, changeable mornings. Pack a real warm/wet layer for the kid for the Day-7 climb.
- **Crowds.** Early July + the holiday week = **busy** Mount Rainier and a holiday-traffic coast. Mount Rainier runs **summer timed-entry reservations** for the Paradise corridor — book ahead (see touring-practical.md). Start early to beat both traffic and afternoon clouds on the mountain.

### Fourth-of-July crowds (Days 3–4)
- The central Oregon coast — **Yachats especially** — sells out hard for the Fourth. **Book the rest-base nights far ahead.** Expect parade-day road closures and crowds in the village; the bikes are parked that day by design.

## Temperature & conditions cheat-sheet (typical early July)

| Zone | Days | Daytime high | Notable | Gear emphasis |
|------|------|--------------|---------|----------------|
| Puget Sound / Hood Canal | 1 | ~70–75 °F | Mild, can be sunny | Standard layers |
| WA/OR coast | 1–4 | ~58–66 °F | Fog, onshore wind, cool | **Warm + waterproof layers** |
| Willamette Valley | 5 | ~80–88 °F | Warm, dry, inland heat | Vented gear, hydrate |
| St. Helens foothills | 5–6 | ~75–82 °F | Warm, dry | Vented |
| Mount Rainier / Chinook Pass | 7 | ~45–60 °F at altitude | Cold mornings, snowbanks, changeable | **Warm/wet layer for everyone, esp. Aslan** |

*The single hardest packing problem of this trip is the swing from a cold, foggy coast to hot
valley to cold alpine pass — all in one week. Plan layers you can add/shed at every stop.*
